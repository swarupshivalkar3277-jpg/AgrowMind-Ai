# Drought Management in Agriculture

# Overview

Drought is a prolonged period of insufficient rainfall resulting in soil moisture deficit, reduced crop growth, lower yields, and economic losses. Drought is one of the most serious climate-related challenges affecting agriculture worldwide.

Effective drought management involves:

* Water conservation
* Soil moisture preservation
* Drought-resistant crop selection
* Efficient irrigation practices
* Crop diversification
* Risk management strategies

Proper drought preparedness can significantly reduce crop losses and improve farm resilience.

---

# What is Drought?

Drought occurs when water availability becomes insufficient to meet crop requirements for normal growth and development.

Drought may result from:

* Low rainfall
* Delayed monsoon
* Early withdrawal of monsoon
* High temperatures
* Excessive evaporation
* Poor water management

---

# Types of Drought

## Meteorological Drought

Occurs when rainfall is significantly below normal levels over an extended period.

### Indicators

* Below-average rainfall
* Delayed monsoon
* Long dry spells

---

## Agricultural Drought

Occurs when soil moisture becomes insufficient for crop growth.

### Indicators

* Dry soil
* Wilting crops
* Reduced growth
* Poor germination

---

## Hydrological Drought

Occurs when water bodies experience reduced water availability.

### Indicators

* Low reservoir levels
* Reduced river flow
* Falling groundwater levels

---

## Socioeconomic Drought

Occurs when water shortages begin affecting economic and social activities.

### Effects

* Reduced farm income
* Food shortages
* Increased production costs

---

## Natural Causes

* Delayed monsoon

---

## Human-Induced Causes

* Excess groundwater extraction

# Impact of Drought on Crops

## Germination Stage

Water deficiency may cause:

* Poor seed germination
* Uneven emergence
* Weak seedlings

---

## Vegetative Stage

Effects include:

* Reduced leaf development
* Stunted growth
* Reduced biomass production

---

## Flowering Stage

Drought during flowering can cause:

* Flower drop
* Poor pollination
* Reduced fruit set

This is often the most sensitive stage.

---

## Fruiting and Grain Filling Stage

Effects include:

* Small fruits
* Poor grain filling
* Reduced yield
* Lower quality produce

---

# Physiological Effects on Plants

Drought stress causes:

* Reduced photosynthesis
* Stomatal closure
* Nutrient uptake reduction
* Reduced root activity
* Increased plant temperature

---

# Symptoms of Drought Stress

## Early Symptoms

* Mild leaf rolling
* Slight wilting during daytime
* Slow growth
* Reduced leaf expansion

---

## Moderate Symptoms

* Persistent wilting
* Yellowing of leaves
* Premature leaf shedding
* Flower abortion

---

## Severe Symptoms

* Dry leaves
* Stem drying
* Fruit drop
* Plant death

---

# Farmer Advisory Guidelines

### If rainfall deficit is less than 20%

* Continue normal cultivation
* Monitor soil moisture

### If rainfall deficit is 20–40%

* Reduce irrigation intervals
* Apply mulch
* Control weeds aggressively

### If rainfall deficit exceeds 40%

* Prioritize critical irrigation
* Shift to drought-tolerant crops
* Reduce plant population if necessary

### During severe drought

* Focus on crop survival
* Harvest salvageable produce
* Protect livestock resources
