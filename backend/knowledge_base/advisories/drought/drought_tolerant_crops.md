# Drought-Tolerant Crops

# Overview

Drought-tolerant crops are crops that can survive and produce reasonable yields under conditions of limited water availability. These crops possess special characteristics such as deep root systems, efficient water utilization, reduced transpiration, and the ability to withstand moisture stress.

Growing drought-tolerant crops is one of the most effective strategies for reducing agricultural risks in drought-prone and rainfed regions.

Benefits include:

* Lower water requirements
* Better survival during dry spells
* Stable yields under moisture stress
* Reduced irrigation costs
* Improved farm resilience

These crops are particularly important in areas with uncertain rainfall and climate variability.

---

# What Makes a Crop Drought-Tolerant?

Drought-tolerant crops possess one or more of the following characteristics:

## Deep Root System

Deep roots allow plants to access water stored in deeper soil layers.

Examples:

* Pigeon pea
* Sorghum
* Cotton

---

## Efficient Water Use

Some crops produce more biomass or grain per unit of water consumed.

Examples:

* Pearl millet
* Finger millet

---

## Reduced Water Loss

Plants may reduce water loss through:

* Smaller leaves
* Leaf rolling
* Waxy leaf coatings
* Reduced stomatal opening

---

## Early Maturity

Short-duration crops complete their life cycle before severe drought develops.

Examples:

* Green gram
* Black gram

---

# Importance of Drought-Tolerant Crops

These crops help farmers:

* Reduce crop failure risk
* Maintain income during dry years
* Improve food security
* Reduce irrigation dependency
* Adapt to climate change

---

# Classification of Drought-Tolerant Crops

## Highly Drought-Tolerant Crops

Can survive prolonged dry periods.

Examples:

* Pearl millet (Bajra)
* Sorghum (Jowar)
* Finger millet (Ragi)
* Pigeon pea (Tur)
* Cowpea

---

## Moderately Drought-Tolerant Crops

Require some moisture but tolerate short drought periods.

Examples:

* Groundnut
* Cotton
* Chickpea
* Sesame

---

## Less Drought-Tolerant Crops

Can tolerate only mild moisture stress.

Examples:

* Maize
* Soybean
* Sunflower

# Contingency Crop Planning

When monsoon is delayed:

### Delay up to 2 weeks

Continue with normal crop selection.

### Delay 2–4 weeks

Shift to short-duration varieties.

### Delay beyond 4 weeks

Choose:

* Green gram
* Cowpea
* Sesame
* Pearl millet

---

# Climate Change Adaptation

Drought-tolerant crops help farmers adapt to:

* Rising temperatures
* Erratic rainfall
* Frequent droughts
* Water scarcity

These crops are important components of climate-smart agriculture.

---

# Best Agronomic Practices

To maximize drought tolerance:

* Use drought-resistant varieties.
* Apply organic matter.
* Use mulching.
* Practice rainwater harvesting.
* Follow conservation tillage.
* Control weeds promptly.

---

# Common Mistakes to Avoid

Avoid:

* Growing high-water-demand crops in dry areas.
* Late sowing during uncertain rainfall.
* Excess nitrogen application.
* Poor moisture conservation practices.

---

# Farmer Advisory Guidelines

### During Normal Rainfall

* Follow recommended crop plans.

### During Rainfall Deficit

* Prefer drought-tolerant crops.
* Reduce plant population if necessary.
* Use moisture conservation techniques.

### During Severe Drought

* Prioritize short-duration crops.
* Focus on fodder security.
* Avoid high-risk crops.
