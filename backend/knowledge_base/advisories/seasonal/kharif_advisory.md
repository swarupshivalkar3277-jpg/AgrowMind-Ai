# Kharif Season Advisory

# Overview

The Kharif season is the primary rainy-season cropping period in many agricultural regions. Crops are generally sown with the onset of monsoon rains and harvested during autumn.

The Kharif season typically extends from:

* June to October (varies by region)

Major Kharif crops include:

* Rice
* Maize
* Sorghum
* Pearl millet
* Soybean
* Groundnut
* Cotton
* Pigeon pea
* Green gram
* Black gram

Proper Kharif management is essential for achieving high productivity and minimizing losses due to monsoon variability.

---

# Importance of Kharif Farming

Kharif agriculture contributes significantly to:

* Food production
* Oilseed production
* Pulse production
* Fodder availability
* Farm income generation

The season depends heavily on rainfall distribution and monsoon performance.
