# Cyclone Alert and Agricultural Management

# Overview

Cyclones are severe weather systems characterized by strong winds, heavy rainfall, storm surges, and flooding. Cyclones can cause extensive damage to crops, livestock, farm infrastructure, irrigation systems, and stored agricultural produce.

Agricultural cyclone preparedness focuses on:

* Early warning response
* Crop protection
* Livestock safety
* Farm infrastructure protection
* Flood and drainage management
* Post-cyclone recovery

Timely action before, during, and after a cyclone can significantly reduce agricultural losses.

---

# What is a Cyclone?

A cyclone is a large-scale atmospheric system with low pressure at its center and strong rotating winds.

Cyclones may bring:

* Heavy rainfall
* Strong winds
* Flooding
* Storm surges
* Coastal inundation
* Landslides in hilly regions

---

# Cyclone Categories and Risk Levels

## Cyclone Watch

Issued when cyclone formation is possible.

Farmers should begin preparations.

---

## Cyclone Alert

Issued when a cyclone is likely to affect the area.

Protective measures should be initiated immediately.

---

## Cyclone Warning

Issued when cyclone impact is imminent.

Emergency actions should be completed.

---

## Post-Cyclone Advisory

Issued after cyclone passage.

Recovery and damage assessment activities begin.

---

# Impact of Cyclones on Agriculture

## Crop Damage

Cyclones may cause:

* Lodging of crops
* Defoliation
* Stem breakage
* Flower drop
* Fruit drop
* Crop submergence

---

## Soil Damage

Possible effects include:

* Soil erosion
* Nutrient loss
* Salinity intrusion in coastal areas
* Structural degradation

---

## Livestock Losses

Risks include:

* Injury
* Drowning
* Shelter damage
* Feed shortages

---

## Infrastructure Damage

Cyclones may damage:

* Farm buildings
* Irrigation systems
* Greenhouses
* Storage facilities
* Power supply systems

# Crop Protection Before Cyclone

## Early Harvesting

Harvest mature crops whenever possible.

Suitable crops include:

* Vegetables
* Fruits
* Mature grain crops

This reduces potential losses.

---

## Stake and Support Crops

Provide support for:

* Banana
* Tomato
* Chilli
* Papaya
* Young fruit trees

Methods include:

* Bamboo staking
* Rope support systems

---

## Reduce Plant Canopy

Light pruning may reduce wind resistance in orchards.

Suitable crops include:

* Mango
* Guava
* Citrus

---

## Harvest Fruits

Harvest market-ready fruits before cyclone landfall.

## Human Safety

Human life is the highest priority.

* Field operations
* Outdoor activities
* Flooded areas

---

## Livestock Safety

Keep animals in secure shelters.

Monitor:

* Water availability
* Shelter integrity

---

## Electrical Safety

Disconnect vulnerable electrical systems when necessary.

# Immediate Post-Cyclone Actions

## Safety Assessment

Ensure:

* Personal safety
* Livestock safety
* Structural stability

Before entering damaged areas.

---

## Damage Assessment

* Crop fields
* Irrigation systems
* Buildings
* Equipment

## Remove Standing Water

Drain excess water immediately.

* Oxygen deficiency
