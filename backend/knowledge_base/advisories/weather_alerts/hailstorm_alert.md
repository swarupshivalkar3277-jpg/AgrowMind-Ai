# Hailstorm Alert and Agricultural Management

# Overview

A hailstorm is a severe weather event in which balls or lumps of ice (hailstones) fall from thunderstorms. Hailstorms can cause sudden and extensive damage to crops, fruit orchards, vegetables, livestock, farm structures, and agricultural equipment.

Agricultural hailstorm management focuses on:

* Early warning preparedness
* Crop protection
* Orchard management
* Livestock safety
* Infrastructure protection
* Post-hailstorm recovery

Timely action before and after a hailstorm can significantly reduce agricultural losses.

---

# What is a Hailstorm?

A hailstorm occurs when strong thunderstorm clouds produce ice particles that grow into hailstones before falling to the ground.

Hailstones may vary in size from:

* Pea-sized hail
* Marble-sized hail
* Golf-ball-sized hail
* Larger destructive hailstones

The severity of crop damage generally increases with hailstone size and wind speed.

## Sudden Occurrence

Hailstorms often develop rapidly with little warning.

---

## Strong Winds

Accompanied by:

* Gusty winds

* Heavy rainfall

---

## Localized Damage

Damage is often concentrated within specific areas.

Nearby farms may experience different levels of impact.

---

# Agricultural Impact of Hailstorms

## Physical Crop Damage

Hailstones may cause:

* Leaf shredding
* Stem breakage
* Fruit bruising
* Flower loss
* Pod damage

---

## Defoliation

Severe hailstorms can remove a large portion of plant foliage.

Consequences include:

* Reduced photosynthesis
* Slower recovery
* Yield reduction

---

## Fruit Damage

Fruit crops may suffer:

* Surface scars
* Cracks
* Bruising
* Premature fruit drop

Market value decreases significantly.

---

## Crop Lodging

Tall crops may be flattened by:

* Wind
* Rain
* Hail impact

Examples:

* Maize
* Sorghum
* Wheat

* Fruits

* Structural supports

## Tree Support Systems

Support young trees with:

* Anchoring systems

## Pruning Management

## Secure Feed Stocks

# Actions During Hailstorm

## Human Safety

Avoid:

* Field operations
* Open areas
* Trees
* Metal structures during lightning activity

Safety should always be the highest priority.

---

## Livestock Safety

Keep animals indoors until the storm passes.

---

## Electrical Safety

Avoid handling electrical equipment during thunderstorms.

---

# Immediate Post-Hailstorm Actions

## Damage Assessment

Inspect:

* Crops
* Orchards
* Livestock
* Buildings
* Irrigation systems

Document losses carefully.

---

## Remove Broken Plant Parts

Remove:

* Severely damaged branches
* Broken stems
* Destroyed fruits

This reduces disease risk.

---

## Improve Drainage

Remove standing water if heavy rainfall occurred.

---

# Crop Recovery Management

## Assess Crop Survival

Evaluate:

* Plant stand
* Leaf area remaining
* Growth stage

Recovery potential depends on crop stage and severity of damage.

---

## Replanting Decisions

Consider replanting when:

* Plant population falls below economic thresholds
* Damage occurs early in the season

---

## Nutrient Support

Apply balanced nutrition to encourage recovery.

Important nutrients:

* Nitrogen
* Potassium
* Micronutrients

---

## Foliar Nutrition

Foliar sprays may help stimulate regrowth.

Common nutrients:

* Potassium
* Zinc
* Micronutrient mixtures

---

## Common Diseases

* Bacterial infections
* Fungal infections
* Fruit rots
* Stem cankers

---

## Preventive Measures

* Remove damaged tissues
* Maintain sanitation
* Improve airflow
