# Heatwave Alert and Agricultural Management

# Overview

A heatwave is a prolonged period of abnormally high temperatures that can severely affect agriculture. Heatwaves increase crop water demand, reduce pollination, cause flower and fruit drop, decrease livestock productivity, and may lead to significant economic losses.

Heatwave management focuses on:

* Crop protection
* Water conservation
* Irrigation management
* Heat stress reduction
* Livestock safety
* Emergency preparedness

Early action can significantly reduce heat-related agricultural losses.

---

# What is a Heatwave?

A heatwave occurs when temperatures remain significantly above normal for several consecutive days.

Characteristics include:

* Extremely high daytime temperatures
* Hot nights
* Low soil moisture
* Increased evaporation
* Increased crop water stress

Heatwaves often occur during summer and pre-monsoon periods.

---

# Causes of Heatwaves

## Climate Change

Rising global temperatures have increased:

* Heatwave frequency
* Heatwave intensity
* Duration of extreme heat events

---

## High Pressure Systems

Persistent high-pressure systems trap hot air and suppress rainfall.

---

## Low Soil Moisture

Dry soils contribute to higher land temperatures.

---

## Urban Heat Effects

In peri-urban farming areas, heat may be intensified by nearby urban development.

# Agricultural Impact of Heatwaves

## Crop Stress

High temperatures may cause:

* Wilting
* Leaf scorching
* Reduced photosynthesis
* Poor growth

---

## Flower Drop

Heat stress can result in:

* Flower abortion
* Reduced pollination
* Lower fruit set

---

## Fruit Damage

Common symptoms include:

* Sunscald
* Fruit cracking
* Reduced fruit size
* Poor quality

---

## Yield Reduction

Severe heat stress may reduce:

* Grain yield
* Fruit yield
* Vegetable production

Ensure:

Use:

## Irrigation Timing

Best irrigation periods:

* Early morning
* Late evening

Avoid midday irrigation.

---

* Efficient water use
* Reduced evaporation

## Life-Saving Irrigation

* Prioritize flowering crops

# Heatwave Emergency Actions

## For Crops

* Increase irrigation frequency if water is available.
* Apply mulches immediately.
* Use temporary shading for nurseries.

---

## For Livestock

* Move animals to shaded areas.
* Increase water availability.
* Reduce handling during peak heat.

---

## For Farm Workers

* Avoid field work during extreme heat.
* Work during cooler hours.
* Maintain hydration.
