# Stem Bleeding Disease in Coconut

## Overview

Stem bleeding is a serious fungal disease affecting coconut palms. It is characterized by the exudation of reddish-brown liquid from cracks in the stem. If left untreated, the disease weakens the palm, reduces nut yield, and may eventually kill the tree.

The disease is commonly found in older coconut palms, especially under poor drainage and unfavorable soil conditions.

---

# Causal Organism

* **Pathogen:** *Thielaviopsis paradoxa*
* Other associated fungi may also contribute under severe conditions.

---

# Affected Crops

* Coconut (*Cocos nucifera*)

---

# Symptoms

## Early Symptoms

* Small cracks appear on the trunk.
* Dark reddish-brown liquid oozes from the cracks.
* Bleeding patches become visible on the stem.

## Advanced Symptoms

* Extensive stem rotting.
* Yellowing and drying of leaves.
* Reduced nut production.
* Wilting of fronds.
* Decay spreads upward and downward along the trunk.
* Internal tissues become soft and rotten.

## Severe Infection

* Palm becomes structurally weak.
* Crown size reduces significantly.
* Tree may die if untreated.

---

# Disease Cycle

The fungus survives in:

* Infected plant debris
* Soil
* Dead organic matter

The pathogen enters the palm through:

* Wounds on the stem
* Mechanical injuries
* Insect damage
* Cracks caused by environmental stress

Moist conditions favor fungal growth and disease spread.

---

# Favorable Conditions

The disease develops rapidly under:

* Poor soil drainage
* Waterlogging
* High humidity
* Excessive moisture
* Nutrient deficiency
* Stem injuries
* Neglected plantation management

---

# Diagnosis

## Visual Identification

Key identifying feature:

* Reddish-brown bleeding patches on the trunk

## Internal Symptoms

When bark is removed:

* Rotten brown tissues
* Foul smell
* Decayed vascular tissues

---

# Prevention

## Cultural Practices

* Maintain proper drainage.
* Avoid water stagnation.
* Apply balanced fertilizers.
* Prevent stem injuries.
* Remove severely infected tissues.
* Maintain field sanitation.

## Plantation Management

* Regular inspection of palms
* Proper spacing between trees
* Timely irrigation management

---

# Treatment and Management

## Mechanical Control

1. Scrape affected bark and diseased tissues.
2. Remove all rotten portions completely.
3. Allow the area to dry.

## Chemical Control

Apply fungicidal paste after scraping:

### Recommended Chemicals

* Bordeaux paste
* Copper oxychloride
* Carbendazim paste

### Application Method

* Apply paste over cleaned infected area.
* Cover with coal tar if required.
* Repeat treatment every 3–4 months if symptoms persist.

---

# Organic Management

* Neem cake application
* Trichoderma-enriched compost
* Organic soil amendments for improving soil health

---

# Integrated Disease Management (IDM)

Best control is achieved by combining:

* Proper drainage
* Nutrient management
* Fungicide application
* Sanitation practices
* Regular monitoring

---

# Economic Importance

Stem bleeding causes:

* Significant yield reduction
* Weakening of coconut palms
* Increased management cost
* Economic loss to farmers

Severe infection may lead to complete loss of productive trees.

---

# Monitoring Tips for Farmers

Farmers should regularly check for:

* Bleeding patches on trunk
* Yellowing leaves
* Reduced nut set
* Cracks in stem
* Soft or rotten bark

Early detection greatly improves treatment success.

---

# Recommended Farmer Action

If symptoms are observed:

1. Isolate severely affected palms.
2. Scrape infected area immediately.
3. Apply recommended fungicide.
4. Improve drainage conditions.
5. Consult agricultural experts if disease spreads rapidly.
