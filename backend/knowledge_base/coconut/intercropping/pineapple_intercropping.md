# Pineapple Intercropping in Coconut Cultivation

## Overview

Pineapple intercropping is a profitable and efficient farming practice where pineapple plants are grown between coconut palms. Pineapple is considered an ideal intercrop because it has a shallow root system, requires moderate shade, and utilizes the vacant space effectively in coconut plantations.

This intercropping system helps improve farm income, conserve soil moisture, suppress weeds, and increase overall land productivity.

Pineapple cultivation is especially suitable in young and moderately spaced coconut plantations where sufficient sunlight reaches the ground.

---

# Importance of Pineapple Intercropping

The system helps farmers by:

* Generating additional income

* Conserving soil moisture
* Reducing weed growth
* Enhancing farm sustainability
* Diversifying crop production

Pineapple provides faster returns compared to coconut, helping farmers earn regular income.

Dense pineapple growth helps reduce weed population by covering the soil surface.

# Spacing and Planting Layout

## Coconut Spacing

Typical coconut spacing:

* 7.5 m × 7.5 m
* 8 m × 8 m

## Pineapple Arrangement

Pineapple is planted in rows between coconut palms while maintaining:

* Proper sunlight exposure
* Adequate air circulation
* Easy field operations

Both crops require balanced nutrient supply.

# Irrigation Management

Pineapple requires moderate but regular moisture.

## Important Practices

* Avoid waterlogging
* Irrigate during dry periods
* Use drip irrigation if possible
* Maintain proper drainage
