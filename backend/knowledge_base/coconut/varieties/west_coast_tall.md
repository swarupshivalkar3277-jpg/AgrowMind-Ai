# West Coast Tall Coconut Variety

## Overview

West Coast Tall (WCT) is one of the most important traditional tall coconut varieties cultivated in India. It is widely grown along the western coastal regions and is known for its robust growth, adaptability, long productive lifespan, and high copra yield.

This variety is highly valued for:

* Copra production
* Coconut oil extraction
* Stable yield performance
* Strong adaptability to coastal climates

West Coast Tall is commonly used in commercial coconut plantations and hybrid breeding programs.

---

# Origin and Distribution

## Origin

West Coast Tall originated along the western coastal belt of India.

---

## Major Growing Areas

The variety is extensively cultivated in:

* Kerala
* Karnataka
* Goa
* Maharashtra coastal regions
* Parts of Tamil Nadu

It performs especially well in humid coastal climates.

# Climatic Requirements

West Coast Tall grows best under:

* Warm humid tropical climate
* Coastal environmental conditions
* Adequate rainfall
* Good sunlight exposure

The variety performs especially well in regions with high humidity.

---

# Soil Requirements

Suitable soils include:

* Sandy loam
* Coastal alluvial soil
* Lateritic soil
* Well-drained loamy soil

Proper drainage is important to prevent root damage.

---

# Irrigation Requirements

Regular irrigation improves:

* Flowering
* Nut development
* Yield stability
* Palm vigor

Irrigation is especially beneficial during:

* Summer months
* Drought conditions

Drip irrigation helps improve water-use efficiency.

# Nutrient Management

Balanced nutrient supply is essential for healthy growth and productivity.

## Recommended Practices

* Apply organic manure regularly
* Use balanced fertilizers
* Provide micronutrients when necessary
* Conduct periodic soil testing

Integrated nutrient management improves long-term productivity.

# Pest and Disease Susceptibility

West Coast Tall may be affected by:

## Common Pests

* Rhinoceros beetle
* Red palm weevil
* Eriophyid mite

## Common Diseases

* Bud rot
* Stem bleeding
* Root wilt disease

Integrated pest and disease management is recommended.
