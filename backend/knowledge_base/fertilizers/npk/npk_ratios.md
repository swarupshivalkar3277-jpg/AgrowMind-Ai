# NPK Ratios

# Overview

NPK ratio means the proportion of three primary plant nutrients present in a fertilizer:

* N = Nitrogen
* P = Phosphorus
* K = Potassium

NPK fertilizers are widely used in agriculture because nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium are required in large quantities for crop growth, root development, flowering, fruiting, grain filling, and overall yield.

Understanding NPK ratios helps farmers choose the right fertilizer for the right crop stage. Wrong NPK selection can cause nutrient imbalance, poor crop growth, low yield, poor fruit quality, higher pest attack, fertilizer wastage, and unnecessary cost.

NPK ratio knowledge helps farmers:

* Select suitable fertilizer grade
* Match fertilizer with crop stage
* Avoid excess nitrogen
* Improve root growth
* Improve flowering and fruit setting
* Improve fruit size and quality
* Improve grain filling
* Reduce fertilizer waste
* Follow soil-test-based nutrient management
* Improve fertilizer-use efficiency

NPK fertilizers should be selected based on soil test, crop requirement, crop stage, irrigation method, previous crop, organic manure use, and local agricultural recommendations.

---

# What is NPK?

NPK represents the three major nutrients required by crops.

## Nitrogen

Nitrogen supports vegetative growth.

It helps in:

* Leaf growth
* Green color
* Photosynthesis
* Protein formation
* Plant vigor
* Tillering and branching

## Phosphorus

Phosphorus supports root and reproductive development.

It helps in:

* Root growth
* Early crop establishment
* Energy transfer
* Flowering
* Seed formation
* Crop maturity

## Potassium

Potassium supports quality, stress tolerance, and crop strength.

It helps in:

* Water regulation
* Disease tolerance
* Drought tolerance
* Fruit quality
* Grain filling
* Stem strength
* Sugar movement
* Shelf life

---

# Meaning of NPK Ratio

An NPK ratio shows the relative proportion of nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium in a fertilizer.

For example:

* 10:10:10 means equal proportion of N, P, and K.
* 19:19:19 means equal N, P, and K at higher concentration.
* 12:32:16 means higher phosphorus than nitrogen and potassium.
* 13:00:45 means nitrogen and high potassium, with no phosphorus.
* 00:52:34 means phosphorus and potassium, with no nitrogen.

NPK numbers on fertilizer bags usually represent nutrient percentage by weight.

---

# Understanding Fertilizer Grade

A fertilizer grade tells the percentage of nutrients present in the fertilizer.

Example:

## 19:19:19

This means 100 kg of fertilizer contains approximately:

* 19 kg nitrogen
* 19 kg phosphorus as P2O5
* 19 kg potassium as K2O

Remaining weight includes filler, salts, moisture, and other components.

## 12:32:16

This means 100 kg of fertilizer contains approximately:

* 12 kg nitrogen
* 32 kg phosphorus as P2O5
* 16 kg potassium as K2O

---

# Important Note About Phosphorus and Potassium Units

Fertilizer labels commonly express phosphorus and potassium as oxide forms:

* Phosphorus is written as P2O5
* Potassium is written as K2O

Farmers usually follow fertilizer recommendations using these same units.

Do not confuse:

* P with P2O5
* K with K2O

Use local fertilizer recommendation tables or expert guidance when converting nutrient requirements.

---

# Difference Between Ratio and Grade

NPK grade and NPK ratio are related but not exactly the same.

## Grade

Grade shows actual nutrient percentage.

Example:

* 20:20:00 contains 20% N, 20% P2O5, and 0% K2O.

## Ratio

Ratio shows relative nutrient balance.

Example:

* 20:20:00 has a ratio of 1:1:0.
* 10:10:00 also has a ratio of 1:1:0, but lower nutrient concentration.

So, two fertilizers can have the same ratio but different nutrient strength.

---

# Why NPK Ratio Matters

Different crop stages need different nutrient balance.

## Early Stage

Plants need phosphorus for roots and nitrogen for initial growth.

## Vegetative Stage

Plants need more nitrogen for leaves and stems.

## Flowering Stage

Plants need balanced nutrition, with phosphorus and potassium important.

## Fruiting or Grain Filling Stage

Plants need more potassium for quality, size, filling, sweetness, and stress tolerance.

Using the same fertilizer ratio throughout the crop may not be ideal.

---

# Role of Nitrogen in NPK Fertilizers

Nitrogen is essential for vegetative growth.

## Benefits

* Promotes leaf growth
* Improves crop color
* Supports photosynthesis
* Increases biomass
* Supports tillering in cereals
* Helps early crop vigor

## Nitrogen Deficiency Symptoms

* Yellowing of older leaves
* Poor growth
* Small leaves
* Weak plants
* Low tillering
* Reduced yield

## Excess Nitrogen Problems

* Excessive leafy growth
* Weak stems
* Lodging in cereals
* Delayed flowering
* Poor fruiting
* More pest attack
* More disease susceptibility
* Poor fruit quality
* Lower shelf life
* Higher production cost

Nitrogen should be applied in split doses where recommended.

---

# Role of Phosphorus in NPK Fertilizers

Phosphorus is important for roots and reproduction.

## Benefits

* Strong root system
* Better seedling establishment
* Early crop growth
* Better flowering
* Better seed formation
* Better energy transfer
* Improved maturity

## Phosphorus Deficiency Symptoms

* Stunted growth
* Poor root development
* Purple discoloration in some crops
* Delayed maturity
* Poor flowering
* Poor seed formation

## Excess Phosphorus Problems

* Zinc deficiency risk
* Iron deficiency risk
* Nutrient imbalance
* Fertilizer waste
* Environmental pollution risk

Phosphorus is usually applied as basal fertilizer because it moves slowly in soil.

---

# Role of Potassium in NPK Fertilizers

Potassium is important for crop strength and quality.

## Benefits

* Improves water regulation
* Improves drought tolerance
* Improves disease tolerance
* Improves fruit size
* Improves fruit color
* Improves sweetness in some fruits
* Improves grain filling
* Improves stem strength
* Improves shelf life
* Improves stress tolerance

## Potassium Deficiency Symptoms

* Yellowing or scorching of older leaf edges
* Weak stems
* Poor fruit quality
* Small fruits
* Poor grain filling
* Low stress tolerance
* Increased disease sensitivity

## Excess Potassium Problems

* Calcium deficiency risk
* Magnesium deficiency risk
* Blossom-end rot risk in tomato if calcium uptake is reduced
* Nutrient imbalance
* Higher fertilizer cost

Potassium is especially important during flowering, fruiting, and grain filling.

# Balanced NPK Ratios

Balanced NPK fertilizers contain similar amounts of N, P, and K.

Examples:

* 15:15:15
* 16:16:16
* 17:17:17
* 18:18:18
* 19:19:19

## Suitable Use

Balanced NPK may be useful when crops need all three nutrients in similar proportion.

It may be used in:

* Vegetables
* Nurseries
* Fruit crops
* General crop growth
* Fertigation programs
* Corrective feeding where all nutrients are needed

## Caution

Balanced NPK is not always correct. If soil already has high phosphorus or potassium, balanced NPK may waste nutrients.

---

# High Nitrogen Ratios

High nitrogen fertilizers contain more nitrogen compared with phosphorus and potassium.

Examples:

* Urea: 46:00:00
* Calcium nitrate: around 15.5:00:00 plus calcium
* 30:10:10 in some specialty fertilizers
* 20:10:10 in some crop programs

## Suitable Use

High nitrogen fertilizers may be useful during vegetative growth.

## Caution

Avoid excess nitrogen during flowering and fruiting because it can increase leafy growth and reduce fruit quality.

---

# High Phosphorus Ratios

High phosphorus fertilizers contain more phosphorus.

Examples:

* 12:32:16
* 14:35:14
* 00:52:34
* DAP: 18:46:00
* SSP: around 00:16:00 plus sulfur and calcium

## Suitable Use

High phosphorus fertilizers are useful for:

* Root establishment
* Early growth
* Transplanting stage
* Flower initiation where recommended
* Basal application
* Poor phosphorus soils

## Caution

Excess phosphorus can cause micronutrient imbalance, especially zinc deficiency.

---

# High Potassium Ratios

High potassium fertilizers contain more potassium.

Examples:

* 13:00:45
* 00:00:50
* 00:00:60
* 00:52:34
* 12:12:36
* 05:05:45 in some specialty products

## Suitable Use

High potassium fertilizers are useful during:

* Flowering
* Fruiting
* Fruit enlargement
* Grain filling
* Stress periods
* Quality improvement stage

## Benefits

* Better fruit quality
* Better fruit size
* Better color
* Better sweetness
* Better shelf life
* Better stress tolerance

## Caution

Excess potassium can reduce calcium and magnesium uptake.

# NPK Ratios by Crop Stage

| Crop Stage         | Nutrient Need                              | Suitable NPK Type                  |
| ------------------ | ------------------------------------------ | ---------------------------------- |
| Nursery            | Balanced and mild nutrition                | Low-dose balanced NPK              |
| Root establishment | More phosphorus                            | High phosphorus NPK                |
| Vegetative growth  | More nitrogen                              | Nitrogen-rich or balanced NPK      |
| Flowering          | Balanced NPK with phosphorus and potassium | Balanced or P-K rich fertilizer    |
| Fruit development  | More potassium                             | High potassium fertilizer          |
| Grain filling      | More potassium and balanced nitrogen       | K-rich or crop-specific fertilizer |
| Maturity           | Lower nitrogen                             | Avoid excess nitrogen              |

---

# NPK Ratio for Nursery Stage

Nursery plants need gentle and balanced nutrition.

## Requirements

* Healthy roots
* Strong seedlings
* Moderate leaf growth
* Disease resistance
* No salt stress

## Advisory

Use mild fertilizer concentration.

Avoid strong fertilizer dose because young seedlings are sensitive.

Over-fertilization can burn roots and produce weak seedlings.

---

# NPK Ratio for Transplanting Stage

At transplanting, crops need root development and recovery.

## Useful Nutrient Focus

* Phosphorus for root growth
* Moderate nitrogen for early growth
* Potassium for stress tolerance

## Advisory

High-phosphorus fertilizers may be used where recommended.

Do not place concentrated fertilizer directly touching roots.

---

# NPK Ratio for Vegetative Stage

During vegetative stage, crops need nitrogen for leaf and stem development.

## Suitable Fertilizer Type

* Nitrogen-rich fertilizer
* Balanced NPK
* Split nitrogen application
* Crop-specific fertigation schedule

## Advisory

Avoid overuse of nitrogen, especially in vegetables and fruit crops.

Excess vegetative growth can delay flowering and increase pest attack.

---

# NPK Ratio for Flowering Stage

During flowering, plants need balanced nutrition.

## Nutrient Focus

* Phosphorus for flowering and energy
* Potassium for flower retention and stress tolerance
* Moderate nitrogen to avoid excessive leaf growth

## Advisory

Avoid sudden heavy nitrogen application during flowering.

Maintain irrigation and micronutrients such as boron and zinc where needed.

---

# NPK Ratio for Fruiting Stage

During fruiting, potassium becomes very important.

## Nutrient Focus

* Potassium for fruit size and quality
* Calcium for fruit firmness
* Magnesium for photosynthesis
* Moderate nitrogen for continued growth
* Balanced micronutrients

## Advisory

Use potassium-rich fertilizer only if crop needs it.

Avoid excessive potassium because it can reduce calcium uptake and increase blossom-end rot risk in tomato and chilli.

---

# NPK Ratio for Grain Filling Stage

Grain crops need potassium and balanced nitrogen during grain filling.

## Benefits of Potassium

* Better grain filling
* Improved stress tolerance
* Stronger stems
* Better grain weight
* Reduced lodging risk

## Advisory

Avoid late excess nitrogen because it may delay maturity and increase lodging risk.

---

# NPK Ratio for Tomato

Tomato has different nutrient needs at different stages.

## Nursery Stage

* Mild balanced nutrition
* Avoid high salt concentration

## Vegetative Stage

* Moderate nitrogen
* Balanced phosphorus and potassium

## Flowering Stage

* Balanced NPK
* Avoid excess nitrogen
* Maintain boron and calcium

## Fruiting Stage

* Higher potassium
* Adequate calcium
* Balanced magnesium
* Avoid excess nitrogen

## Suggested Ratio Logic

| Tomato Stage        | NPK Preference                      |
| ------------------- | ----------------------------------- |
| Root establishment  | Higher phosphorus                   |
| Vegetative growth   | Balanced NPK with moderate nitrogen |
| Flowering           | Balanced NPK, avoid excess N        |
| Fruit enlargement   | Higher potassium                    |
| Fruit quality stage | Potassium plus calcium management   |

## Important Warning

Do not use high potassium blindly. Excess potassium may reduce calcium uptake and increase blossom-end rot risk.

---

# NPK Ratio for Chilli

Chilli requires balanced nutrition and careful nitrogen management.

## Stage-Wise Logic

| Chilli Stage      | NPK Preference                              |
| ----------------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| Early root stage  | Higher phosphorus                           |
| Vegetative growth | Moderate nitrogen and balanced NPK          |
| Flowering         | Balanced NPK                                |
| Fruit formation   | Higher potassium                            |
| Harvesting period | Balanced fertigation with potassium support |

## Caution

Too much nitrogen may cause more leaves, flower drop, and pest attack.

---

# NPK Ratio for Onion

Onion needs good root development and balanced nutrition.

## Stage-Wise Logic

| Onion Stage      | NPK Preference        |
| ---------------- | --------------------- |
| Early stage      | Phosphorus support    |
| Bulb initiation  | Balanced NPK          |
| Bulb development | Higher potassium      |
| Late stage       | Avoid excess nitrogen |

## Caution

Excess nitrogen late in onion can delay maturity and reduce storage quality.

---

# NPK Ratio for Rice

Rice requires nitrogen in split doses and balanced phosphorus and potassium.

## Nutrient Logic

* Nitrogen supports tillering and growth.
* Phosphorus supports root growth.
* Potassium improves stress tolerance and grain filling.

## Advisory

Apply phosphorus and potassium based on soil test.

Split nitrogen application improves efficiency.

Avoid excess nitrogen because it may increase lodging and pest attack.

---

# NPK Ratio for Wheat

Wheat needs nitrogen for tillering and grain formation, phosphorus for roots, and potassium for stress tolerance.

## Stage-Wise Logic

| Wheat Stage     | NPK Preference                              |
| --------------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| Basal           | Phosphorus and potassium with some nitrogen |
| Tillering       | Nitrogen support                            |
| Stem elongation | Balanced nutrition                          |
| Grain filling   | Potassium and balanced nitrogen             |

## Caution

Excess nitrogen can cause lodging.

---

# NPK Ratio for Maize

Maize is a heavy feeder.

## Nutrient Logic

* Nitrogen supports rapid vegetative growth.
* Phosphorus supports root growth.
* Potassium supports stalk strength and grain filling.

## Advisory

Maize often needs split nitrogen application and adequate potassium for strong stalks.

---

# NPK Ratio for Sugarcane

Sugarcane requires high nutrients over a long duration.

## Nutrient Logic

* Nitrogen for cane growth
* Phosphorus for root development
* Potassium for cane quality and sugar accumulation

## Advisory

Potassium is very important for sugarcane quality and stress tolerance.

Use soil-test-based schedule.

---

# NPK Ratio for Coconut

Coconut is a perennial crop with long-term nutrient needs.

## Nutrient Logic

* Nitrogen supports leaf and palm growth.
* Phosphorus supports root health.
* Potassium is highly important for nut yield and quality.

## Advisory

Coconut often responds well to potassium where deficient.

Use organic manure, basin mulching, and split fertilizer application where recommended.

---

# NPK Ratio for Mango

Mango nutrient management changes with tree age and crop stage.

## Stage-Wise Logic

| Mango Stage       | NPK Preference                       |
| ----------------- | ------------------------------------ |
| Young tree growth | Balanced nutrition                   |
| Before flowering  | Avoid excess nitrogen                |
| Fruit development | Potassium support                    |
| After harvest     | Balanced nutrition for tree recovery |

## Caution

Excess nitrogen before flowering may promote vegetative flush instead of flowering.

---

# NPK Ratio for Pulses

Pulses need less nitrogen than cereals because they can fix atmospheric nitrogen through Rhizobium.

## Nutrient Logic

* Phosphorus is important for roots and nodulation.
* Potassium supports stress tolerance.
* Sulfur and micronutrients may be important.
* Seed treatment with Rhizobium is useful.

## Advisory

Avoid heavy nitrogen application in pulses.

Use phosphorus based on soil test.

---

# NPK Ratio for Oilseeds

Oilseeds need balanced NPK and sulfur.

## Nutrient Logic

* Nitrogen supports growth.
* Phosphorus supports roots.
* Potassium supports stress tolerance.
* Sulfur supports oil formation and quality.

## Advisory

Do not ignore sulfur in oilseed crops.

# Water-Soluble NPK Fertilizers

Water-soluble NPK fertilizers are used in fertigation and foliar feeding.

Common grades:

* 19:19:19
* 12:61:00
* 00:52:34
* 13:00:45
* 00:00:50
* 20:20:20
* 18:18:18

## Benefits

* Quick nutrient availability
* Suitable for drip irrigation
* Can match crop stage
* Better nutrient-use efficiency
* Useful in high-value crops

## Caution

Use correct concentration.

High concentration can burn roots or leaves.

Check compatibility before mixing fertilizers.

---

# NPK Fertigation

Fertigation means applying fertilizers through irrigation water.

## Benefits

* Better nutrient-use efficiency
* Split nutrient application
* Reduced labor
* Uniform nutrient delivery
* Better crop response
* Suitable for drip irrigation

## Important Practices

* Use water-soluble fertilizers.
* Follow crop-stage schedule.
* Flush drip system after fertigation.
* Avoid incompatible mixing.
* Clean filters regularly.
* Monitor soil moisture.

---

# Foliar NPK Sprays

Foliar NPK sprays are used to correct temporary nutrient stress or support critical crop stages.

## Suitable Situations

* Nutrient stress
* Flowering support
* Fruit development support
* Poor root uptake due to stress
* Quick correction where recommended

## Caution

Foliar spray cannot replace full soil nutrition.

Use low recommended concentration.

Avoid spraying during hot afternoon.

NPK fertilizers supply nutrients, but organic manures improve soil health.

Use NPK along with:

* Farmyard manure
* Compost
* Vermicompost
* Green manure
* Crop residues
* Biofertilizers
* Mulch

Organic matter improves:

* Soil structure
* Water retention
* Microbial activity
* Nutrient cycling
* Fertilizer efficiency

# NPK and Soil Testing

Soil testing is essential before deciding NPK fertilizer.

Soil test helps know:

* Available nitrogen
* Available phosphorus
* Available potassium
* Soil pH
* Salinity
* Organic carbon
* Micronutrients

Without soil testing, farmers may apply nutrients that are already sufficient and ignore deficient nutrients.

---

# NPK and Soil pH

Soil pH affects nutrient availability.

## Acidic Soil

Phosphorus availability may reduce.

Lime may be needed if soil test recommends.

## Alkaline Soil

Phosphorus and micronutrients such as zinc and iron may become less available.

Organic matter and proper fertilizer placement help.

---

# NPK and Fertilizer Placement

Correct placement improves nutrient-use efficiency.

## Nitrogen

Can be split and applied near root zone.

## Phosphorus

Usually applied as basal because it moves slowly in soil.

## Potassium

Can be basal or split depending on crop and soil.

In drip irrigation, soluble fertilizers can be applied in split doses.

---

# NPK Calculation Basics

To calculate nutrient supplied:

Nutrient supplied = Fertilizer quantity × nutrient percentage / 100

## Example 1

If farmer applies 100 kg of 19:19:19:

* Nitrogen supplied = 100 × 19 / 100 = 19 kg
* P2O5 supplied = 100 × 19 / 100 = 19 kg
* K2O supplied = 100 × 19 / 100 = 19 kg

## Example 2

If farmer applies 50 kg of 12:32:16:

* Nitrogen supplied = 50 × 12 / 100 = 6 kg
* P2O5 supplied = 50 × 32 / 100 = 16 kg
* K2O supplied = 50 × 16 / 100 = 8 kg

# Choosing NPK Fertilizer

Farmers should choose NPK fertilizer based on:

1. Soil test
2. Crop requirement
3. Crop stage
4. Irrigation method
5. Fertilizer availability
6. Cost per nutrient
7. Organic manure use
8. Previous crop
9. Soil pH
10. Local recommendation

Do not choose fertilizer only because it is popular or promoted by a dealer.

---

# Cost Per Nutrient

Farmers should compare fertilizers based on nutrient content, not only bag price.

A cheaper bag may supply fewer nutrients.

A costly fertilizer may be economical if nutrient concentration and crop response are better.

Calculate cost per kg nutrient when possible.

---

# Common NPK Mistakes

Farmers should avoid:

* Using same NPK grade for all crops
* Using same NPK throughout crop season
* Applying high nitrogen during flowering
* Applying high potassium without calcium balance
* Ignoring soil test
* Ignoring micronutrients
* Applying phosphorus repeatedly when soil P is already high
* Applying fertilizer before heavy rain
* Mixing incompatible water-soluble fertilizers
* Using high concentration foliar spray
* Applying fertilizer too close to seed or roots
* Ignoring organic matter

# NPK Ratio and Deficiency Symptoms

| Symptom                     | Possible Nutrient Issue                        |
| --------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------- |
| Yellow older leaves         | Nitrogen deficiency                            |
| Purple leaves or poor roots | Phosphorus deficiency                          |
| Leaf edge scorching         | Potassium deficiency                           |
| Excess leafy growth         | Too much nitrogen                              |
| Poor fruit quality          | Potassium, calcium, or micronutrient imbalance |
| Poor flowering              | Phosphorus, boron, or stress issue             |
| Weak stems                  | Potassium deficiency or excess nitrogen        |
| Delayed maturity            | Excess nitrogen                                |

Symptoms should be confirmed with soil or tissue testing where possible.

# NPK Application Checklist

## Before Application

* Check soil test report.
* Identify crop stage.
* Select suitable NPK grade.
* Calculate required quantity.
* Check fertilizer quality.
* Check irrigation availability.
* Avoid application before heavy rain.

## During Application

* Apply correct dose.
* Avoid direct seed contact.
* Apply uniformly.
* Use split application where recommended.
* Use fertigation for drip crops if suitable.
* Keep fertilizer away from wet storage conditions.

## After Application

* Irrigate if needed.
* Observe crop response.
* Record fertilizer used.
* Watch for deficiency or toxicity symptoms.
* Adjust future dose based on crop response and test results.

# Farmer Recommendations

Farmers are advised to:

1. Understand NPK numbers before buying fertilizer.
2. Use soil-test-based fertilizer planning.
3. Match NPK ratio with crop stage.
4. Use phosphorus mainly for root and early growth.
5. Use nitrogen carefully for vegetative growth.
6. Use potassium during fruiting and grain filling.
7. Avoid excess nitrogen during flowering and fruiting.
8. Avoid excess potassium where calcium deficiency risk exists.
9. Combine NPK fertilizers with organic manure and micronutrients.
10. Keep fertilizer records for each field and crop.
