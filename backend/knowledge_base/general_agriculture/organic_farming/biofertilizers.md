# Biofertilizers in Organic Farming

# Overview

Biofertilizers are natural preparations containing beneficial living microorganisms that improve soil fertility, nutrient availability, plant growth, and crop productivity. They are an important part of organic farming, sustainable agriculture, integrated nutrient management, and climate-smart farming.

Biofertilizers help crops by fixing atmospheric nitrogen, solubilizing phosphorus, mobilizing potassium, improving root growth, increasing nutrient uptake, and supporting soil microbial activity.

Biofertilizers help farmers:

* Reduce dependence on chemical fertilizers
* Improve soil fertility naturally
* Increase nutrient availability
* Improve root development
* Support organic farming
* Improve soil health
* Reduce input cost over time
* Improve crop resilience
* Support sustainable crop production

Biofertilizers do not replace all fertilizers immediately. They work best when used with compost, farmyard manure, green manure, crop residues, and balanced nutrient management.

---

# What are Biofertilizers?

Biofertilizers are products that contain beneficial microorganisms such as bacteria, fungi, or algae. When applied to seed, soil, compost, nursery, or plant roots, these microorganisms multiply and help plants obtain nutrients from the soil and atmosphere.

They improve nutrient availability through natural biological processes.

Biofertilizers may help with:

* Nitrogen fixation
* Phosphorus solubilization
* Potassium mobilization
* Zinc solubilization
* Organic matter decomposition
* Root growth stimulation
* Soil microbial balance

---

# Importance of Biofertilizers

Biofertilizers are important because they improve soil fertility in an eco-friendly way.

They help:

* Maintain soil biological activity
* Improve nutrient cycling
* Reduce fertilizer wastage
* Improve crop growth
* Increase soil organic matter activity
* Reduce environmental pollution
* Support long-term farm sustainability

In organic farming, biofertilizers are useful because chemical fertilizers are avoided or restricted.

---

# How Biofertilizers Work

Biofertilizers work through beneficial microorganisms.

These microorganisms may:

1. Fix nitrogen from the atmosphere.
2. Convert unavailable phosphorus into available form.
3. Mobilize potassium and micronutrients.
4. Produce plant growth-promoting substances.
5. Improve root surface area.
6. Support beneficial soil microbial communities.
7. Improve decomposition of organic matter.

# Major Types of Biofertilizers

---

# Nitrogen-Fixing Biofertilizers

Nitrogen-fixing biofertilizers convert atmospheric nitrogen into forms that plants can use.

## Examples

* Rhizobium
* Azotobacter
* Azospirillum
* Blue-green algae
* Azolla

---

# Phosphorus-Solubilizing Biofertilizers

These microorganisms solubilize insoluble phosphorus in soil and make it available to plants.

## Examples

* Phosphate Solubilizing Bacteria
* Phosphate Solubilizing Fungi

Commonly known as PSB.

---

# Potassium-Mobilizing Biofertilizers

These biofertilizers help release potassium from soil minerals.

## Examples

* Potassium Mobilizing Bacteria
* Potassium Solubilizing Bacteria

Commonly known as KMB or KSB.

---

# Zinc-Solubilizing Biofertilizers

These microorganisms help improve zinc availability.

Zinc is important for:

* Enzyme activity
* Plant growth
* Flowering
* Grain development
* Fruit quality

---

# Mycorrhizal Biofertilizers

Mycorrhizae are beneficial fungi that form a relationship with plant roots.

They help plants absorb:

* Phosphorus
* Water
* Micronutrients

Mycorrhizae are especially useful in poor soils, orchards, plantation crops, and drought-prone areas.

---

# Plant Growth-Promoting Rhizobacteria

Plant Growth-Promoting Rhizobacteria, or PGPR, are beneficial bacteria found near plant roots.

They may help by:

* Improving root growth
* Producing growth-promoting substances
* Enhancing nutrient uptake
* Supporting crop stress tolerance

---

# Decomposer Biofertilizers

Decomposer cultures help break down crop residues and organic waste.

They are useful for:

* Composting
* Farm waste decomposition
* Crop residue management
* Improving organic manure quality

# Biofertilizer Application Methods

---

# Seed Treatment

Seed treatment is one of the most common methods.

## Procedure

1. Prepare a mild jaggery or sugar solution as sticker.
2. Mix biofertilizer with the solution.
3. Coat seeds uniformly.
4. Dry treated seeds in shade.
5. Sow soon after treatment.

## Suitable For

* Pulses
* Cereals
* Oilseeds
* Vegetables

## Important Precaution

Do not dry treated seed under direct sunlight.

---

# Seedling Root Dip

Seedling root dipping is useful for transplanted crops.

## Procedure

1. Mix biofertilizer in water or slurry.
2. Dip seedling roots for recommended time.
3. Transplant immediately.

## Suitable Crops

* Rice
* Tomato
* Chilli
* Brinjal
* Onion
* Cabbage
* Cauliflower

---

# Soil Application

Biofertilizers can be mixed with compost or farmyard manure and applied to soil.

## Procedure

1. Mix biofertilizer with well-decomposed compost or FYM.
2. Keep mixture in shade for some time.
3. Apply near the root zone or in the field.
4. Maintain adequate soil moisture.

## Suitable For

* Field crops
* Vegetables
* Orchards
* Plantation crops

---

# Nursery Application

Biofertilizers may be applied in nursery beds or potting media.

## Benefits

* Improves seedling growth
* Enhances root development
* Reduces transplant shock
* Supports healthy nursery production

---

# Drip or Fertigation Application

Some liquid biofertilizers may be applied through drip irrigation if they are compatible and properly filtered.

## Precautions

* Use only recommended liquid formulations.
* Avoid clogging.
* Follow supplier or expert guidance.
* Do not mix with harsh chemicals.

# Compatibility of Biofertilizers

Biofertilizers contain living organisms, so compatibility is important.

## Compatible With

* Compost
* Farmyard manure
* Vermicompost
* Green manure
* Organic mulches
* Soil application
* Some bio-pesticides if recommended

## Avoid Mixing With

* Strong chemical pesticides
* Fungicides
* Highly concentrated fertilizers
* Hot compost
* Direct sunlight
* Chlorinated water in high concentration

Chemical seed treatment and biofertilizer seed treatment should not be mixed directly unless recommended.

---

# Storage of Biofertilizers

Biofertilizers are living products and must be stored carefully.

## Storage Guidelines

* Store in a cool and dry place.
* Keep away from direct sunlight.
* Do not store near pesticides or chemicals.
* Check expiry date before use.
* Use before expiry.
* Avoid high temperature storage.
* Keep packets sealed until use.

Expired or overheated biofertilizers may not work effectively.

---

# Quality of Biofertilizers

Good-quality biofertilizers are important for results.

## Check Before Buying

# Limitations of Biofertilizers

Biofertilizers are useful but have limitations.

## Limitations

* Results may be slow.
* They need proper storage.
* They are crop-specific in some cases.
* They do not supply large nutrient quantities instantly.
* Poor-quality products may fail.
* Dry soil reduces effectiveness.
* Direct sunlight and heat can kill microbes.

Farmers should use biofertilizers as part of a complete nutrient management plan.

---

# Common Mistakes to Avoid

Farmers should avoid:

* Using expired biofertilizers
* Keeping packets in sunlight
* Mixing directly with strong chemicals
* Applying to very dry soil
* Using wrong biofertilizer for the crop
* Expecting instant fertilizer-like response
* Using poor-quality or fake products
* Applying with hot compost
* Ignoring organic matter and soil moisture

# Farmer Recommendations

Farmers are advised to:

1. Select biofertilizer according to crop and purpose.
2. Use Rhizobium only for suitable legume crops.
3. Use PSB where phosphorus availability is low.
4. Use VAM for orchards, plantation crops, and nurseries.
5. Store biofertilizers in cool and shaded conditions.
6. Check expiry date before use.
7. Use biofertilizers with compost or farmyard manure.
8. Maintain soil moisture after application.
9. Avoid direct mixing with pesticides or fungicides.
10. Use biofertilizers as part of a full nutrient management plan.
