# Soil Organic Matter

# Overview

Soil organic matter is one of the most important components of healthy and productive soil. It is made from decomposed plant residues, animal waste, roots, microorganisms, compost, manure, and other organic materials present in the soil.

Soil organic matter improves soil fertility, soil structure, water-holding capacity, nutrient availability, microbial activity, and crop resilience. It is especially important in sustainable farming, organic farming, climate-smart agriculture, dryland farming, and long-term soil health management.

Soil organic matter helps farmers:

* Improve soil fertility
* Increase water retention
* Improve soil structure
* Support beneficial microorganisms
* Reduce soil erosion
* Improve nutrient cycling
* Reduce drought stress
* Improve crop growth
* Increase fertilizer-use efficiency
* Build long-term farm productivity

Low soil organic matter can lead to poor soil fertility, hard soil, low water retention, weak crop growth, erosion, and higher input dependency.

---

# What is Soil Organic Matter?

Soil organic matter is the organic portion of soil formed from living and decomposed materials.

It includes:

* Decomposed crop residues
* Plant roots
* Dead leaves
* Farmyard manure
* Compost
* Vermicompost
* Green manure
* Soil microorganisms
* Earthworm castings
* Humus

Soil organic matter is not just waste material. It is a living and active part of soil that supports crop growth and soil health.

---

# Components of Soil Organic Matter

Soil organic matter includes different stages of decomposition.

## Fresh Organic Material

This includes recently added plant and animal materials.

Examples:

* Crop residues
* Leaves
* Straw
* Grass clippings
* Animal dung
* Fresh roots

These materials decompose over time and release nutrients.

---

## Partially Decomposed Organic Matter

This material has started breaking down but is not fully decomposed.

Examples:

* Partially decomposed compost
* Crop residues in soil
* Mulch undergoing decomposition
* Manure in decomposition stage

---

## Humus

Humus is stable, dark, well-decomposed organic matter.

It improves:

* Nutrient holding capacity
* Soil structure
* Water retention
* Soil color
* Microbial activity

Humus decomposes slowly and provides long-term soil benefits.

---

## Living Soil Organisms

Soil organic matter supports many living organisms.

Examples:

* Bacteria
* Fungi
* Actinomycetes
* Earthworms
* Protozoa
* Beneficial nematodes
* Soil insects

These organisms help decompose residues and release nutrients.

---

# Importance of Soil Organic Matter

Soil organic matter is important because it improves physical, chemical, and biological properties of soil.

## Physical Benefits

* Improves soil structure
* Reduces compaction
* Improves root penetration
* Increases water infiltration
* Reduces runoff
* Reduces crust formation
* Improves soil aeration

---

## Chemical Benefits

* Improves nutrient availability
* Holds nutrients in the root zone
* Reduces nutrient leaching
* Improves cation exchange capacity
* Supports balanced fertility
* Buffers soil pH changes

---

## Biological Benefits

* Provides food for soil microorganisms
* Increases earthworm activity
* Supports nutrient cycling
* Improves decomposition
* Encourages beneficial microbes
* Helps suppress some soil-borne problems

---

# Soil Organic Matter and Soil Fertility

Soil organic matter slowly releases nutrients as it decomposes.

It supplies or improves availability of:

* Nitrogen
* Phosphorus
* Potassium
* Sulfur
* Calcium
* Magnesium
* Micronutrients

Organic matter also improves the efficiency of chemical fertilizers by helping nutrients remain available to crop roots for a longer time.

---

# Soil Organic Matter and Water Retention

Organic matter improves the soil’s ability to hold water.

This is useful in:

* Sandy soils
* Drought-prone areas
* Rainfed farming
* Vegetable cultivation
* Orchards
* Plantation crops

Soils with higher organic matter can store more water and release it gradually to crops.

---

# Soil Organic Matter and Soil Structure

Organic matter helps soil particles bind together into stable aggregates.

Good aggregation improves:

* Root growth
* Water movement
* Air movement
* Drainage
* Soil workability
* Resistance to erosion

Poor organic matter can make soil hard, compacted, crusted, and difficult for roots to penetrate.

---

# Soil Organic Matter and Microbial Activity

Soil microorganisms use organic matter as food.

Beneficial microorganisms help:

* Decompose residues
* Fix nitrogen
* Solubilize phosphorus
* Mobilize nutrients
* Improve root-zone health
* Support plant growth

Organic matter is the main energy source for soil biological life.

---

# Sources of Soil Organic Matter

---

# Farmyard Manure

Farmyard manure is a common source of organic matter.

## Benefits

* Adds nutrients
* Improves soil structure
* Improves microbial activity
* Increases water retention

## Precaution

Use well-decomposed farmyard manure. Fresh manure may contain weed seeds, pathogens, or excess heat.

---

# Compost

Compost is decomposed organic waste prepared from crop residues, animal waste, leaves, and other biodegradable materials.

## Benefits

* Adds stable organic matter
* Improves soil health
* Recycles farm waste
* Reduces waste burning

---

# Vermicompost

Vermicompost is organic manure produced by earthworms.

## Benefits

* Rich in beneficial microbes
* Improves root growth
* Supports nursery and vegetable crops
* Enhances soil biological activity

---

# Green Manure

Green manure crops are grown and incorporated into soil before maturity.

## Examples

* Sunhemp
* Dhaincha
* Cowpea
* Green gram
* Sesbania

## Benefits

* Adds biomass
* Improves nitrogen
* Increases soil organic matter
* Improves microbial activity

---

# Crop Residues

Crop residues include straw, stalks, leaves, husks, and roots remaining after harvest.

## Good Practices

* Use residues as mulch
* Incorporate residues where suitable
* Compost residues
* Avoid burning residues

Residue recycling is one of the easiest ways to increase soil organic matter.

---

# Mulch

Mulch is material placed on the soil surface.

## Organic Mulch Materials

* Straw
* Dry leaves
* Grass clippings
* Coconut leaves
* Sugarcane trash
* Crop residues
* Compost

As organic mulch decomposes, it adds organic matter to soil.

---

# Cover Crops

Cover crops are grown to protect soil during fallow periods or between main crops.

## Benefits

* Adds biomass
* Prevents erosion
* Improves soil structure
* Suppresses weeds
* Improves soil moisture

---

# Root Biomass

Plant roots contribute significantly to soil organic matter.

Deep-rooted and fibrous-rooted crops add organic carbon below the soil surface.

Examples:

* Grasses
* Millets
* Sorghum
* Pigeon pea
* Cover crops

---

# Biochar

Biochar is a carbon-rich material produced from biomass under limited oxygen.

## Benefits

* Adds stable carbon
* Improves water retention
* Supports nutrient holding
* May improve microbial habitat

Biochar should be used carefully and preferably mixed with compost or manure.

---

# Factors Affecting Soil Organic Matter

---

# Climate

Hot and humid climates decompose organic matter faster.

Dry climates may slow decomposition but also produce less biomass.

Farmers in warm regions need regular organic matter addition.

---

# Soil Texture

## Sandy Soil

Sandy soils usually have low organic matter because they drain quickly and have low nutrient-holding capacity.

## Clay Soil

Clay soils can hold organic matter better but may suffer from poor aeration if compacted.

## Loamy Soil

Loamy soils can maintain organic matter better with proper management.

---

# Tillage

Frequent deep tillage exposes organic matter to air and speeds decomposition.

Reduced tillage can help conserve soil organic matter.

---

# Crop Rotation

Crop rotation affects organic matter levels.

Rotations with legumes, cover crops, green manure, and high-biomass crops improve organic matter.

Continuous monocropping may reduce organic matter.

---

# Residue Management

Removing or burning all residues reduces soil organic matter.

Returning residues to soil improves organic matter over time.

---

# Erosion

Erosion removes topsoil, which contains the highest amount of organic matter.

Soil conservation is necessary to protect organic matter.

---

# Irrigation and Drainage

Balanced moisture supports decomposition and microbial activity.

Excess water may reduce aeration, while dry soil slows microbial activity.

---

# Causes of Low Soil Organic Matter

Soil organic matter decreases due to:

* Burning crop residues
* Removing all biomass from the field
* Excessive tillage
* Soil erosion
* Low manure application
* Continuous cropping
* Lack of cover crops
* Poor crop rotation
* Overgrazing
* Low root biomass
* High temperature and rapid decomposition

---

# Signs of Low Soil Organic Matter

Fields with low organic matter may show:

* Hard soil surface
* Poor water retention
* Crusting after rain
* Low earthworm activity
* Poor crop growth
* Low fertilizer response
* Weak root growth
* High runoff
* Soil erosion
* Poor soil color
* Frequent drought stress

Soil testing can confirm organic carbon status.

---

# Soil Organic Carbon

Soil organic carbon is the carbon portion of soil organic matter.

It is an important indicator of soil health.

Higher soil organic carbon usually means:

* Better fertility
* Better structure
* Better water retention
* Better microbial activity
* Better climate resilience

Soil organic carbon improves slowly and needs long-term management.

---

# Soil Testing for Organic Matter

Soil testing can measure organic carbon or organic matter.

Farmers should test soil to know:

* Organic carbon level
* Nutrient status
* Soil pH
* Salinity
* Fertility condition

Soil organic matter should be monitored over years rather than expecting quick changes in one season.

---

# How to Improve Soil Organic Matter

---

# Add Compost Regularly

Compost adds stable organic material and improves soil biological activity.

Recommendations:

* Apply before sowing or planting
* Mix with topsoil
* Use mature compost
* Avoid contaminated compost

---

# Apply Farmyard Manure

Farmyard manure improves soil fertility and water retention.

Recommendations:

* Use well-rotted manure
* Apply before land preparation
* Avoid applying fresh manure directly to seedlings
* Store manure properly before use

---

# Use Vermicompost

Vermicompost is especially useful for vegetables, nurseries, fruits, and high-value crops.

Recommendations:

* Use mature vermicompost
* Apply near root zone
* Store in shade
* Combine with other organic sources

---

# Grow Green Manure Crops

Green manure quickly adds biomass and improves fertility.

Suitable green manure crops:

* Sunhemp
* Dhaincha
* Cowpea
* Green gram
* Sesbania

Incorporate green manure before flowering for faster decomposition.

---

# Retain Crop Residues

Crop residues should be recycled instead of burned.

Good options:

* Mulching
* Composting
* Soil incorporation
* Decomposer culture use

Residue retention improves organic matter and moisture conservation.

---

# Use Cover Crops

Cover crops protect soil and add biomass.

Examples:

* Cowpea
* Sunhemp
* Clover
* Green gram
* Fodder grasses

They are useful during fallow periods and between orchard rows.

---

# Practice Crop Rotation

Good crop rotation improves organic matter by including different root systems and biomass sources.

Recommended rotations:

* Cereal → Pulse
* Vegetable → Cereal → Pulse
* Crop → Green manure
* Orchard → Cover crop

---

# Reduce Excessive Tillage

Reduced tillage helps protect soil organic matter.

Benefits:

* Less organic matter loss
* Better soil structure
* Less erosion
* Better moisture retention

---

# Prevent Soil Erosion

Soil erosion removes organic-rich topsoil.

Practices:

* Mulching
* Cover crops
* Contour bunding
* Field bunding
* Grass strips
* Terracing
* Windbreaks

---

# Integrate Livestock and Crops

Livestock can help recycle nutrients through manure.

Good practices:

* Collect manure properly
* Compost manure
* Use biogas slurry
* Apply manure to fields
* Avoid unmanaged waste runoff

---

# Soil Organic Matter in Different Soil Types

---

# Sandy Soil

Sandy soils need frequent organic matter addition.

Recommendations:

* Apply compost and FYM regularly
* Use mulch
* Grow cover crops
* Avoid over-irrigation
* Use drip irrigation where possible

---

# Clay Soil

Clay soils benefit from organic matter because it improves structure and aeration.

Recommendations:

* Add compost
* Avoid working soil when too wet
* Improve drainage
* Use deep-rooted crops
* Reduce compaction

---

# Loamy Soil

Loamy soils are easier to manage but still need regular organic matter addition.

Recommendations:

* Maintain crop rotation
* Add manure or compost
* Recycle residues
* Use green manure when possible

---

# Soil Organic Matter in Crop Systems

---

# Vegetable Farming

Vegetables often remove high nutrients and require good soil structure.

Recommended practices:

* Compost application
* Vermicompost in nursery
* Mulching
* Crop rotation
* Biofertilizers
* Green manure between crops

---

# Fruit Orchards

Orchards benefit from long-term organic matter management.

Recommended practices:

* Basin mulching
* Compost or FYM application
* Cover crops between rows
* Recycling pruning biomass
* Biofertilizer use

---

# Plantation Crops

Plantation crops such as coconut, coffee, tea, cocoa, and arecanut benefit from biomass recycling.

Recommended practices:

* Leaf litter recycling
* Basin mulching
* Green manure crops
* Intercropping
* Organic manure application

---

# Grain Crops

Grain crops produce residues that should be recycled.

Recommended practices:

* Avoid burning straw
* Use residue incorporation
* Include pulses in rotation
* Apply compost or FYM where available

---

# Rainfed Farming

Organic matter is very important in rainfed farming because it improves water retention.

Recommended practices:

* Mulching
* Conservation tillage
* Farmyard manure
* Cover crops
* Crop residue retention
* Drought-tolerant crops

---

# Soil Organic Matter in Tomato

Tomato requires fertile, well-drained soil with good organic matter.

Benefits:

* Better root growth
* Better nutrient availability
* Reduced moisture stress
* Improved soil structure
* Better microbial activity

Recommended practices:

* Apply well-decomposed compost or FYM
* Use vermicompost in nursery
* Apply mulch
* Rotate with cereals or pulses
* Avoid fresh undecomposed manure near roots

---

# Soil Organic Matter in Coconut

Coconut benefits strongly from organic matter in basins.

Recommended practices:

* Recycle coconut leaves as mulch
* Apply FYM or compost
* Grow green manure crops in basins
* Use intercrops
* Maintain basin moisture
* Use biofertilizers with compost

---

# Soil Organic Matter in Mango

Mango orchards benefit from basin organic matter management.

Recommended practices:

* Apply compost or FYM around tree basins
* Use mulch
* Recycle pruning residues where safe
* Grow cover crops between rows
* Avoid deep digging near roots

---

# Soil Organic Matter and Nutrient Cycling

Organic matter acts as a slow-release nutrient source.

As it decomposes, nutrients become available to plants.

Benefits:

* Reduces nutrient loss
* Improves fertilizer efficiency
* Supports microbial nutrient transformation
* Improves long-term soil fertility

---

# Soil Organic Matter and Climate Resilience

Soil organic matter improves resilience to climate stress.

## During Drought

* Stores more water
* Reduces evaporation
* Supports deeper roots
* Reduces plant stress

## During Heavy Rain

* Improves infiltration
* Reduces runoff
* Reduces erosion
* Helps soil recover faster

## During Heat

* Mulch and organic matter reduce soil temperature
* Supports microbial activity
* Protects roots

---

# Soil Organic Matter and Carbon Farming

Soil organic matter stores carbon in soil.

Practices that build soil organic matter also support carbon farming:

* Composting
* Green manuring
* Cover cropping
* Reduced tillage
* Agroforestry
* Residue retention
* Biochar use

Increasing soil organic matter is a long-term climate-smart farming strategy.

---

# Soil Organic Matter and Fertilizer Efficiency

Organic matter improves fertilizer-use efficiency by:

* Holding nutrients in the root zone
* Reducing leaching
* Supporting microbial nutrient release
* Improving root growth
* Improving soil moisture

Fields with very low organic matter may show poor response to fertilizers.

---

# Soil Organic Matter and Pest-Disease Resistance

Healthy soils with organic matter support stronger plants.

Benefits:

* Better root health
* Improved nutrient balance
* Better crop recovery
* Increased beneficial microbial activity

However, undecomposed or contaminated organic waste can increase disease problems. Use well-decomposed organic inputs.

---

# Soil Organic Matter Management Plan

## Step 1: Test Soil

Check organic carbon, pH, NPK, and micronutrients.

## Step 2: Identify Organic Matter Sources

List available sources:

* Farmyard manure
* Compost
* Crop residues
* Green manure seeds
* Vermicompost
* Livestock manure
* Leaf litter

## Step 3: Reduce Organic Matter Loss

Avoid:

* Burning residues
* Excessive tillage
* Soil erosion
* Overgrazing
* Bare soil exposure

## Step 4: Add Organic Matter Regularly

Use compost, manure, green manure, mulch, and residues.

## Step 5: Keep Soil Covered

Use mulch, cover crops, or crop residues.

## Step 6: Monitor Crop Response

Check crop growth, soil moisture, earthworms, and yield.

---

# Common Mistakes to Avoid

Farmers should avoid:

* Burning crop residues
* Applying fresh manure directly near roots
* Removing all biomass from the field
* Keeping soil bare for long periods
* Excessive deep tillage
* Ignoring erosion control
* Using contaminated compost
* Expecting organic matter to improve in one season
* Ignoring soil testing
* Over-irrigating organic matter-rich soils

---

# Farmer Recommendations

Farmers are advised to:

1. Add organic matter every season where possible.
2. Use well-decomposed compost, FYM, or vermicompost.
3. Grow green manure or cover crops.
4. Retain crop residues instead of burning them.
5. Use mulch to protect soil.
6. Reduce unnecessary deep tillage.
7. Prevent erosion through bunds, cover crops, and contour farming.
8. Include legumes in crop rotation.
9. Maintain soil moisture for microbial activity.
10. Test soil organic carbon regularly.

---

# Soil Organic Matter Decision Guide

| Field Situation          | Recommended Action                      |
| ------------------------ | --------------------------------------- |
| Sandy soil dries quickly | Add compost, mulch, and use cover crops |
| Hard soil surface        | Add organic matter and reduce tillage   |
| Low earthworm activity   | Add compost and avoid residue burning   |
| Poor fertilizer response | Improve organic matter and check pH     |
| Drought-prone field      | Use mulch and organic manure            |
| Vegetable field          | Use compost, vermicompost, and rotation |
| Orchard basin            | Apply mulch and FYM or compost          |
| Erosion-prone field      | Use cover crops and bunding             |
| Crop residues available  | Compost or use as mulch                 |
| Low organic carbon       | Long-term organic matter addition plan  |

# Soil Organic Matter Checklist

## Before Season

* Check available compost or manure.
* Plan green manure or cover crop.
* Collect crop residues.
* Prepare mulch material.
* Test soil if possible.

## During Crop Growth

* Maintain mulch cover.
* Avoid unnecessary soil disturbance.
* Keep weeds under control.
* Maintain soil moisture.
* Monitor crop response.

## After Harvest

* Retain or compost crop residues.
* Avoid burning.
* Add organic matter before next crop.
* Record yield and soil condition.
* Plan crop rotation.
