# Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana

# Overview

Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana, commonly called PMFBY, is a crop insurance scheme of the Government of India designed to protect farmers from financial losses caused by crop failure or crop damage due to non-preventable natural risks.

PMFBY provides crop insurance coverage for notified crops grown in notified areas during notified seasons. It helps farmers manage risks such as drought, flood, cyclone, hailstorm, unseasonal rain, pest attack, disease outbreak, prevented sowing, standing crop loss, and post-harvest loss, depending on scheme rules and state notification.

PMFBY helps farmers:

* Reduce financial loss after crop damage
* Protect farm income
* Recover after natural calamities
* Continue farming after crop failure
* Reduce debt burden
* Manage weather and climate risks
* Support farm risk management
* Improve confidence in crop investment

Farmers should always check current scheme details, crop notifications, premium rates, deadlines, insurance company details, and claim procedures from the official PMFBY portal, bank, Common Service Centre, agriculture department, or notified insurance company.

---

# What is PMFBY?

PMFBY is a crop insurance scheme that provides risk coverage to farmers for crop losses caused by specified natural and biological risks.

The scheme works through:

* Government notification of crops and areas
* Farmer enrollment before deadline
* Premium payment by farmer
* Government subsidy support
* Insurance company implementation
* Crop loss assessment
* Claim settlement to eligible farmers

The scheme is implemented season-wise, usually for Kharif and Rabi crops, and may also include annual commercial and horticultural crops where notified.

---

# Main Objective of PMFBY

The main objective of PMFBY is to provide affordable crop insurance to farmers and protect them from crop losses caused by unforeseen and non-preventable events.

PMFBY aims to:

1. Provide financial support to farmers after crop loss.
2. Stabilize farm income.
3. Encourage farmers to continue farming after disaster.
4. Support adoption of modern farming practices.
5. Reduce risk in agricultural production.
6. Improve credit flow to agriculture.
7. Support sustainable agricultural production.

# Why PMFBY is Important

Agriculture faces many risks that farmers cannot fully control.

Examples:

* Delayed monsoon
* Drought
* Flooding
* Heavy rainfall
* Cyclone
* Hailstorm
* Pest outbreak
* Disease outbreak
* Unseasonal rainfall
* Crop failure
* Yield reduction

Without insurance, a single disaster can create serious financial stress for farmers. PMFBY reduces this risk by providing compensation according to scheme rules.

# Key Features of PMFBY

## Affordable Premium

PMFBY provides crop insurance at subsidized premium rates for farmers.

The farmer premium share is generally lower than the actuarial premium, and the remaining premium is supported by the government as subsidy.

---

## Wide Risk Coverage

The scheme provides coverage for many non-preventable natural risks from pre-sowing to post-harvest stage, subject to scheme rules and state notification.

---

## Notified Crop and Area System

Insurance is available for crops and areas officially notified by the state or government authority.

Farmers should confirm that their crop and village or insurance unit are notified before enrollment.

---

## Technology Use

PMFBY uses digital systems such as the National Crop Insurance Portal, mobile apps, remote sensing, weather data, and crop assessment systems where applicable.

---

## Claim Payment to Bank Account

Approved claims are usually credited directly to the farmer’s registered bank account.

# Farmer Premium Rates

Under PMFBY, the maximum farmer premium share is generally:

| Season or Crop Type                       | Maximum Farmer Premium Share |
| ----------------------------------------- | ---------------------------- |
| Kharif food and oilseed crops             | 2% of sum insured            |
| Rabi food and oilseed crops               | 1.5% of sum insured          |
| Annual commercial and horticultural crops | 5% of sum insured            |

The remaining premium amount is subsidized according to scheme rules.

Premium details may vary by crop, state, season, notification, and policy conditions. Farmers should calculate current premium using the official premium calculator or confirm with authorized channels.

# Loanee Farmers

Loanee farmers are those who take crop loans from banks or financial institutions.

Important points:

* Banks may enroll eligible crop-loan farmers for notified crops.
* Farmers should verify crop name, season, area, and premium details.
* If the farmer grows a crop different from the crop recorded in the loan, the farmer should inform the bank within the allowed time.
* Farmer should keep premium deduction proof and insurance application details.

# Important Terms in PMFBY

---

# Notified Crop

A crop officially listed for insurance coverage in a particular area and season.

Only notified crops are eligible for scheme coverage.

---

# Notified Area

The geographical area declared eligible for insurance coverage.

It may be a village, gram panchayat, revenue circle, block, taluka, district, or other insurance unit.

---

# Insurance Unit

Insurance unit is the area used for assessment and claim calculation.

For many standing crop losses, claims are calculated using area-level yield assessment.

---

# Sum Insured

Sum insured is the maximum insured value for a crop area as per notification.

It is linked to scale of finance or notified value.

---

# Premium

Premium is the amount paid for crop insurance coverage.

The farmer pays only the farmer share. The remaining premium is supported by government subsidy according to scheme rules.

---

# Threshold Yield

Threshold yield is the benchmark yield used for claim calculation in area-based yield insurance.

If actual yield falls below threshold yield, claim may be payable according to formula.

---

# Crop Cutting Experiment

Crop cutting experiment is a yield estimation method used for assessing actual yield in an insurance unit.

---

# Claim

Claim is the compensation payable to eligible farmers after covered crop loss is assessed and approved.

---

# Risks Covered Under PMFBY

PMFBY coverage may include the following risks, subject to scheme rules and notification.

---

# Prevented Sowing or Planting Risk

This applies when farmers cannot sow or plant the insured crop due to adverse seasonal conditions such as deficit rainfall or other notified conditions.

---

# Standing Crop Loss

Standing crop loss coverage applies from sowing to harvesting.

Covered risks may include:

* Drought
* Dry spell
* Flood
* Inundation
* Landslide
* Natural fire due to lightning
* Storm
* Hailstorm
* Cyclone
* Widespread pest attack
* Widespread disease attack

---

# Localized Calamity

Localized calamity applies when specific local events damage individual farms or small affected areas.

Examples may include:

* Hailstorm
* Landslide
* Inundation
* Cloudburst
* Natural fire due to lightning

Farmers usually need to report localized loss quickly through official channels.

---

# Post-Harvest Loss

Post-harvest loss coverage applies when harvested crop kept in the field for drying is damaged by specified events.

Examples:

* Cyclone
* Cyclonic rain
* Unseasonal rain
* Hailstorm

Coverage period and conditions depend on scheme rules.

---

# Mid-Season Adversity

Mid-season adversity coverage may apply when severe crop stress occurs during the season and expected yield is likely to be much lower than normal.

Examples:

* Long dry spell
* Flood
* Severe seasonal crop stress

Assessment is usually area-based.

---

# Risks Not Usually Covered

PMFBY does not cover every type of crop loss.

Common exclusions may include:

* Loss due to negligence
* Intentional damage
* War or nuclear risks
* Theft
* Crop not insured
* Crop different from insured crop
* Damage outside notified area
* Loss after claim reporting deadline
* Poor farming practices not caused by covered risk
* Storage loss beyond covered period
* Damage after harvest outside eligible conditions

Farmers should read policy conditions carefully.

# Claim Process Under PMFBY

PMFBY claims may be processed differently depending on loss type.

---

# Area-Based Claim

For widespread standing crop yield loss, claims are generally calculated using yield assessment at the insurance-unit level.

Farmers may not always need to file individual claims for area-based losses.

---

# Individual Claim Intimation

For localized calamity and post-harvest losses, farmers generally need to report crop loss quickly.

Report through:

* PMFBY portal
* Crop insurance mobile app
* Insurance company helpline
* Agriculture department
* Bank
* Common Service Centre
* Local notified authority

---

# Claim Intimation Information

When reporting loss, provide:

* Application or policy number
* Farmer name
* Mobile number
* Crop name
* Field location
* Survey number
* Date of loss
* Cause of loss
* Affected area
* Photos or videos if possible

---

# Claim Evidence

Farmers should collect:

* Date-stamped photos
* Videos of damaged crop
* Weather event details
* Field location
* Local officer report where possible
* Crop stage details
* Harvest date for post-harvest claims
* Claim acknowledgement number

---

# Claim Settlement

Claim settlement depends on:

* Crop loss assessment

* Bank details

Approved claims are usually credited to the farmer’s registered bank account.

# PMFBY for Tomato Farmers

Tomato may be insured only if notified in the farmer’s area and season.

Tomato farmers should:

* Check whether tomato is notified.
* Confirm premium and sum insured.
* Maintain sowing or transplanting records.
* Take field photos regularly.
* Report hailstorm, flood, or post-harvest damage quickly where covered.
* Maintain sales and harvest records.

Tomato is highly vulnerable to:

* Heavy rain
* Flooding
* Heat stress
* Pest attack
* Disease outbreak
* Price risk

PMFBY may cover production risk, but it usually does not cover market price fall.

---

# PMFBY for Coconut Farmers

Coconut insurance may depend on separate schemes or state notification.

Coconut farmers should:

* Check whether coconut is covered under PMFBY or another relevant scheme.
* Confirm insurance rules for perennial crop coverage.
* Maintain plantation records.
* Keep tree count and age details.
* Monitor cyclone, drought, pest, and storm damage.
* Check whether coconut palm insurance or horticulture insurance is available locally.

---

# PMFBY for Grain Farmers

Grain crops such as rice, wheat, maize, millets, and pulses are commonly covered where notified.

Grain farmers should:

* Check notified crop and insurance unit.
* Keep sowing records.
* Verify insured area.
* Track weather and pest damage.
* Keep harvest and yield records.
* Track claim status after crop cutting experiments.

---

# PMFBY for Horticulture Farmers

Horticultural crops may have higher premium share and crop-specific conditions.

Farmers growing crops such as onion, potato, banana, chilli, tomato, mango, or other horticultural crops should:

* Confirm crop notification.
* Check premium and sum insured.
* Confirm whether post-harvest coverage applies.
* Maintain crop stage records.
* Report localized losses quickly.

# Solutions to Common Problems

| Problem                               | Suggested Action                                     |
| ------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| Crop not visible for enrollment       | Check state notification or contact CSC              |
| Wrong crop entered                    | Contact bank, CSC, or insurer immediately            |
| Premium deducted but policy not found | Check application status and bank records            |
| Claim pending                         | Track status and contact insurer                     |
| Payment failed                        | Check bank account, IFSC, and KYC                    |
| Claim rejected                        | Ask reason and file grievance                        |
| No policy receipt                     | Download from portal or CSC                          |
| Local loss occurred                   | Report quickly and save acknowledgement              |
| Crop changed after loan               | Inform bank within allowed time                      |
| No insurer contact                    | Check official portal or district agriculture office |

---

# Grievance Redressal

If a farmer faces PMFBY issues, the farmer should:

1. Check application or claim status.
2. Contact insurance company helpline.
3. Visit bank or CSC if enrollment-related.
4. Contact agriculture department office.
5. Submit written complaint with documents.
6. Keep complaint number.
7. Follow up with district-level mechanism where available.

Documents for grievance:

* Application number
* Policy number
* Premium receipt
* Bank statement
* Land record
* Crop details
* Claim acknowledgement
* Photos of crop damage
* Mobile number
