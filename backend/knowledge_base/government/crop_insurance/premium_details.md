# Crop Insurance Premium Details

# Overview

Crop insurance premium is the amount paid to obtain crop insurance coverage for a notified crop during a notified season. Under schemes such as Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana, also called PMFBY, farmers pay only a fixed farmer share of premium, while the remaining actuarial premium is subsidized by the government according to scheme rules.

Crop insurance premium details are important because they help farmers understand:

* How much premium must be paid
* What crop area is insured
* What sum insured is applicable
* How farmer premium is calculated
* How government subsidy supports the scheme
* Whether the crop is affordable to insure
* How to compare cost and protection
* How to avoid enrollment mistakes

Premium rates, notified crops, sum insured, insurer details, and enrollment deadlines can change by state, district, season, crop, and year. Farmers should always verify the latest premium details from the official PMFBY portal, bank, Common Service Centre, agriculture department, or notified insurance company.

---

# What is Crop Insurance Premium?

Crop insurance premium is the cost of buying insurance protection for a crop.

In simple words:

* Farmer pays premium.
* Crop gets insured for a specified crop, area, and season.
* If covered crop loss occurs, eligible claim compensation may be paid according to scheme rules.

The premium is calculated based on:

* Crop
* Season
* Area insured
* Sum insured
* Premium rate
* State notification
* Insurance company premium rate
* Government subsidy rules

# Farmer Premium Share Under PMFBY

Under PMFBY, the farmer premium share is generally capped at fixed rates.

| Crop Season or Crop Type      | Farmer Premium Share |
| ----------------------------- | -------------------- |
| Kharif food and oilseed crops | 2% of sum insured    |
| Rabi food and oilseed crops   | 1.5% of sum insured  |
| Annual commercial crops       | 5% of sum insured    |
| Annual horticultural crops    | 5% of sum insured    |

These are general PMFBY farmer premium rates. Exact payable premium should be checked from the current season notification or official premium calculator.

---

# Government Premium Subsidy

The actual insurance premium charged by the insurance company may be higher than the farmer premium share.

The difference between:

* Actuarial or bidded premium
* Farmer premium share

is paid as government subsidy according to scheme rules.

## Example

If the actuarial premium is 12% of the sum insured and the farmer share is 2%, the remaining 10% is paid as subsidy by government according to applicable sharing rules.

---

# Centre and State Premium Sharing

Government subsidy is generally shared between the Central Government and State Government.

Common sharing pattern:

| Region               | Subsidy Sharing Pattern                        |
| -------------------- | ---------------------------------------------- |
| Most states          | Centre and State share subsidy generally 50:50 |
| North Eastern states | Centre and State share subsidy generally 90:10 |

Farmers should check the latest applicable sharing rules because government policy may change.

---

# Premium Formula

The basic formula for farmer premium is:

Farmer Premium = Sum Insured × Farmer Premium Rate × Insured Area

If sum insured is already given for the full insured area, then:

Farmer Premium = Total Sum Insured × Farmer Premium Rate

---

# Premium Calculation Example for Kharif Crop

## Example

A farmer insures a Kharif crop with:

* Sum insured per hectare: ₹40,000
* Area insured: 2 hectares
* Farmer premium rate: 2%

## Calculation

Total Sum Insured = ₹40,000 × 2 = ₹80,000

Farmer Premium = ₹80,000 × 2 / 100 = ₹1,600

So, the farmer pays ₹1,600 as premium.

---

# Premium Calculation Example for Rabi Crop

## Example

A farmer insures a Rabi crop with:

* Sum insured per hectare: ₹50,000
* Area insured: 1 hectare
* Farmer premium rate: 1.5%

## Calculation

Farmer Premium = ₹50,000 × 1.5 / 100 = ₹750

So, the farmer pays ₹750 as premium.

---

# Premium Calculation Example for Horticultural Crop

## Example

A farmer insures a horticultural crop with:

* Sum insured per hectare: ₹1,20,000
* Area insured: 0.5 hectare
* Farmer premium rate: 5%

## Calculation

Total Sum Insured = ₹1,20,000 × 0.5 = ₹60,000

Farmer Premium = ₹60,000 × 5 / 100 = ₹3,000

So, the farmer pays ₹3,000 as premium.

# Important Premium Terms

---

# Sum Insured

Sum insured is the maximum insurance coverage amount for the crop and area.

It depends on:

* Crop
* District or area notification
* Scale of finance
* Season
* State rules
* Crop type
* Insured area

Higher sum insured usually means higher premium but also higher potential coverage.

---

# Farmer Share

Farmer share is the premium amount paid by the farmer.

Under PMFBY, this is usually capped at the applicable crop category rate.

---

# Actuarial Premium

Actuarial premium is the actual risk-based premium calculated by the insurance company.

It depends on crop risk, weather risk, historical losses, district, season, and bidding process.

The farmer usually pays only the capped farmer share, while the remaining premium is subsidized.

---

# Premium Subsidy

Premium subsidy is the amount paid by government to make insurance affordable for farmers.

---

# Gross Premium

Gross premium means the total premium payable to the insurance company.

Gross Premium = Farmer Premium + Government Subsidy

---

# Premium Rate

Premium rate is the percentage applied to the sum insured to calculate premium.

---

# Insured Area

Insured area is the land area covered under insurance.

It may be recorded in:

* Hectare
* Acre
* Local land units

Farmers should ensure that the insured area is correct.

---

# Notified Crop

Only crops notified for insurance in a particular area and season can be insured.

---

# Notified Area

The village, gram panchayat, block, taluka, district, or other insurance unit notified for insurance coverage.

---

# Factors Affecting Premium Amount

---

# Crop Type

Different crop categories have different farmer premium rates.

Examples:

* Kharif food crops
* Rabi food crops
* Oilseeds
* Commercial crops
* Horticultural crops

---

# Season

Premium rate differs between Kharif and Rabi crops.

Kharif crops usually have 2% farmer premium share.

Rabi food and oilseed crops usually have 1.5% farmer premium share.

---

# Sum Insured

Higher sum insured means higher premium amount.

---

# Area Insured

Premium increases as insured area increases.

Example:

Premium for 2 hectares is usually double the premium for 1 hectare, if crop and sum insured are the same.

---

# State and District Notification

Premium details depend on state and district crop notification.

The same crop may have different sum insured in different districts.

---

# Insurance Company Bid

The actuarial premium may vary depending on the insurance company selected for the cluster or district.

---

# Government Subsidy

Farmer premium may remain capped, but total premium subsidy depends on actuarial premium.

---

# Irrigated or Rainfed Condition

Some crop notifications may distinguish irrigated and rainfed crops.

Sum insured and risk may differ.

---

# Crop Duration and Risk

High-risk crops or areas may have higher actuarial premiums.

# Premium and Claim Relationship

Paying premium does not guarantee claim payment for every crop loss.

Claim payment depends on:

* Covered risk
* Correct enrollment
* Policy status
* Crop loss assessment
* Area yield data
* Weather trigger data
* Localized loss reporting
* Post-harvest claim reporting
* Scheme formula
* Claim approval

Premium provides eligibility for coverage, but claims are paid only when scheme conditions are met.

---

# Premium and Sum Insured Relationship

Premium is calculated as a percentage of sum insured.

## Higher Sum Insured

* Higher premium
* Higher potential coverage

## Lower Sum Insured

* Lower premium
* Lower potential coverage

Farmers should check whether sum insured is sufficient for the crop investment.

---

# Premium and Crop Area Accuracy

Crop area accuracy is very important.

If insured area is lower than actual area:

* Only the insured area may be covered.

If insured area is higher than actual area:

* Claim may be restricted or questioned during verification.

Farmers should enter correct land area.

---

# Premium and Crop Accuracy

The insured crop must match the crop actually grown.

Example:

If soybean is insured but cotton is grown, claim may be rejected.

Farmers should verify crop details immediately after enrollment.

---

# Premium and Enrollment Deadline

Premium must be paid before the enrollment cut-off date.

Late payment or failed transaction may result in no coverage.

Farmers should not wait until the last day because portal, bank, or document issues may delay enrollment.

---

# Premium Refund

Premium refund rules depend on scheme conditions.

Refund may be considered in cases such as:

* Duplicate payment
* Enrollment rejection
* Crop not notified
* Transaction failure
* Technical error

Farmers should contact the bank, CSC, portal support, or insurance company for refund-related issues.

---

# Low Premium Does Not Mean Low Risk

PMFBY farmer premium is subsidized, so the farmer pays a small share.

However, crop risk may still be high.

Farmers should combine insurance with:

* Good agronomic practices
* Crop diversification
* Water management
* Pest monitoring
* Weather advisories
* Safe storage
* Risk planning

Insurance is financial protection, not crop protection.

---

# Common Premium-Related Mistakes

Farmers should avoid:

* Paying premium for wrong crop
* Entering wrong area
* Missing enrollment deadline
* Not saving receipt
