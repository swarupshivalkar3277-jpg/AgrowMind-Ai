# Kisan Credit Card

# Overview

Kisan Credit Card, commonly called KCC, is a credit facility designed to provide timely and affordable institutional credit to farmers for crop production, allied agricultural activities, and working-capital needs. It helps farmers meet seasonal expenses without depending on high-interest informal loans.

KCC can be used for:

* Crop cultivation expenses
* Purchase of seeds, fertilizers, pesticides, and manure
* Irrigation expenses
* Farm labor expenses
* Harvesting and marketing expenses
* Post-harvest expenses
* Repair and maintenance of farm assets
* Dairy, animal husbandry, and fisheries working capital where eligible
* Household consumption needs within permitted limits
* Other agriculture-related needs approved by the bank

KCC is useful because farming requires money before harvest income is received. It provides flexible credit so farmers can withdraw and repay according to crop and income cycles.

---

# What is Kisan Credit Card?

Kisan Credit Card is a bank credit product for farmers. It provides a sanctioned credit limit that farmers can use for crop and farm-related expenses.

Instead of applying for a new loan every season, a farmer can use the KCC limit repeatedly within the validity period and bank rules.

KCC works like a revolving cash-credit facility.

The farmer can:

* Withdraw money when needed
* Use it for eligible agricultural expenses
* Repay after harvest or as per schedule
* Reuse the available limit
* Renew or enhance the limit based on bank assessment

---

# Main Purpose of KCC

The main purpose of KCC is to provide easy and timely credit to farmers.

It helps farmers:

1. Buy inputs on time.
2. Avoid high-interest moneylenders.
3. Manage crop production expenses.
4. Meet working-capital needs.
5. Reduce financial stress during crop season.
6. Improve farm productivity.
7. Access interest subvention benefits where eligible.
8. Build a formal credit history with banks.

# Why KCC is Important for Farmers

Farmers often need money at several stages:

* Before sowing
* During land preparation
* During fertilizer application
* During irrigation
* During pest and disease management
* During harvesting
* During transport and marketing

If money is not available on time, crop operations may be delayed and yield may reduce.

KCC helps farmers complete farm activities at the correct time.

# KCC Credit Limit

The KCC credit limit is decided by the bank.

It may depend on:

* Landholding area
* Crop grown
* Scale of finance
* Cropping pattern
* Irrigation status
* Cost of cultivation
* Post-harvest expenses
* Farm household consumption needs
* Maintenance expenses
* Allied activity requirement
* Existing loan liability
* Repayment capacity
* Bank assessment

The limit may be reviewed and enhanced periodically if the farmer repays on time and farm needs increase.

---

# Scale of Finance

Scale of finance is the estimated cost of cultivation per acre or hectare for a crop. It is usually decided by district-level banking or technical committees.

It may include:

* Seed cost
* Fertilizer cost
* Pesticide cost
* Labor cost
* Irrigation cost
* Machinery cost
* Other crop expenses

KCC crop limit is often calculated using scale of finance and cultivated area.

---

# KCC Limit Calculation

A simple crop KCC limit may be calculated using:

Crop Loan Requirement = Scale of Finance × Crop Area

Additional components may be added for:

* Post-harvest expenses
* Farm maintenance
* Consumption needs
* Insurance
* Cost escalation
* Allied activity working capital

Final limit is approved by the bank.

---

# Example of KCC Limit Calculation

## Example

A farmer grows tomato on 1 acre.

If the scale of finance is ₹80,000 per acre:

Crop Loan Requirement = ₹80,000 × 1 = ₹80,000

The bank may add permitted additional amounts for post-harvest expenses, maintenance, or household consumption according to rules.

Final sanctioned limit may differ depending on bank assessment.

# Interest Subvention and Prompt Repayment Incentive

Under the Modified Interest Subvention Scheme, eligible short-term agricultural loans through KCC may receive interest support.

Current official releases mention:

* Short-term agriculture loans through KCC up to ₹3 lakh may be available at 7% interest.
* Farmers who repay on time may receive up to 3% prompt repayment incentive.
* This can reduce the effective interest rate to 4%.
* For loans taken exclusively for animal husbandry or fisheries, the interest benefit is applicable up to ₹2 lakh.

Farmers should verify the latest rules with the bank before relying on these benefits.

# Repayment of KCC Loan

Repayment depends on crop duration and bank terms.

For short-duration crops:

* Repayment may be expected after harvest and sale.

For long-duration crops:

* Repayment schedule may be longer.

For allied activities:

* Repayment may be linked to milk sale, fish harvest, livestock income, or business cash flow.

Farmers should confirm due dates at the time of sanction.

---

# Prompt Repayment

Prompt repayment means paying the loan by the due date.

Benefits may include:

* Eligibility for prompt repayment incentive
* Lower effective interest rate where applicable
* Better credit score
* Easier renewal
* Possible higher limit in future
* Better bank relationship

---

# Late Repayment

Late repayment may cause:

* Higher interest
* Loss of prompt repayment incentive
* Penalty charges
* Poor credit history
* Difficulty in renewal
* Difficulty getting future loans
* Possible recovery action

Farmers should contact the bank before due date if repayment difficulty is expected.

# KCC and Crop Insurance

KCC crop loans may be linked with crop insurance where applicable.

Farmers should verify:

* Whether crop insurance premium was deducted
* Which crop is insured
* What area is insured
* Policy or application number
* Insurance company name
* Claim reporting procedure
* Bank account details

Insurance is important for reducing repayment stress after crop loss.

---

# KCC and PM-KISAN

PM-KISAN beneficiaries may be encouraged to apply for KCC through special drives or simplified forms.

However:

* PM-KISAN benefit and KCC loan are different.
* PM-KISAN is income support.
* KCC is a loan that must be repaid.
* Being a PM-KISAN beneficiary does not automatically mean loan approval.
* Bank assessment and documents are still required.

Farmers should use KCC for productive farm needs.

Good uses include:

* Quality seed purchase
* Soil-test-based fertilizer use
* Timely pest control
* Efficient irrigation
* Harvesting and transport
* Fodder and feed
* Farm asset repair
* Working capital for allied activities

# Poor Use of KCC Funds

Farmers should avoid using KCC mainly for:

* Non-farm luxury expenses
* Social functions beyond repayment capacity
* Repaying old high-interest loans without a plan
* Speculative investment
* Unplanned machinery purchase
* High-risk crop expansion without water or market
* Repeated withdrawals without repayment plan

Misuse of KCC funds can create debt problems.

# KCC Checklist for Farmers

## Before Applying

* Decide credit need.
* Prepare crop or activity budget.
* Collect land or cultivation documents.
* Prepare KYC documents.
* Check existing loans.
* Ask bank about interest and due dates.
* Ask about crop insurance.

## During Application

* Fill correct crop and area details.
* Submit all documents.
* Provide mobile number.
* Ask for acknowledgement.
* Ask expected processing time.
* Check whether collateral is required.

## After Sanction

* Read sanction terms.
* Note credit limit.
* Note interest rate.
* Note repayment date.
* Save card or account details.
* Use funds for farm needs.
* Keep withdrawal records.

## During Crop Season

* Use funds carefully.
* Avoid unnecessary spending.
* Keep crop records.
* Monitor weather and pests.
* Track outstanding amount.
* Repay after harvest.

## Before Renewal

* Clear dues where possible.
* Submit updated documents.
* Update crop plan.
* Ask for limit enhancement if needed.
* Verify insurance and interest benefit.

# Common Mistakes to Avoid

Farmers should avoid:

* Borrowing more than repayment capacity
* Using KCC for non-farm expenses
* Missing repayment due date
* Not checking interest benefit rules
* Not maintaining transaction records
* Ignoring crop insurance details
* Not renewing KCC on time
* Assuming PM-KISAN and KCC are the same
* Not informing bank about crop change
* Waiting until default before contacting bank

---

# Farmer Recommendations

Farmers are advised to:

1. Use KCC mainly for productive agriculture and allied activities.
2. Borrow according to crop budget and repayment capacity.
3. Verify interest rate and due date with the bank.
4. Repay on time to receive benefits where eligible.
5. Maintain withdrawal and repayment records.
6. Check crop insurance linkage.
7. Inform bank if crop or area changes.
8. Renew KCC before expiry.
9. Avoid high-risk crop expansion using borrowed money without planning.
10. Contact bank early if crop loss or repayment difficulty occurs.
