# PM-KISAN Benefits

# Overview

Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi, commonly called PM-KISAN, is a Government of India income support scheme for eligible landholding farmer families. The scheme provides direct financial assistance to help farmers meet agriculture-related and household-linked needs.

Under PM-KISAN, eligible farmer families receive financial support of ₹6,000 per year in three equal instalments of ₹2,000 each. The amount is transferred directly into the beneficiary farmer’s bank account through Direct Benefit Transfer.

PM-KISAN helps farmers:

* Meet small farm expenses
* Purchase seeds, fertilizers, pesticides, and other inputs
* Support seasonal crop operations
* Reduce dependence on informal borrowing
* Improve cash availability before crop activities
* Support household and farm-linked needs
* Strengthen farmer income stability
* Access direct government support through DBT

PM-KISAN is not a loan. Farmers do not need to repay the benefit amount.

---

# What is PM-KISAN?

PM-KISAN is an income support scheme for eligible farmer families with cultivable landholding.

The scheme provides direct financial assistance to eligible farmers to support their farming and related needs.

The benefit is:

* ₹6,000 per year
* Paid in three instalments
* ₹2,000 per instalment
* Transferred directly to bank account
* Funded by the Government of India
* Linked with farmer records, Aadhaar, and bank account details

---

The main benefit of PM-KISAN is direct income support.

| Benefit Item          | Details                                             |
| --------------------- | --------------------------------------------------- |
| Annual Benefit        | ₹6,000 per eligible farmer family                   |
| Number of Instalments | 3 instalments per year                              |
| Amount Per Instalment | ₹2,000                                              |
| Payment Mode          | Direct Benefit Transfer                             |

| Repayment             | No repayment required                               |

# Why PM-KISAN is Important

Farming requires regular cash for crop activities. Farmers often need money before harvest income comes.

PM-KISAN helps farmers manage small but important expenses such as:

* Seed purchase
* Fertilizer purchase
* Pesticide purchase
* Organic manure
* Irrigation support
* Farm labor
* Small farm tools
* Transport of inputs
* Crop protection needs

The scheme gives timely support and reduces pressure on farmers during crop season.

---

# Direct Benefit Transfer

PM-KISAN uses Direct Benefit Transfer, also called DBT.

DBT means the government transfers money directly into the eligible farmer’s bank account.

## Benefits of DBT

* Reduces middlemen
* Improves transparency
* Speeds up payment
* Reduces leakage
* Allows status tracking
* Ensures payment reaches registered beneficiary account

Farmers should ensure their bank account details are correct and active.

---

# Instalment Structure

PM-KISAN benefit is paid in three instalments during the year.

Each instalment is ₹2,000.

## Instalment Pattern

| Instalment           | Amount |
| -------------------- | ------ |
| First Instalment     | ₹2,000 |
| Second Instalment    | ₹2,000 |
| Third Instalment     | ₹2,000 |
| Total Annual Benefit | ₹6,000 |

The exact release date of each instalment may vary depending on government schedule, verification, and beneficiary eligibility status.

---

# PM-KISAN is Not a Loan

PM-KISAN is income support, not a loan.

Farmers:

* Do not pay interest
* Do not repay the amount
* Do not need collateral
* Do not need to use it for only one fixed input
* Receive it directly if eligible and verified

However, farmers should use the benefit wisely for productive farm and household needs.

# Who Benefits from PM-KISAN?

PM-KISAN benefits eligible farmer families with cultivable landholding, subject to scheme rules.

A farmer family generally means:

* Husband
* Wife
* Minor children

Eligibility is based on land records and scheme guidelines.

State governments and Union Territory administrations identify eligible farmer families according to scheme rules.

---

# Eligible Farmer Family

An eligible farmer family should generally have:

* Cultivable landholding
* Correct land records
* Valid identity details
* Bank account details
* Aadhaar details as required
* Completed e-KYC where required
* No exclusion under scheme rules

Farmers should check current eligibility rules from the official PM-KISAN portal or local agriculture office.

# PM-KISAN and Kisan Credit Card

PM-KISAN and Kisan Credit Card are different.

| Scheme            | Meaning                      |
| ----------------- | ---------------------------- |
| PM-KISAN          | Direct income support        |
| Kisan Credit Card | Bank credit or loan facility |

PM-KISAN amount does not need repayment.

KCC loan must be repaid with interest according to bank rules.

PM-KISAN beneficiaries may be encouraged to apply for KCC, but KCC approval depends on bank rules and documents.

---

# PM-KISAN and Crop Insurance

PM-KISAN is not crop insurance.

| Support Type   | Purpose                              |
| -------------- | ------------------------------------ |
| PM-KISAN       | Regular income support               |
| Crop Insurance | Compensation after insured crop loss |

Farmers should use both wisely.

PM-KISAN can help with small farm expenses, while crop insurance can help after crop damage where coverage exists.

To receive PM-KISAN benefit smoothly, farmers should ensure:

* Correct name in records
* Correct land details
* Aadhaar details updated
* Bank account active
* Bank account linked as required
* e-KYC completed where required

* No exclusion category issue

---

# e-KYC Requirement

e-KYC is important for PM-KISAN payment verification.

Farmers may complete e-KYC through:

* OTP-based e-KYC on official portal where available
* Common Service Centre
* Biometric authentication
* Face authentication where available through official app or channels

If e-KYC is incomplete, instalments may be delayed or stopped.

---

# Aadhaar and Bank Account Linking

PM-KISAN payments are generally linked with Aadhaar and bank account verification.

Farmers should check:

* Aadhaar number is correct
* Name matches records
* Bank account is active
* Bank account is seeded or linked as required
* IFSC is correct
* Account is not closed or frozen
* KYC is updated in bank

Payment failure often happens due to bank or Aadhaar mismatch.

# Fraud and Safety Advisory

Farmers should be careful about fraud.

Do not share:

* OTP
* ATM PIN
* UPI PIN
* Internet banking password
* Aadhaar OTP
* Bank password

Use only official PM-KISAN portal, CSC, bank, agriculture office, or authorized government channels.

PM-KISAN benefit does not require payment to private agents for instalment release.
