# PM-KISAN Eligibility

# Overview

Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi, commonly called PM-KISAN, is a Government of India income support scheme for eligible landholding farmer families. The scheme provides direct financial assistance to farmers who meet the eligibility conditions and are not covered under exclusion categories.

PM-KISAN eligibility is mainly based on:

* Farmer family status
* Cultivable landholding
* Land records
* Aadhaar details
* Bank account details
* e-KYC status
* State or Union Territory verification
* Exclusion criteria

Eligible farmer families receive ₹6,000 per year in three equal instalments of ₹2,000 each through Direct Benefit Transfer.

Farmers should check the latest eligibility rules, beneficiary status, e-KYC status, and land verification status from the official PM-KISAN portal, Common Service Centre, agriculture department, or local revenue office.

---

# What is PM-KISAN Eligibility?

PM-KISAN eligibility means the conditions a farmer family must satisfy to receive benefits under the scheme.

A farmer family is generally eligible if:

* The family owns cultivable land.
* The land is recorded in official land records.
* The family is not covered under exclusion categories.
* Aadhaar and bank details are correct.
* Required verification and e-KYC are completed.
* State or Union Territory records approve the beneficiary.

PM-KISAN benefit is given to eligible farmer families, not separately to every individual family member.

---

# Main Eligibility Rule

The main eligibility rule is:

All landholding farmer families with cultivable landholding in their names are eligible for PM-KISAN benefit, subject to exclusion criteria.

This means the farmer family should have cultivable agricultural land recorded in official land records.

---

# Meaning of Farmer Family

For PM-KISAN, a farmer family generally means:

* Husband
* Wife
* Minor children

Only one benefit is allowed for one eligible farmer family.

If more than one family member is receiving benefit from the same family unit, the case may be treated as duplicate or suspicious and may require verification.

---

# Meaning of Landholding Farmer

A landholding farmer is a farmer whose name appears in land records as owner of cultivable agricultural land.

Important points:

* Land should be cultivable.
* Land should be recorded in official state or Union Territory records.
* Farmer details should match government records.
* Land ownership details should be verified by the state or local authority.

---

# Meaning of Cultivable Land

Cultivable land means agricultural land that can be used for crop cultivation or farming.

Examples may include:

* Crop land
* Irrigated agricultural land
* Rainfed agricultural land
* Orchard land
* Plantation land where recorded as agricultural land
* Land used for farming activities

Land classification depends on official land records.

---

# Who is Eligible for PM-KISAN?

The following may be eligible if they satisfy scheme rules:

* Landholding farmer families
* Small farmers
* Marginal farmers
* Medium farmers
* Large farmers, if not excluded
* Farmers with cultivable land in their name
* Women farmers with land in their name
* Joint landholders, subject to family and land-record verification
* Farmer families identified and approved by State or Union Territory authorities

The scheme was initially focused on small and marginal farmers but was later extended to all landholding farmer families, subject to exclusions.

---

# Eligibility for Small and Marginal Farmers

Small and marginal farmers are eligible if they own cultivable land and meet scheme requirements.

They should ensure:

* Land records are updated.
* Aadhaar details are correct.
* Bank account is active.
* e-KYC is completed.
* Beneficiary status is active.
* No exclusion category applies.

---

# Eligibility for Women Farmers

Women farmers can be eligible if cultivable land is recorded in their name and they meet scheme rules.

Women farmers should check:

* Land ownership record
* Aadhaar details
* Bank account details
* e-KYC status
* Mobile number
* Beneficiary status

If land is transferred to a woman farmer, updated land records may be required before approval.

---

# Eligibility for Joint Landholders

Joint landholders may be eligible depending on family structure, land records, and scheme verification.

Important points:

* PM-KISAN benefit is family-based.
* Husband, wife, and minor children together are treated as one family unit.
* If joint landholders belong to separate eligible families, verification may be required.
* Duplicate benefit within the same family is not allowed.

Farmers should confirm with the local agriculture or revenue office if land is jointly held.

---

# Eligibility for Inherited Land

Farmers who receive land through inheritance may be eligible if:

* The land mutation is completed.
* The farmer’s name appears in land records.
* The land is cultivable.
* The farmer family is not excluded.
* Required documents are verified.
* e-KYC and bank details are completed.

Until land records are updated, approval may be delayed.

---

# Eligibility for Newly Purchased Land

Eligibility for farmers who recently acquired land may require verification.

Official PM-KISAN portal notices have indicated that suspected cases such as farmers who acquired land ownership after a specified cut-off date may be temporarily withheld until physical verification is completed.

Farmers who purchased or received land recently should contact the local agriculture or revenue office for current rules and verification procedure.

---

# Eligibility for Tenant Farmers

PM-KISAN eligibility is generally linked with landholding recorded in official land records.

Tenant farmers, sharecroppers, and oral lessees may face difficulty if land is not recorded in their name.

They should check with:

* Local agriculture office
* Revenue office
* State farmer registration system
* Common Service Centre
* Gram panchayat or local authority

Eligibility for tenant farmers depends on scheme rules and state verification systems.

---

# Eligibility for Sharecroppers

Sharecroppers may not automatically qualify if land ownership is not recorded in their name.

They should verify locally whether any state-level provision, cultivation certificate, or farmer registration can support their case.

PM-KISAN is not the same as crop loan eligibility or crop insurance eligibility.

---

# Eligibility for Forest Land or Unclear Land Records

If land records are unclear, disputed, forest-category, encroached, or not officially recorded as cultivable land in the farmer’s name, PM-KISAN approval may be difficult.

Farmers should correct land records through the revenue department before applying.

---

# Eligibility for Farmers Living Outside Village

A farmer may live outside the village but own cultivable land in official records.

Eligibility depends on:

* Land ownership
* Family status
* Aadhaar
* Bank account
* e-KYC
* Exclusion criteria
* State verification

Residence alone may not decide eligibility, but local verification may be required.

---

# Exclusion Categories

Certain higher-income or institutional categories are not eligible for PM-KISAN.

Common exclusion categories include:

* Institutional landholders
* Present or former holders of constitutional posts
* Present or former Ministers
* Present or former Members of Parliament
* Present or former Members of State Legislative Assemblies
* Present or former Members of State Legislative Councils
* Former or present Mayors of Municipal Corporations
* Former or present Chairpersons of District Panchayats
* Serving or retired officers and employees of Central Government
* Serving or retired officers and employees of State Government
* Serving or retired officers and employees of Public Sector Undertakings
* Serving or retired officers and employees of government autonomous bodies
* Regular employees of local bodies
* Income tax payers in the last assessment year
* Professionals such as doctors, engineers, lawyers, chartered accountants, and architects registered with professional bodies and carrying out profession
* Pensioners receiving pension above the prescribed limit, except certain lower-category employees as per rules

Farmers should verify the latest exclusion list from official PM-KISAN guidelines.

---

# Institutional Landholders

Institutional landholders are not eligible.

Examples may include land held by:

* Companies
* Trusts
* Societies
* Institutions
* Religious institutions
* Educational institutions
* Government bodies
* Corporate farms

PM-KISAN is intended for eligible farmer families, not institutional land ownership.

---

# Government Employees and Pensioners

Certain government employees and pensioners are excluded.

The exclusion generally applies to serving or retired officers and employees of:

* Central Government
* State Government
* Ministries
* Departments
* Public Sector Undertakings
* Government autonomous institutions
* Regular local body employees

However, serving or retired Multi Tasking Staff, Class IV, or Group D employees may be treated differently as per scheme rules if otherwise eligible.

Farmers should confirm category-specific eligibility from official guidelines.

---

# Income Tax Payers

If a farmer or farmer family member paid income tax in the last assessment year, the family may be excluded from PM-KISAN benefit.

Farmers should not apply or continue receiving benefit if they fall under the income-tax exclusion category.

Wrongful benefit may lead to recovery.

---

# Professionals

Certain professionals are excluded if they are registered with professional bodies and carrying out profession.

Examples include:

* Doctors
* Engineers
* Lawyers
* Chartered accountants
* Architects

If such a professional also owns agricultural land, the family may still be excluded according to scheme rules.

---

# Constitutional and Political Position Holders

Families with members who are present or former holders of certain constitutional or political positions may be excluded.

Examples include:

* Constitutional post holders
* Ministers
* Members of Parliament
* Members of Legislative Assembly
* Members of Legislative Council
* Mayors of Municipal Corporations
* Chairpersons of District Panchayats

Farmers should check current official rules for exact categories.

---

# Pension-Related Exclusion

Certain pensioners receiving monthly pension above the prescribed limit may be excluded.

The exclusion may not apply to certain lower-category employees such as Multi Tasking Staff, Class IV, or Group D employees, depending on scheme rules.

Farmers should confirm pension-related eligibility from official sources.

---

# Aadhaar Requirement

Aadhaar is important for PM-KISAN verification and payment.

Farmers should ensure:

* Aadhaar number is correct.
* Name matches land and bank records.
* Date of birth details are not causing mismatch.
* Aadhaar is linked or seeded where required.
* Aadhaar authentication is successful.
* Aadhaar is not duplicated in multiple records.

Aadhaar mismatch can delay instalments.

---

# Bank Account Requirement

PM-KISAN benefit is transferred directly to the registered bank account.

Farmers should ensure:

* Bank account is active.
* Account number is correct.
* IFSC is correct.
* Account holder name matches beneficiary details.
* KYC is updated.
* Aadhaar-bank seeding is completed where required.
* Account is not closed, frozen, or dormant.

Bank errors are common reasons for payment failure.

---

# e-KYC Requirement

e-KYC is required for PM-KISAN beneficiary verification.

Farmers may complete e-KYC through:

* OTP-based e-KYC on official PM-KISAN portal where available
* Common Service Centre
* Biometric authentication
* Face authentication through official channels where available

If e-KYC is pending, payment may be stopped or delayed.

---

# Land Seeding and Record Verification

Land seeding means linking the farmer’s land record with the PM-KISAN beneficiary record.

Farmers should check whether:

* Land record is seeded.
* Survey number or khata details are correct.
* Name in land record matches PM-KISAN record.
* Mutation is updated.
* Land ownership is verified.
* State approval is completed.

Land seeding pending status can delay benefit.

---

# Documents Required for Eligibility Verification

Documents may vary by state and correction type.

Common documents include:

* Aadhaar card
* Bank passbook
* Land record
* 7/12 extract where applicable
* Khata or khasra document
* Mutation record
* Farmer registration number
* Mobile number
* Identity proof
* e-KYC confirmation
* Self-declaration where required
* Family details
* Death certificate or succession document in inheritance cases

Farmers should confirm required documents locally.

---

# PM-KISAN Registration Eligibility Check

Before applying, farmer should check:

1. Is cultivable land in my name?
2. Is land record updated?
3. Is my family already receiving PM-KISAN?
4. Am I in any exclusion category?
5. Is my Aadhaar correct?
6. Is my bank account active?
7. Can I complete e-KYC?
8. Are land details available for verification?
9. Is my mobile number active?
10. Are state records matching my details?

---

# How Eligibility is Verified

Eligibility is usually verified through:

* Land records
* State or Union Territory database
* Aadhaar authentication
* Bank account validation
* e-KYC
* Self-declaration
* Physical verification where required
* Exclusion category checks
* Duplicate beneficiary checks
* Income tax database checks where applicable

State and local authorities play a major role in beneficiary identification and verification.

# Beneficiary Status and Eligibility

Farmers should check beneficiary status to know whether they are approved, pending, rejected, or under verification.

Status may show:

* Eligible
* Under verification
* e-KYC pending
* Land seeding pending
* Aadhaar authentication failed
* Bank account error
* Payment processed

* Rejected

* Ineligible due to exclusion category

Farmers should correct errors early.

* Government employee exclusion
* Professional exclusion
* Pensioner exclusion
* Aadhaar mismatch
* Bank account error
* e-KYC pending
* Land record mismatch
* Land seeding pending
* Incorrect family details
* Farmer under exclusion category
* Physical verification pending
* Wrong registration details

---

# Eligibility for Deceased Beneficiary Family

If a registered beneficiary dies, family members should contact the local agriculture or revenue office.

Possible steps may include:

* Updating death record
* Completing land mutation
* Registering eligible successor
* Updating Aadhaar and bank details
* Submitting inheritance documents
* Completing e-KYC

Benefit may not automatically transfer without proper verification.

---

# Surrendering PM-KISAN Benefit

If a farmer is not eligible but has received benefit, the farmer should surrender or return the benefit according to official process.

Situations may include:

* Income tax payer
* Government employee exclusion
* Duplicate family benefit
* Institutional landholding
* Wrong registration
* Professional exclusion
* Land not eligible
* Other exclusion category

Receiving benefit while ineligible may lead to recovery.

---

# Difference Between Eligibility and Payment Status

Eligibility means the farmer meets scheme rules.

Payment status means whether instalment has been credited.

A farmer may be eligible but payment can fail due to:

* Bank account issue
* Aadhaar issue
* e-KYC pending
* Land seeding pending
* Technical issue
* State approval pending

Both eligibility and payment status should be checked.

---

# PM-KISAN Eligibility and KCC

PM-KISAN and Kisan Credit Card are different.

| PM-KISAN                    | Kisan Credit Card               |
| --------------------------- | ------------------------------- |
| Income support scheme       | Loan facility                   |
| No repayment required       | Loan must be repaid             |
| Based on scheme eligibility | Based on bank credit assessment |
| ₹6,000 yearly benefit       | Credit limit depends on bank    |
| Direct benefit transfer     | Bank loan account               |

Being eligible for PM-KISAN does not automatically guarantee KCC loan approval, but PM-KISAN beneficiaries may be encouraged to apply for KCC.

---

# PM-KISAN Eligibility and Crop Insurance

PM-KISAN and crop insurance are also different.

| PM-KISAN                         | Crop Insurance                 |
| -------------------------------- | ------------------------------ |
| Income support                   | Crop loss compensation         |
| Regular instalments              | Claim after insured loss       |
| No premium                       | Premium required               |
| Based on landholding eligibility | Based on insured crop and area |
| Not linked to market price       | Covers specified crop risks    |

Farmers should use both schemes properly where eligible.

---

# Safety and Fraud Advisory

Farmers should avoid fraud while checking eligibility.

Do not share:

* OTP
* UPI PIN
* ATM PIN
* Aadhaar OTP
* Bank password
* Internet banking password

Use only:

* Official PM-KISAN portal
* Common Service Centre
* Agriculture department
* Bank branch
* Authorized government channels

Private agents cannot guarantee approval or early instalment release.

---

# Farmer Recommendations

Farmers are advised to:

1. Check whether cultivable land is recorded in their name.
2. Confirm that the family is not under exclusion category.
3. Complete e-KYC.
4. Ensure Aadhaar details are correct.
5. Keep bank account active and updated.
6. Check land seeding status.
7. Avoid duplicate registration within the same family.
8. Correct name, bank, and land record errors early.
9. Use only official channels for registration and correction.
10. Surrender benefit if found ineligible.
