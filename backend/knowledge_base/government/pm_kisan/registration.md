# PM-KISAN Registration

# Overview

Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi, commonly called PM-KISAN, is a Government of India income support scheme for eligible landholding farmer families. PM-KISAN registration is the process through which an eligible farmer applies to receive financial assistance under the scheme.

Under PM-KISAN, eligible farmer families receive ₹6,000 per year in three equal instalments of ₹2,000 each through Direct Benefit Transfer into the registered bank account.

PM-KISAN registration helps eligible farmers:

* Receive direct income support
* Link land records with farmer details
* Register Aadhaar and bank account information
* Complete farmer verification
* Track beneficiary status
* Receive instalments through DBT
* Correct records where needed
* Access other farmer services connected with official records

Farmers should register only through the official PM-KISAN portal, Common Service Centre, agriculture department, or authorized government channels.

---

# What is PM-KISAN Registration?

PM-KISAN registration is the official process of adding an eligible farmer family into the PM-KISAN beneficiary database.

During registration, farmer details such as Aadhaar number, land record, bank account, mobile number, and state-level farmer information are verified.

Registration does not automatically guarantee payment. The application must be verified and approved according to scheme rules.

---

# Who Should Register?

Farmers should register if:

* They are eligible landholding farmer families.
* Cultivable agricultural land is recorded in their name.
* They are not already receiving PM-KISAN benefit.
* They are not covered under exclusion categories.
* Aadhaar details are correct.
* Bank account is active.
* e-KYC can be completed.
* Land records can be verified.

Farmers should avoid duplicate registration within the same family.

---

# Who Should Not Register?

Farmers should not register if they are not eligible under PM-KISAN rules.

Common ineligible categories may include:

* Institutional landholders
* Income tax payers in the last assessment year
* Certain serving or retired government employees
* Certain pensioners above prescribed pension limit
* Present or former holders of constitutional posts
* Present or former Ministers
* Present or former Members of Parliament
* Present or former Members of State Legislative Assemblies
* Present or former Mayors of Municipal Corporations
* Present or former Chairpersons of District Panchayats
* Registered professionals such as doctors, engineers, lawyers, chartered accountants, and architects carrying out profession
* Families already receiving duplicate PM-KISAN benefit

Farmers should confirm the latest exclusion rules from the official portal or local agriculture office.

---

# Aadhaar Requirement

Aadhaar is very important for PM-KISAN registration.

During new farmer registration, the Aadhaar number is authenticated with UIDAI. Registration can proceed only after Aadhaar authentication is successful.

Farmers should ensure:

* Aadhaar number is correct.
* Name matches land and bank records as far as possible.
* Aadhaar is active.
* Mobile number linked with Aadhaar is available for OTP-based services.
* Aadhaar details are not already registered incorrectly.
* Aadhaar is not used for duplicate benefit.

---

# Bank Account Requirement

PM-KISAN instalments are transferred through Direct Benefit Transfer.

Farmers should provide a correct and active bank account.

Bank account should have:

* Correct account number
* Correct IFSC code
* Active status
* Completed KYC
* Aadhaar seeding where required
* Name matching beneficiary details
* No freeze or closure issue

Payment may fail if bank details are incorrect.

---

# Land Record Requirement

PM-KISAN eligibility is linked to cultivable landholding.

Farmers should ensure:

* Land is in the farmer’s name.
* Land is recorded as cultivable.
* Land mutation is completed.
* Survey number or khasra details are correct.
* Joint landholding details are clear.
* Land seeding can be completed.
* State land record database supports verification.

If land records are incorrect, registration may be delayed or rejected.

---

# Mobile Number Requirement

A mobile number helps farmers receive OTP, SMS alerts, status updates, and communication.

Farmers should use an active mobile number.

The mobile number may be needed for:

* OTP verification
* e-KYC
* Registration updates
* Beneficiary status
* Payment alerts
* Correction requests
* Grievance tracking

# PM-KISAN Registration Process

---

# Step 1: Check Eligibility

Before registration, the farmer should check whether the family is eligible.

Check:

* Cultivable land ownership
* Family beneficiary status
* Exclusion categories
* Aadhaar availability
* Bank account status
* Land records
* e-KYC requirement

---

# Step 2: Keep Documents Ready

Prepare:

* Aadhaar card
* Bank passbook
* Land record
* Mobile number
* Farmer registration details where applicable
* Self-declaration if required

---

# Step 3: Use Official Registration Channel

Register through the official PM-KISAN portal, CSC, or agriculture department.

For online registration, use the New Farmer Registration option on the official portal.

---

# Step 4: Enter Aadhaar Details

Enter Aadhaar number carefully.

Aadhaar authentication is required before proceeding.

If Aadhaar authentication fails, check:

* Aadhaar number
* Name spelling
* Mobile linking
* UIDAI status
* Existing registration
* Data mismatch

---

# Step 5: Fill Farmer Details

Enter farmer details such as:

* Name
* State
* District
* Village
* Category where required
* Mobile number
* Land details
* Bank details
* Family details
* Declaration

Use correct information only.

---

# Step 6: Submit Land Details

Enter or upload land details as required.

This may include:

* Survey number
* Khata number
* Khasra number
* Area
* Ownership type
* Land record document
* Mutation details

Land details are verified by state or local authorities.

---

# Step 7: Submit Bank Details

Enter bank account number and IFSC code carefully.

Incorrect bank details may cause payment failure.

---

# Step 8: Submit Application

Review the full application before submission.

Check:

* Aadhaar number
* Farmer name
* Mobile number
* Bank account
* IFSC
* Land details
* Village and district
* Declaration

Then submit the application.

---

# Step 9: Save Registration Number

After submission, save:

* Registration number
* Application acknowledgement
* Screenshot or printout
* CSC receipt if registered through CSC
* Date of application
* Mobile number used

This is important for tracking application status.

---

# Step 10: Complete e-KYC

e-KYC is mandatory for PM-KISAN registered farmers.

Farmers can complete e-KYC through:

* OTP-based e-KYC on official PM-KISAN portal
* Biometric e-KYC at nearest CSC centre
* Face authentication through official app or approved channel where available

If e-KYC is pending, instalment payment may be delayed or stopped.

---

# Step 11: Check Registration Status

After registration, farmer should check the application status.

The official PM-KISAN status page for self-registered or CSC-registered farmers allows checking status using Aadhaar number.

Status may show:

* Pending for approval
* Approved
* Rejected
* Under verification
* e-KYC pending
* Land seeding pending
* Bank details pending
* Aadhaar authentication failed
* Payment processed
* Payment failed

---

# Step 12: Correct Errors if Required

If the application is rejected or pending due to errors, the farmer should correct details through official channels.

Common corrections include:

* Aadhaar correction
* Bank correction
* Name correction
* Land record correction
* e-KYC completion
* Mobile number update
* State approval follow-up
* Duplicate record correction

# Registration Through Common Service Centre

Farmers can register through CSC if they are not comfortable with online registration.

## Steps

1. Visit nearest CSC.
2. Carry Aadhaar card, land record, bank passbook, and mobile number.
3. Ask for PM-KISAN registration.
4. Provide correct details.
5. Complete Aadhaar and document verification.
6. Pay only authorized service charge if applicable.
7. Collect receipt or acknowledgement.
8. Save registration number.
9. Check status later.

---

# Online Self Registration

Farmers who are comfortable with digital services can register online.

## General Steps

1. Visit the official PM-KISAN portal.
2. Go to Farmers Corner.
3. Select New Farmer Registration.
4. Enter Aadhaar number.
5. Complete authentication.
6. Fill farmer, land, and bank details.
7. Submit application.
8. Save registration number.
9. Complete e-KYC.
10. Check status regularly.

# Registration for Women Farmers

Women farmers can register if cultivable land is in their name and they meet eligibility rules.

Important points:

* Land record should show woman farmer’s name.
* Aadhaar should be correct.
* Bank account should be active.
* e-KYC should be completed.
* Family should not receive duplicate benefit.

Women farmers should check land mutation and beneficiary status.

---

# Registration for Joint Landholders

Joint landholding requires careful verification.

Important points:

* PM-KISAN benefit is family-based.
* Husband, wife, and minor children are treated as one family unit.
* Separate families in joint landholding may require verification.
* Duplicate benefit in same family is not allowed.

Farmers should contact the local agriculture office if land is jointly owned.

---

# Registration for Inherited Land

A farmer who inherited land should first ensure land mutation is completed.

Steps:

1. Complete mutation in revenue records.
2. Get updated land record.
3. Register with updated land details.
4. Complete Aadhaar and bank verification.
5. Complete e-KYC.
6. Check status.

Incomplete mutation may delay approval.

---

# Registration for Tenant Farmers

PM-KISAN registration is generally linked with landholding in official records.

Tenant farmers and sharecroppers may face difficulty if land is not in their name.

They should check with:

* State agriculture department
* Revenue office
* CSC centre
* Local farmer registration system

Eligibility depends on official scheme rules and state verification.

---

# Registration for New Landowners

Farmers who recently purchased or received land may need additional verification.

They should:

* Complete mutation
* Update land records
* Keep sale deed or inheritance proof
* Contact agriculture office
* Check current rules for recently acquired land
* Avoid false registration before verification

---

# Registration and e-KYC

Registration alone is not enough.

e-KYC is mandatory for registered farmers.

If e-KYC is not completed:

* Application may remain incomplete.
* Instalment may be delayed.
* Payment may stop.
* Beneficiary status may show pending.

Farmers should complete e-KYC as early as possible.

---

# Registration and Land Seeding

Land seeding means linking the farmer’s land details with the PM-KISAN record.

If land seeding is pending:

* Application may remain under verification.
* Instalment may not be released.
* Farmer may need to visit agriculture or revenue office.
* Updated land records may be required.

---

# Registration and Bank Validation

Bank validation confirms whether payment can be transferred to the account.

Bank validation may fail due to:

* Wrong account number
* Wrong IFSC
* Closed account
* Dormant account
* Name mismatch
* Aadhaar-bank mismatch
* Bank KYC pending
* Account freeze

Farmers should correct bank details immediately.

---

# Registration Safety and Fraud Protection

Farmers should be careful while registering.

Do not share:

* OTP
* UPI PIN
* ATM PIN
* Bank password
* Aadhaar OTP
* Internet banking password

Use only official or authorized channels.

Private agents cannot guarantee PM-KISAN approval or faster instalment release.

---

# Registration Mistakes to Avoid

Farmers should avoid:

* Duplicate registration
* Wrong Aadhaar number
* Wrong bank account number
* Wrong IFSC
* Wrong land details
* Registering under ineligible category
* Not completing e-KYC
* Using fake websites
* Sharing OTP with agents
* Not saving registration number
* Ignoring pending status
