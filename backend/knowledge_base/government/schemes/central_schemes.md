# Central Government Agriculture Schemes

# Overview

Central government agriculture schemes are programs launched or supported by the Government of India to help farmers improve income, reduce farming risks, access credit, adopt better technology, manage natural resources, improve marketing, and strengthen long-term agricultural development.

These schemes may support:

* Income assistance
* Crop insurance
* Farm credit
* Irrigation
* Soil health
* Organic farming
* Mechanization
* Market access
* Farmer Producer Organizations
* Storage and post-harvest management
* Livestock, dairy, fisheries, and allied sectors
* Digital agriculture services
* Climate-resilient agriculture

Farmers should always verify current scheme details, eligibility, subsidy amount, application process, and deadlines from official government portals, agriculture department offices, banks, Common Service Centres, or local extension officers.

---

# Why Central Schemes are Important

Central schemes help farmers by reducing financial pressure and improving access to services that may otherwise be costly or difficult to obtain.

Central schemes can help farmers:

* Receive direct income support
* Get crop insurance protection
* Access institutional credit
* Improve irrigation and water efficiency
* Test soil and manage nutrients
* Adopt organic or natural farming
* Purchase machinery or use custom hiring services
* Sell produce through better market platforms
* Join FPOs for collective strength
* Reduce crop failure risk
* Improve productivity and profitability

A farmer should choose schemes based on actual farm need, eligibility, location, crop, and available documents.

# 1. Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi

Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi, commonly called PM-KISAN, is an income support scheme for eligible landholding farmer families.

## Main Benefit

Eligible farmer families receive:

* ₹6,000 per year
* Three instalments per year
* ₹2,000 per instalment
* Direct Benefit Transfer into bank account

## Purpose

PM-KISAN helps farmers meet small farm and household-linked expenses.

It may support:

* Seed purchase
* Fertilizer purchase
* Organic manure
* Pesticides or biopesticides
* Irrigation support
* Small farm tools
* Labor expenses
* Emergency crop needs

## Key Requirements

Farmers usually need:

* Cultivable landholding
* Aadhaar details
* Active bank account
* e-KYC
* Land record verification
* Beneficiary status approval

## Farmer Advisory

PM-KISAN is not a loan and does not need repayment. It is also not crop insurance. Farmers should complete e-KYC and keep bank and land records updated.

# 2. Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana

Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana, commonly called PMFBY, is a crop insurance scheme that protects farmers from financial losses due to crop damage or crop failure caused by covered risks.

## Main Purpose

PMFBY provides crop insurance coverage for notified crops in notified areas.

## Covered Risks May Include

Depending on scheme rules and notification, coverage may include:

* Drought
* Dry spell
* Flood
* Inundation
* Cyclone
* Hailstorm
* Unseasonal rainfall
* Natural fire due to lightning
* Landslide
* Widespread pest attack
* Widespread disease attack
* Prevented sowing
* Post-harvest damage
* Localized calamity

## Farmer Premium Share

General PMFBY farmer premium rates are:

| Crop Type                                 | Farmer Premium Share |
| ----------------------------------------- | -------------------- |
| Kharif food and oilseed crops             | 2% of sum insured    |
| Rabi food and oilseed crops               | 1.5% of sum insured  |
| Annual commercial and horticultural crops | 5% of sum insured    |

Actual premium, notified crop, insurer, and deadline should be checked every season.

## Farmer Advisory

Farmers should confirm crop, area, premium, sum insured, bank details, and policy number after enrollment. Localized calamity and post-harvest losses usually require quick claim intimation.

# 3. Kisan Credit Card

Kisan Credit Card, or KCC, provides timely credit to farmers for crop production and working-capital needs.

## Main Uses

KCC may be used for:

* Seeds
* Fertilizers
* Pesticides
* Organic inputs
* Irrigation expenses
* Labor
* Harvesting
* Post-harvest expenses
* Farm asset maintenance
* Dairy, animal husbandry, and fisheries working capital where eligible

## Benefits

* Flexible credit withdrawal
* Reusable credit limit
* Reduced dependence on moneylenders
* Interest support for eligible timely repayment
* Useful for seasonal farm expenses

## Farmer Advisory

KCC is a loan and must be repaid. Farmers should borrow according to crop budget and repayment capacity.

# 4. Soil Health Card Scheme

The Soil Health Card Scheme supports soil testing and nutrient management.

## Main Purpose

The scheme helps farmers understand soil fertility status and apply fertilizers based on soil test recommendations.

## Soil Test May Include

* Soil pH
* Electrical conductivity
* Organic carbon
* Nitrogen
* Phosphorus
* Potassium
* Sulfur
* Micronutrients
* Soil amendment recommendations

## Benefits

* Reduces unnecessary fertilizer use
* Improves nutrient balance
* Helps correct deficiencies
* Improves crop yield
* Reduces input cost
* Protects soil health

## Farmer Advisory

Farmers should collect representative soil samples and use test reports for crop-wise fertilizer planning.

Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana, commonly called PMKSY, supports irrigation development and efficient water use.

## Main Objectives

PMKSY aims to improve water availability and water-use efficiency in agriculture.

## Major Focus Areas

* Irrigation coverage
* Water conservation
* Micro-irrigation
* Drip irrigation
* Sprinkler irrigation
* Watershed development
* Water harvesting
* Per drop more crop approach

## Benefits

* Reduces drought risk

* Improves crop reliability
* Supports high-value crops

Farmers should check state subsidy rules for drip, sprinkler, farm pond, and irrigation infrastructure.

# 6. National Agriculture Market

National Agriculture Market, commonly called e-NAM, is an online trading platform that connects agricultural markets.

## Main Purpose

e-NAM helps farmers sell produce through a transparent electronic trading system.

## Benefits for Farmers

* Better price discovery
* Access to more buyers
* Access to more markets
* Real-time price and arrival information
* Transparent bidding
* Quality-based sale through assaying where available
* Online payment options

## Farmer Advisory

Farmers should register through their mandi or official e-NAM process and understand grading, assaying, and payment rules before trading.

# How Farmers Should Choose a Scheme

Farmers should not apply randomly. They should select schemes based on farm need.

## Step 1: Identify Main Problem

Examples:

* Low income
* Crop loss risk
* No irrigation
* High fertilizer cost
* No market access
* Labor shortage
* Need storage
* Want organic farming

## Step 2: Match Scheme

Choose the scheme that solves the problem.

Example:

* Crop loss risk: PMFBY
* Seasonal money need: KCC
* Soil fertility issue: Soil Health Card
* Water shortage: PMKSY
* Marketing issue: e-NAM or FPO

## Step 3: Check Eligibility

Before applying, check:

* Land requirement
* Farmer category
* Crop eligibility
* Area eligibility
* Documents
* Deadline
* Subsidy rules
* Bank linkage
* State implementation

## Step 4: Apply Through Official Channel

Use only official portals, CSCs, banks, government offices, or authorized agencies.

## Step 5: Keep Records

Save:

* Application number
* Receipt
* Payment proof
* Sanction letter
* Beneficiary status
* Bank transaction
* Scheme approval documents

# Scheme Benefits and Limitations

Central schemes are helpful, but farmers should understand limitations.

## Benefits

* Reduces financial burden
* Improves access to inputs and services
* Encourages modern practices
* Supports risk management
* Improves market participation
* Helps build farm assets

## Limitations

* Eligibility conditions apply
* Deadlines must be followed
* Subsidy may not be immediate
* State implementation may vary
* Documents are required
* Benefits may be limited by budget or target
* Application approval is not guaranteed
* Some schemes require farmer contribution

---

# Common Mistakes to Avoid

Farmers should avoid:

* Applying without checking eligibility
* Missing deadlines
* Submitting wrong documents
* Using fake websites or agents
* Not saving application number
* Not checking beneficiary status
* Confusing loan, subsidy, insurance, and income support
* Assuming all schemes are available everywhere
* Ignoring state-specific rules
* Not following up after application

---

# Fraud and Safety Advisory

Farmers should be careful while applying for schemes.

Do not share:

* OTP
* ATM PIN
* UPI PIN
* Bank password
* Aadhaar OTP
* Internet banking password

Use only:

* Official government portals
* CSC centres
* Bank branches
* Agriculture department offices
* Authorized scheme officials

No private person can guarantee benefit approval in exchange for unofficial payment.

---

# Farmer Recommendations

Farmers are advised to:

1. Identify the farm problem before selecting a scheme.
2. Check eligibility and deadline.
3. Use official portals or authorized offices.
4. Keep Aadhaar, bank, and land records updated.
5. Complete e-KYC where required.
6. Maintain farm records.
7. Save all application receipts.
8. Follow up regularly.
9. Join FPOs where useful.
10. Verify current scheme rules every season.

# Central Scheme Checklist

## Before Applying

* Identify scheme name.
* Check eligibility.
* Check documents.
* Check deadline.
* Check official portal.
* Check whether scheme is active in your state.
* Check farmer contribution if any.
* Check bank linkage if required.

## During Application

* Fill correct details.
* Upload clear documents.
* Use correct bank account.
* Provide active mobile number.
* Save application number.
* Collect receipt.

## After Application

* Check status.
* Respond to verification.
* Keep documents ready.
* Track benefit payment or sanction.
* File grievance if needed.
* Maintain records.
