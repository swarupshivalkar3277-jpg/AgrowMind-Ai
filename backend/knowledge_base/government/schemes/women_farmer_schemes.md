# Women Farmer Schemes

# Overview

Women farmer schemes are government programs, credit facilities, training initiatives, livelihood missions, subsidies, and support services designed to improve the participation, productivity, income, recognition, and empowerment of women in agriculture and allied sectors.

Women play an important role in farming activities such as sowing, transplanting, weeding, irrigation support, livestock care, seed selection, harvesting, post-harvest handling, food processing, value addition, and marketing. However, many women farmers face challenges such as limited land ownership, low access to credit, limited training, lack of machinery access, lower recognition as farmers, and weaker market linkages.

Women farmer schemes help women farmers:

* Access government benefits
* Improve farm productivity
* Build sustainable livelihoods
* Access training and technology
* Join Self Help Groups and Farmer Producer Organizations
* Access credit and savings systems
* Improve livestock and allied income
* Adopt organic and climate-resilient farming
* Improve post-harvest and value addition activities
* Strengthen economic independence

Women farmers should verify current scheme rules, eligibility, application process, subsidy amount, and documents from official government portals, agriculture department offices, rural livelihood missions, banks, Common Service Centres, Krishi Vigyan Kendras, or local extension officers.

---

# Who is a Woman Farmer?

A woman farmer may be:

* A woman who owns agricultural land
* A woman who cultivates family land
* A woman tenant farmer
* A woman sharecropper
* A woman agricultural laborer
* A woman involved in livestock rearing
* A woman managing dairy, poultry, goatery, fisheries, or backyard farming
* A woman member of a Self Help Group
* A woman member of a Farmer Producer Organization
* A woman involved in post-harvest processing or value addition
* A woman managing kitchen garden, seed production, nursery, or organic inputs

Official scheme eligibility may differ. Some schemes require land in the woman’s name, while others support women through SHGs, groups, training, or livelihood activities.

* Livelihood improvement
* Community institutions
* Skill building
* Agro-ecological practices
* Livestock and allied livelihoods

MKSP may support women farmers through:

* Training
* Demonstrations
* Community resource persons
* Sustainable farming practices
* Livelihood planning
* Group-based support
* Capacity building
* Low-cost agriculture methods
* Livestock-related activities

# 6. Kisan Credit Card for Women Farmers

Women farmers with land or eligible cultivation proof may apply for Kisan Credit Card.

* Labor

* Bank checks documents and repayment capacity.
* Land or cultivation proof may be required.
* Timely repayment may provide interest benefits where eligible.

---

# 7. PM-KISAN for Women Farmers

Women farmers can benefit from PM-KISAN if they are eligible landholding farmers and meet scheme requirements.

## Key Requirements

* Cultivable land in the woman farmer’s name
* Aadhaar details
* Active bank account
* e-KYC
* Land seeding
* No exclusion category
* State verification

## Benefits

* ₹6,000 per year where eligible
* Three instalments of ₹2,000 each
* Direct transfer to bank account
* No repayment required

## Farmer Advisory

Women farmers should keep land records, Aadhaar, bank details, and e-KYC updated.

---

# 8. Crop Insurance for Women Farmers

Women farmers growing notified crops may enroll in crop insurance schemes such as PMFBY where available.

## Benefits

* Crop loss protection
* Financial support after covered crop damage
* Reduced risk from drought, flood, hailstorm, cyclone, pests, and diseases where covered
* Support for post-harvest and localized losses where applicable

## Farmer Advisory

Women farmers should verify:

* Crop is notified
* Area is notified
* Premium is paid
* Policy number is saved
* Bank account is correct
* Claim reporting rules are known

---

# Step 1: Identify the Need

Women farmers should first identify the main need.

Examples:

* Crop input support
* Training
* Dairy animal
* Poultry unit
* Goat unit
* Kitchen garden
* Drip irrigation
* Farm tool
* Processing unit
* Credit

---

# Step 2: Select the Correct Scheme

Choose the scheme based on need.

Examples:

* Income support: PM-KISAN where eligible
* Crop loss protection: PMFBY
* Credit: KCC or SHG-bank linkage
* Sustainable agriculture: MKSP or organic farming scheme
* Livestock: Animal husbandry department scheme
* Processing: SHG or food-processing scheme
* Market linkage: FPO or state marketing scheme

---

# Step 3: Check Eligibility

Check:

* Is land required?
* Is SHG membership required?
* Is bank account required?
* Is training required?
* Is the scheme active in the district?
* Is the woman applicant eligible?
* Is there any category preference?
* Is farmer contribution required?
* Is prior approval required?

---

# Step 4: Collect Documents

Keep all required documents ready before applying.

# Safety and Fraud Advisory

Women farmers should be careful while applying for schemes.

Do not share:

* OTP
* UPI PIN
* ATM PIN
* Bank password
* Aadhaar OTP
* Internet banking password

Use only official channels such as:

* Government portals
* CSC centres
* Banks
* Agriculture department offices
* SHG federation offices
* FPO offices
* Rural livelihood mission offices

No private agent can guarantee government scheme approval.

---

# Common Mistakes to Avoid

Women farmers should avoid:

* Applying through fake agents
* Sharing OTP or bank PIN
* Not saving application number
* Not checking eligibility
* Submitting wrong bank details
* Not completing e-KYC
* Taking loans without repayment plan
* Starting enterprise without market linkage
* Not keeping records
* Ignoring training opportunities
* Not joining groups where group support is available

---

# Farmer Recommendations

Women farmers are advised to:

1. Join SHGs, FPOs, or farmer groups where available.
2. Keep Aadhaar, bank account, and mobile number updated.
3. Check whether land records can be updated in the woman farmer’s name where applicable.
4. Use PM-KISAN if eligible and verified.
5. Use KCC or SHG credit carefully with repayment planning.
6. Attend training through agriculture department, KVK, or livelihood mission.
7. Explore livestock, kitchen garden, organic input, and processing activities.
8. Use official channels only for scheme applications.
9. Maintain all scheme, loan, and production records.
10. Build market linkage before starting larger enterprises.
