# Fertilizer Subsidy

# Overview

Fertilizer subsidy is government support provided to make fertilizers available to farmers at affordable prices. Fertilizers are important for crop nutrition, but their market prices can be high because of raw material cost, import cost, energy cost, transport cost, and global price changes.

The Government of India supports fertilizer availability through subsidy systems so that farmers can access essential nutrients such as nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium, and sulfur at reasonable prices.

Fertilizer subsidy helps farmers:

* Reduce input cost
* Maintain crop productivity
* Access important fertilizers at affordable prices
* Support balanced nutrient use
* Improve food production
* Reduce financial stress during crop season
* Improve fertilizer availability
* Support national food security

Farmers should use fertilizers carefully and based on soil testing, crop requirement, and local agricultural recommendations.

---

# What is Fertilizer Subsidy?

Fertilizer subsidy is financial support provided by the government to reduce the cost of fertilizers for farmers.

In India, subsidy is generally provided to fertilizer companies or manufacturers so that farmers can buy fertilizers at controlled, subsidized, or affordable prices.

This means farmers may not always receive fertilizer subsidy as direct cash. Instead, the subsidy benefit is reflected in lower fertilizer prices at authorized sale points.

---

# Why Fertilizer Subsidy is Needed

Fertilizers are essential for crop growth, but fertilizer prices can increase due to:

* Imported raw material cost
* Natural gas price
* International market price
* Currency exchange rate
* Freight and transport cost
* Supply disruptions
* Manufacturing cost
* Seasonal demand

Without subsidy, fertilizer prices may become unaffordable for many farmers.

Fertilizer subsidy helps ensure that farmers can buy fertilizers in time for sowing, transplanting, top dressing, flowering, and crop growth stages.

---

The main objectives are:

1. Make fertilizers affordable for farmers.
2. Ensure timely availability of fertilizers.
3. Support crop productivity.
4. Promote balanced nutrient use.
5. Reduce input cost burden.

# Urea Subsidy

Urea is a nitrogen fertilizer widely used by farmers.

## Importance of Urea

Urea supplies nitrogen, which supports:

* Leaf growth
* Green color
* Vegetative growth
* Photosynthesis
* Protein formation

## Subsidy System

Urea price is controlled by the government, and subsidy is provided to manufacturers or importers to make urea available to farmers at affordable prices.

## Farmer Advisory

Farmers should avoid excessive urea use. Too much urea can cause:

* Excess vegetative growth
* Weak stems
* More pest and disease attack
* Delayed maturity
* Poor fruit quality
* Nutrient imbalance
* Soil health problems
* Higher production cost

Use urea according to crop stage and soil test recommendation.

---

# Nutrient Based Subsidy

Nutrient Based Subsidy, commonly called NBS, applies to Phosphatic and Potassic fertilizers.

## Fertilizers Covered

NBS generally covers P and K fertilizers such as:

* DAP
* MOP
* SSP
* NPK grades
* NPKS grades
* Other approved P and K fertilizers

## How NBS Works

Under NBS, subsidy is based on nutrient content.

Nutrients considered may include:

* Nitrogen
* Phosphorus
* Potassium
* Sulfur

The government notifies subsidy rates for these nutrients for a season.

## Purpose of NBS

NBS aims to:

* Make P and K fertilizers affordable
* Encourage balanced fertilizer use
* Reduce overdependence on urea
* Support soil health
* Improve crop productivity
* Support availability during crop season

The NBS scheme was introduced from 1 April 2010 and is designed to make fertilizers available to farmers at subsidized, affordable, and fair prices while encouraging balanced and efficient use. For Kharif 2026, the government approved NBS rates for P and K fertilizers, including DAP and NPKS grades, for the period from 1 April 2026 to 30 September 2026.

# Fertilizer Subsidy and DBT

Fertilizer subsidy in India uses a Direct Benefit Transfer system for fertilizers, but it is different from farmer cash transfer schemes.

## Important Point

In fertilizer DBT, subsidy is generally released to fertilizer companies after sale is recorded through Point of Sale machines at authorized fertilizer retailers.

Farmers buy fertilizer at subsidized price from retailers.

## Farmer Role

Farmers may need to provide:

* Aadhaar number or farmer identification where required
* Mobile number
* Land or crop details where asked
* Fertilizer purchase details

The retailer records the sale through the PoS system.

---

# How Farmers Receive Fertilizer Subsidy Benefit

Farmers usually receive the benefit through reduced fertilizer price, not direct cash.

## Example

If the market cost of a fertilizer is high, the government provides subsidy support so that the farmer pays a lower retail price.

The subsidy is paid to the fertilizer company or supplier as per government rules.

# Balanced Fertilizer Use

Fertilizer subsidy is useful only when fertilizers are used properly.

Balanced fertilizer use means applying nutrients according to:

* Soil test
* Crop requirement
* Crop stage
* Irrigation availability
* Organic matter status
* Previous crop
* Local recommendation

Balanced fertilization includes:

* Nitrogen
* Phosphorus
* Potassium
* Sulfur
* Micronutrients
* Organic manure
* Biofertilizers

---

# Why Balanced Fertilization is Important

Balanced fertilization helps:

* Improve crop yield
* Improve fertilizer-use efficiency
* Reduce nutrient wastage
* Improve soil health
* Reduce pest and disease susceptibility
* Improve fruit and grain quality
* Reduce long-term soil nutrient imbalance

Overuse of one fertilizer and underuse of others can reduce productivity.

---

# Problems from Imbalanced Fertilizer Use

Farmers should avoid applying fertilizers only by habit.

Imbalanced fertilizer use can cause:

* Nitrogen excess
* Phosphorus deficiency
* Potassium deficiency
* Micronutrient deficiency
* Poor root growth
* Low flowering
* Poor fruit quality
* Soil acidity or alkalinity problems
* Lower fertilizer response
* Higher crop disease risk

---

# Role of Soil Testing

Soil testing helps farmers decide which fertilizers are actually needed.

A soil test may show:

* Soil pH
* Electrical conductivity
* Organic carbon
* Nitrogen status
* Phosphorus status
* Potassium status
* Sulfur status
* Micronutrient status

Farmers should use soil test reports to reduce unnecessary fertilizer purchase.

* Farmyard manure

Integrated nutrient management gives better results than depending only on chemical fertilizers.

# Fertilizer Subsidy and Micronutrients

Fertilizer subsidy mainly supports major fertilizers, but farmers should not ignore micronutrients.

Common micronutrient deficiencies include:

# Fertilizer Purchase Guidelines for Farmers

Farmers should buy fertilizers carefully.

## Before Purchase

* Check crop requirement.
* Check soil test report.
* Calculate required quantity.
* Compare available fertilizer grades.
* Confirm fertilizer availability.
* Plan purchase before peak demand.

## During Purchase

* Buy from authorized retailer.
* Check fertilizer name and grade.
* Check bag seal.
* Check manufacturing details.
* Check weight.
* Ask for bill or receipt.
* Ensure sale is recorded properly.

## After Purchase

* Store fertilizer in dry place.
* Keep away from moisture.
* Keep away from children and animals.
* Use according to recommendation.
* Keep purchase receipt for records.

---

# Fertilizer Bill and Receipt

Farmers should always collect a bill.

The bill may help with:

* Purchase proof
* Complaint in case of poor quality
* Farm record keeping
* Cost calculation
* Loan or subsidy documentation
* Insurance or scheme records where needed

---

# Identifying Genuine Fertilizer

Farmers should check:

* Authorized brand
* Proper packaging
* Clear nutrient grade
* Batch number
* Manufacturing date
* MRP
* Weight
* Company name
* Bag seal
* No tampering
* No moisture damage

Do not buy loose or suspicious fertilizers without proper details.

---

# Fertilizer Storage

Fertilizers should be stored properly.

## Storage Guidelines

* Store in dry place.
* Keep bags on wooden planks or raised platform.
* Avoid contact with water.
* Keep away from direct rain.
* Keep away from seeds, food, and animal feed.
* Do not mix different fertilizers randomly.
* Keep bags closed after opening.
* Use older stock first.

Moisture can cause caking and nutrient loss.

---

# Fertilizer Application Best Practices

## Right Source

Choose the correct fertilizer based on nutrient need.

## Right Dose

Apply recommended quantity.

## Right Time

Apply at crop stage when plants need nutrients.

## Right Method

Apply near root zone, through basal application, top dressing, fertigation, or foliar application as recommended.

## Right Place

Avoid placing fertilizer too close to seed or roots if it may cause burning.

# Fertilizer Subsidy and Crop Insurance

Fertilizer purchase records may help show crop activity in some documentation contexts.

However, fertilizer subsidy is different from crop insurance.

| Fertilizer Subsidy                | Crop Insurance                                |
| --------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------- |
| Reduces input cost                | Provides compensation after insured crop loss |

# What to Do if Fertilizer is Not Available

Farmers should:

1. Contact authorized retailer.
2. Check nearby cooperative or fertilizer society.
3. Ask agriculture department about stock position.
4. Avoid black-market purchase.
5. Do not pay above official price.
6. Use alternative fertilizer grades only after expert advice.
7. Adjust fertilizer plan based on nutrient equivalence.
8. Keep records of purchase attempts if needed.

---

# What to Do if Retailer Overcharges

If a retailer charges more than allowed price, farmer should:

* Ask for printed bill.
* Check MRP on fertilizer bag.
* Note retailer name.
* Keep bag and receipt.
* Contact agriculture department or fertilizer inspector.
* Report through official complaint channels.
* Avoid buying from unauthorized sellers.

---

# What to Do if Fertilizer Quality is Doubtful

If fertilizer seems fake or poor quality:

* Do not apply immediately.
* Keep the bag and bill.
* Take photos of bag details.
* Contact agriculture officer.
* Ask for sample testing where available.
* Report suspicious seller.
* Avoid buying loose fertilizer without label.
