# Irrigation Subsidy

# Overview

Irrigation subsidy is financial assistance provided by the government to help farmers install, improve, or maintain irrigation systems. It is mainly used to promote efficient water use, reduce irrigation cost, improve crop productivity, and support farming in drought-prone or water-scarce areas.

In India, irrigation subsidy is commonly linked with schemes such as Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana, also called PMKSY, and its Per Drop More Crop component. State governments may also provide additional subsidy or top-up support for drip irrigation, sprinkler irrigation, farm ponds, pipelines, water storage tanks, solar pumps, and water conservation structures.

Irrigation subsidy helps farmers:

* Reduce cost of irrigation system installation
* Save water
* Improve crop yield and quality
* Reduce drought risk
* Improve fertilizer-use efficiency
* Reduce labor cost
* Support high-value crops
* Increase area under irrigation
* Improve farm income
* Promote climate-resilient agriculture

Farmers should always verify current subsidy percentage, eligibility, application process, approved vendors, technical specifications, and documents from official agriculture department portals, PMKSY/PDMC portal, state agriculture department, horticulture department, Common Service Centre, or local agriculture officer.

* Pipelines

* Farm ponds
* Borewell recharge structures
* Solar pumps

The exact support depends on the scheme, state, farmer category, crop, landholding, and budget availability.

# Why Irrigation Subsidy is Important

Water is one of the most important resources in agriculture. Many farmers face problems such as irregular rainfall, groundwater decline, drought, high irrigation cost, electricity shortage, poor water distribution, and low water-use efficiency.

Irrigation subsidy helps farmers adopt water-saving systems that may otherwise be costly.

It is especially important for:

* Small farmers
* Marginal farmers
* Rainfed farmers
* Drought-prone areas
* Horticulture farmers
* Vegetable growers
* Orchard farmers
* Farmers using borewell water
* Farmers with limited labor
* Farmers growing high-value crops

# Major Irrigation Subsidy Schemes

---

# Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana

Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana, commonly called PMKSY, is a major government program for irrigation and water management.

## Main Aim

The broad aim is to improve irrigation access and water-use efficiency.

## Important Components

PMKSY may include:

* Per Drop More Crop
* Har Khet Ko Pani
* Watershed development
* Water conservation
* Source creation
* Field-level irrigation efficiency

## Farmer Advisory

Farmers should check which PMKSY component is active in their state or district.

---

# Per Drop More Crop

Per Drop More Crop, commonly called PDMC, promotes micro-irrigation technologies such as drip and sprinkler irrigation.

## Main Focus

PDMC focuses on improving water-use efficiency at the farm level.

## Supported Systems

* Drip irrigation
* Sprinkler irrigation
* Micro-sprinkler
* Rain gun
* Filters
* Fertigation units
* Pipes and accessories as per approved design
* Other micro-irrigation components approved by the scheme

## General Subsidy Pattern

Under PDMC, financial assistance is generally:

| Farmer Category            | General Assistance |
| -------------------------- | ------------------ |
| Small and marginal farmers | 55%                |
| Other farmers              | 45%                |

Some states may provide additional top-up subsidy from state budget.

## Area Limit

Assistance for micro-irrigation installation is generally limited to 5 hectares per beneficiary.

## Important Note

Actual subsidy amount depends on current guidelines, crop spacing, system design, approved unit cost, farmer category, state rules, and vendor quotation.

# Types of Irrigation Subsidy

---

# 1. Drip Irrigation Subsidy

Drip irrigation delivers water slowly and directly near plant roots through pipes, laterals, emitters, and control valves.

## Suitable Crops

* Tomato
* Chilli
* Brinjal
* Onion
* Cotton
* Sugarcane
* Banana
* Grapes
* Pomegranate
* Mango
* Coconut
* Citrus
* Flowers
* Vegetables
* Plantation crops

## Benefits

* Saves water
* Reduces weed growth
* Improves fertilizer efficiency
* Supports fertigation
* Reduces labor
* Improves fruit quality
* Reduces water stress
* Suitable for high-value crops

## Farmer Advisory

Drip irrigation requires proper filtration, pressure management, maintenance, and flushing.

---

# 2. Sprinkler Irrigation Subsidy

Sprinkler irrigation applies water like rainfall through sprinkler nozzles.

## Suitable Crops

* Wheat
* Pulses
* Oilseeds
* Groundnut
* Fodder crops
* Vegetables
* Millets
* Lawns
* Light-textured soils

## Benefits

* Uniform water application
* Useful in sandy soils
* Reduces water wastage
* Suitable for undulating land
* Useful where flood irrigation is difficult

## Farmer Advisory

Avoid sprinkler irrigation during high wind because water distribution becomes uneven.

---

# 3. Micro-Sprinkler Subsidy

Micro-sprinklers apply water over a smaller area than normal sprinklers.

## Suitable For

* Orchards
* Coconut
* Mango
* Banana
* Nursery
* Vegetables
* Plantation crops

## Benefits

* Saves water
* Suitable for tree basins
* Helps maintain root-zone moisture
* Useful in sandy or light soils

---

# 4. Rain Gun Subsidy

Rain guns apply water over a larger area using high-pressure spray.

## Suitable For

* Fodder crops
* Groundnut
* Vegetables
* Large field crops
* Emergency irrigation

## Benefits

* Covers larger area
* Useful for protective irrigation
* Can be shifted between fields

## Precaution

Rain guns need adequate water pressure and may be affected by wind.

---

# 5. Farm Pond Subsidy

Farm pond subsidy supports construction of ponds for rainwater harvesting and protective irrigation.

## Benefits

* Stores rainwater
* Supports life-saving irrigation
* Reduces drought risk
* Useful for vegetables and orchards
* Supports fish farming where suitable
* Helps groundwater recharge in some designs

## Farmer Advisory

Farmers should plan site, size, lining, inlet, outlet, safety fencing, and water source before construction.

---

# 6. Pipeline Subsidy

Pipeline subsidy supports conveyance of water from source to field.

## Benefits

* Reduces water loss
* Saves labor
* Prevents seepage loss in open channels
* Improves water distribution
* Supports drip or sprinkler systems

## Suitable For

* Borewell to field conveyance
* Farm pond to field
* Canal outlet to farm
* Community irrigation

---

# 7. Solar Pump Subsidy

Solar pump schemes support solar-powered irrigation pumps.

## Benefits

* Reduces diesel cost
* Reduces electricity dependence
* Supports irrigation in remote areas
* Useful for small irrigation systems
* Can be combined with drip irrigation

## Farmer Advisory

Solar pump capacity should match water source, crop water requirement, and irrigation system design.

---

# 8. Water Storage Tank Subsidy

Water storage tanks help farmers store water for irrigation.

## Benefits

* Helps schedule irrigation
* Supports drip systems
* Useful where electricity supply is irregular
* Supports fertigation
* Helps manage water from borewell, canal, or farm pond

---

# 9. Community Irrigation Subsidy

Community irrigation schemes support groups of farmers.

## Examples

* Community wells
* Water tanks
* Lift irrigation systems
* Pipeline networks
* Shared drip systems
* Solar pump systems
* Water user groups

## Benefits

* Reduces cost per farmer
* Helps small farmers
* Supports water sharing
* Improves irrigation access

## Farmer Advisory

Community systems require clear water-sharing rules and maintenance responsibilities.

* Agriculture department
* CSC centre
* District agriculture office
* Taluka agriculture office

# Step 5: Field Verification

Officials or technical staff may verify:

* Farmer identity
* Land location
* Water source
* Crop area
* Irrigation need
* Technical feasibility
* Previous subsidy status

---

# Step 6: Vendor Selection and Design

The vendor may prepare:

* System design

Some schemes require approval before installation.

Farmers should not install the system before approval if prior sanction is required.

---

# Step 8: Installation

Farmer should check:

* Mainline
* Sub-main
* Laterals

* Filters
* Valves
* Pressure
* Flushing system
* Fertigation unit
* Uniformity
* Warranty

---

# Step 9: Inspection

After installation, officials may inspect the system.

They may check:

* Actual area covered
* Component quality
* System functioning
* Approved design compliance
* Farmer contribution
* Vendor bill
* GPS or photo evidence
* Crop and field details

---

# Step 10: Subsidy Release

Subsidy may be released:

* Directly to farmer
* To vendor
* Through DBT
* As reimbursement
* Through bank-linked system

This depends on state and scheme rules.

---

# Farmer Contribution

Most subsidy schemes do not cover full cost.

Farmer may need to pay:

* Remaining cost after subsidy
* Additional cost above approved unit cost
* Maintenance cost
* Repairs
* Filter cleaning material
* Labor for installation support
* Extra fittings outside approved design

Farmers should understand their share before applying.

---

# Approved Unit Cost

Subsidy is usually calculated on approved unit cost, not always on the actual market quotation.

If actual cost is higher than approved unit cost, the farmer may need to pay the difference.

---

# Importance of Approved Vendors

Many schemes require irrigation system purchase from approved or registered vendors.

Benefits of approved vendors:

* Standard quality
* Proper design
* Warranty support
* Scheme compliance
* Inspection support
* Subsidy processing

Farmers should avoid purchasing unapproved systems if scheme rules require approved vendors.

# Technical Considerations Before Installation

Before installing irrigation system, check:

* Water source capacity
* Water quality
* Field slope
* Soil type

* Pump capacity
* Pressure requirement
* Filter requirement
* Power supply
* Area to be irrigated

# Water Quality Testing

Water quality affects drip and sprinkler performance.

Important parameters:

* Salinity
* pH
* Hardness
* Iron
* Sand or silt
* Algae
* Bicarbonates
* Suspended particles

Poor water quality can clog emitters and reduce system life.

---

# Filter Requirement

Filters are essential in drip irrigation.

Common filters include:

* Screen filter
* Disc filter
* Sand media filter
* Hydrocyclone filter

Filter choice depends on water source.

| Water Source    | Suggested Filter Need                 |
| --------------- | ------------------------------------- |
| Borewell        | Screen or disc filter                 |
| Open well       | Screen or disc filter                 |
| Farm pond       | Sand media plus screen or disc filter |
| Canal water     | Sand media plus screen or disc filter |
| Water with sand | Hydrocyclone may be needed            |

---

# Maintenance of Drip Irrigation

Drip system needs regular maintenance.

## Important Tasks

* Clean filters regularly
* Flush mainline and laterals
* Check emitter clogging
* Repair leaks
* Maintain pressure
* Clean fertigation tank
* Avoid physical damage
* Check rodent damage
* Use acid or chlorine treatment only under expert advice

---

# Maintenance of Sprinkler System

Sprinkler system maintenance includes:

* Cleaning nozzles
* Checking pressure
* Preventing leakage
* Moving pipes carefully
* Avoiding operation in strong wind
* Checking uniformity
* Protecting pipes from damage
* Storing pipes properly after use

# Irrigation Subsidy and Fertigation

Fertigation means applying fertilizers through irrigation water.

## Benefits

* Better nutrient-use efficiency
* Smaller split applications
* Reduced labor
* Better crop response
* Less fertilizer wastage

## Precautions

* Use only water-soluble fertilizers where required.
* Clean filters after fertigation.
* Do not mix incompatible fertilizers.
* Follow expert schedule.
* Avoid over-fertilization.

---

# Irrigation Subsidy and Water Saving

Micro-irrigation saves water by applying water near the root zone and reducing losses.

Water savings may occur due to:

* Less evaporation
* Less runoff
* Less deep percolation
* Reduced weed area
* Better irrigation scheduling
* Better distribution uniformity

Actual saving depends on system design and management.
