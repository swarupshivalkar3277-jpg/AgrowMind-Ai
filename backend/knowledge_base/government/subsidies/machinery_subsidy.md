# Farm Machinery Subsidy

# Overview

Farm machinery subsidy is government financial assistance provided to farmers, farmer groups, Self Help Groups, Farmer Producer Organizations, cooperatives, rural entrepreneurs, and custom hiring centres for purchasing or accessing agricultural machinery and equipment.

Farm mechanization helps farmers complete agricultural operations on time, reduce labor dependency, improve efficiency, reduce drudgery, improve crop productivity, and manage larger areas with better precision.

Machinery subsidy may support:

* Tractors
* Power tillers
* Seed drills
* Rotavators
* Cultivators
* Weeders
* Sprayers
* Reapers
* Harvesters
* Threshers
* Planters
* Transplanters
* Brush cutters
* Chaff cutters
* Post-harvest machines
* Custom hiring centres
* Farm machinery banks
* Drone-based agriculture services where eligible

Subsidy rules vary by scheme, state, farmer category, machine type, approved cost, budget availability, and application portal. Farmers should always verify current subsidy rates, eligible machinery, vendor rules, and application procedure from the agriculture department, state DBT portal, district agriculture office, Common Service Centre, or official machinery scheme portal.

---

# What is Farm Machinery Subsidy?

Farm machinery subsidy is financial support that reduces the cost of buying or accessing agricultural machines.

It may be provided as:

* Direct subsidy to farmer
* Reimbursement after purchase and verification
* Subsidy to approved vendor
* Credit-linked capital subsidy
* Support for custom hiring centres
* Support for farm machinery banks
* Support for machinery demonstrations
* Support for machinery training
* Support for machinery services through farmer groups

The main purpose is to make modern agricultural equipment affordable and accessible, especially for small and marginal farmers.

---

# Importance of Farm Mechanization

Farm mechanization is important because agriculture requires timely operations. Delay in land preparation, sowing, weeding, spraying, harvesting, or threshing can reduce yield and quality.

Mechanization helps farmers:

* Complete operations on time
* Reduce labor shortage problems
* Save time
* Reduce physical drudgery
* Improve precision
* Reduce post-harvest losses
* Improve crop establishment
* Improve input-use efficiency
* Reduce cost in the long term
* Increase farm productivity

Mechanization is especially useful where labor is costly or unavailable during peak season.

# Sub-Mission on Agricultural Mechanization

Sub-Mission on Agricultural Mechanization, commonly called SMAM, is a major government initiative that promotes agricultural mechanization.

## Main Objectives

SMAM focuses on:

* Increasing farm mechanization among small and marginal farmers
* Improving mechanization in areas with low farm power availability
* Promoting custom hiring services
* Establishing farm machinery banks
* Supporting training and demonstrations
* Encouraging certified and tested machinery
* Promoting machinery for crop production, post-harvest management, and value addition

## Main Support Areas

SMAM may support:

* Individual farm machinery purchase
* Custom hiring centres
* Farm machinery banks
* High-tech machinery hubs
* Demonstration and training
* Post-harvest technology
* Crop residue management equipment
* Drone technology where eligible
* Mechanization in low-mechanized areas

## Farmer Advisory

Farmers should check state implementation rules because SMAM benefits are delivered through state annual action plans and state portals.

# Custom Hiring Centres

Custom Hiring Centres, also called CHCs, provide agricultural machinery on rent to farmers.

## Why CHCs are Important

Many small farmers cannot afford costly machines such as tractors, harvesters, transplanters, or high-end sprayers. CHCs allow farmers to use machines without buying them.

## Benefits

* Reduces need for individual machinery purchase
* Helps small and marginal farmers
* Provides machines during peak season
* Reduces labor cost
* Improves timely operations
* Supports expensive machinery use
* Creates rural entrepreneurship

## Suitable Machines for CHCs

* Tractor
* Rotavator
* Seed drill
* Happy seeder
* Reaper
* Thresher
* Combine harvester
* Power weeder
* Sprayer
* Planter
* Transplanter
* Baler
* Laser land leveler
* Drone spraying services where allowed

## Farmer Advisory

Farmers should book machinery before peak season and compare custom hiring cost with machine ownership cost.

# Application Process

---

# Step 1: Identify Machinery Need

Farmer should decide which operation needs mechanization.

Examples:

* Land preparation
* Sowing
* Weeding
* Spraying
* Harvesting
* Threshing
* Processing
* Crop residue management
* Fodder cutting

---

# Step 2: Check Scheme Eligibility

Check:

* Is the machine eligible?
* Is the farmer category eligible?
* What is the subsidy rate?
* What is the approved cost?
* Is prior approval required?
* Is an approved vendor required?
* Is bank loan needed?
* Is the machine already subsidized earlier?
* What documents are needed?
* What is the application deadline?

---

# Step 3: Select Machine Carefully

Select machine based on:

* Crop
* Farm size
* Soil type
* Power source
* Tractor HP requirement
* Labor availability
* Maintenance ability
* Service centre availability
* Spare parts availability
* Expected annual use
* Custom hiring potential

Do not buy a machine only because subsidy is available.

---

# Step 4: Apply Through Official Channel

Application may be done through:

* State agriculture portal
* Agriculture department office
* DBT portal
* District agriculture office
* Taluka agriculture office
* CSC centre
* SMAM/Farm Mechanization portal where applicable
* Bank branch for credit-linked subsidy
* FPO or SHG channel where applicable

---

# Step 5: Submit Documents and Quotation

Submit:

* Application form
* Farmer documents
* Land record
* Machine quotation
* Vendor details
* Bank details
* Category certificate where applicable
* Self-declaration

---

# Step 6: Get Approval or Sanction

Some schemes require approval before purchase.

Farmers should not purchase machinery before approval if scheme rules require prior sanction.

---

# Step 7: Purchase from Approved Source

Buy machinery from approved dealer or vendor if required.

Check:

* Model number
* Serial number
* Warranty
* Bill
* GST invoice
* User manual
* Demonstration
* Spare parts availability
* Service support

---

# Step 8: Inspection and Verification

Officials may inspect:

* Machine physically
* Serial number
* Bill and invoice
* Farmer identity
* Land details
* Machine use
* Photographs
* GPS location where required

---

# Step 9: Subsidy Release

Subsidy may be released:

* Directly to farmer bank account
* To vendor
* Through bank loan adjustment
* As reimbursement
* As credit-linked subsidy

This depends on state and scheme rules.

---

# Step 10: Use and Maintain Machinery

Farmers should use machinery properly and maintain it regularly.

Subsidized machinery should not be sold or transferred before the lock-in period if scheme rules prohibit it.

---

# Important Points Before Buying Machinery

Farmers should ask:

1. Is this machine really needed?
2. How many days per year will it be used?
3. Can it be rented instead?
4. Can it generate custom hiring income?
5. Is spare part support available?
6. Who will operate it?
7. Is training needed?
8. What is fuel cost?
9. What is repair cost?
10. Can loan repayment be managed?

---

# Buying vs Custom Hiring

Small farmers should compare buying and renting.

| Situation                     | Better Option                |
| ----------------------------- | ---------------------------- |
| Small landholding             | Custom hiring may be better  |
| Machine needed only few days  | Rent from CHC                |
| Machine used many days yearly | Purchase may be useful       |
| High-cost machinery           | Group ownership or CHC       |
| FPO has many members          | Machinery bank may be useful |
| No operator available         | Custom hiring with operator  |
| Repair support unavailable    | Avoid purchase or rent       |

# Machinery Safety

Farm machinery can cause serious injuries if used carelessly.

## Safety Practices

* Read user manual.
* Use trained operators.
* Keep children away.
* Do not wear loose clothing near moving parts.
* Stop engine before repair.
* Use guards and shields.
* Avoid operating machines under alcohol or fatigue.
* Use gloves, shoes, goggles, and mask where needed.
* Keep first-aid kit available.
* Do not overload machines.

---

# Sprayer Safety

Sprayers require special safety care.

## Recommendations

* Use mask, gloves, goggles, and protective clothing.
* Do not spray against wind.
* Use correct nozzle.
* Avoid leakage.
* Wash equipment after spraying.
* Follow pesticide label instructions.
* Keep pesticide away from children and animals.
