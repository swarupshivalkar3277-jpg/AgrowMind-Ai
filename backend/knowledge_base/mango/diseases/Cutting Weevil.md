# Cutting Weevil in Mango

## Overview

Cutting weevil is an important insect pest of mango that damages tender shoots, leaves, and young branches. Adult weevils feed on plant tissues and may cut or girdle tender shoots, resulting in wilting and drying of affected portions. Severe infestations can reduce vegetative growth and affect fruit production.

## Scientific Classification

* Common Name: Cutting Weevil
* Scientific Name: *Deporaus marginatus* (commonly associated with mango cutting damage in some regions)
* Pest Type: Beetle (Weevil)
* Affected Crop: Mango (*Mangifera indica*)

## Importance

The pest primarily affects young mango plants and newly emerging shoots. Continuous feeding damage weakens plant growth and can reduce orchard productivity.

## Identification

### Adult Weevil

* Small to medium-sized beetle.
* Brown to reddish-brown coloration.
* Possesses a characteristic elongated snout.
* Active during warm and humid periods.

### Larvae

* Creamy white, legless grubs.
* Found within damaged plant tissues or protected sites.
* Feed on plant material during development.

## Symptoms of Damage

### On Leaves

* Irregular feeding holes on leaves.
* Leaf margins cut or damaged.
* Reduced leaf area available for photosynthesis.

### On Shoots

* Tender shoots cut or girdled.
* Wilting of affected shoots.
* Drying of young branches.

### On Young Plants

* Reduced vegetative growth.
* Weak plant development.
* Delayed canopy formation.

## Favorable Conditions

* Warm temperatures.
* High humidity.
* Dense orchard vegetation.
* Presence of unmanaged weeds.
* Poor orchard sanitation.

## Life Cycle

1. Adult females lay eggs on suitable plant tissues.
2. Eggs hatch into larvae.
3. Larvae feed and develop within protected locations.
4. Pupation occurs in sheltered areas.
5. Adults emerge and begin feeding on young shoots and leaves.

## Economic Impact

* Reduced shoot growth.
* Loss of young branches.
* Delayed canopy development.
* Lower fruit production potential.
* Increased management costs.

## Identification Features

* Cut or girdled tender shoots.
* Wilting young branches.
* Presence of adult weevils on foliage.
* Feeding notches on leaf margins.

## Integrated Pest Management (IPM)

### Cultural Practices

* Maintain orchard cleanliness.
* Remove weeds and alternate host plants.
* Collect and destroy heavily damaged shoots.
* Conduct regular orchard monitoring.

### Mechanical Control

* Handpick adult weevils where infestation is low.
* Destroy infested plant material.
* Remove severely damaged shoots.

### Biological Control

* Encourage natural predators such as birds and beneficial insects.
* Promote biodiversity within the orchard ecosystem.

### Chemical Control

Where pest populations exceed economic thresholds:

* Neem-based formulations as recommended.
* Approved insecticides containing suitable active ingredients recommended by local agricultural authorities.

Always follow label instructions and local agricultural recommendations.

## Prevention

* Monitor orchards regularly during active growth periods.
* Maintain proper orchard sanitation.
* Remove infested shoots promptly.
* Encourage natural enemies.
* Avoid excessive vegetation growth around trees.

## Monitoring Checklist

* Inspect tender shoots weekly.
* Look for cut or wilted branches.
* Examine leaves for feeding damage.
* Monitor adult weevil populations.
* Record infestation levels for timely intervention.

## Best Management Practices

* Integrate cultural, biological, and chemical controls.
* Maintain healthy tree growth through balanced nutrition.
* Conduct regular pruning and sanitation.
* Monitor young orchards carefully, as they are more susceptible.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Why are young mango shoots drying suddenly?

Cutting weevil damage can cause girdling or cutting of tender shoots, leading to wilting and drying.

### How can cutting weevil be controlled?

Regular monitoring, sanitation, removal of damaged shoots, and recommended pest management practices help control infestations.

### Which trees are most vulnerable?

Young mango trees and actively growing shoots are generally most susceptible.
