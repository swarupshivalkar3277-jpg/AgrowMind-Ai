# Gall Midge in Mango

## Overview

Gall midge is an important insect pest of mango that attacks leaves, flower panicles, and developing fruits. The larvae feed within plant tissues, causing gall formation, tissue distortion, flower drop, and reduced fruit set. Severe infestations can significantly affect mango yield and orchard productivity.

## Scientific Classification

* Common Name: Mango Gall Midge
* Scientific Name: *Procontarinia matteiana* and related species
* Pest Type: Dipteran Insect (Midge)
* Affected Crop: Mango (*Mangifera indica*)

## Importance

Gall midge is particularly damaging during flowering and fruit-setting stages. Infestation can result in substantial losses due to flower destruction and poor fruit development.

## Identification

### Adult

* Small mosquito-like fly.
* Delicate body structure.
* Short lifespan.
* Active mainly during flowering periods.

### Larvae

* Tiny, legless maggots.
* Creamy white to pale yellow.
* Feed inside plant tissues.

## Symptoms

### On Leaves

* Small galls or swellings on leaves.
* Distorted leaf growth.
* Curling and deformation.
* Premature leaf drop in severe infestations.

### On Flower Panicles

* Small lesions and swelling on flower stalks.
* Drying of flowers.
* Flower drop before fruit set.
* Reduced pollination success.

### On Fruits

* Scarring and deformities.
* Premature fruit drop.
* Reduced fruit quality.

## Favorable Conditions

* Warm temperatures.
* High humidity.
* Dense tree canopy.
* Poor orchard sanitation.
* Presence of infested plant debris.

## Life Cycle

1. Adult females lay eggs on young tissues.
2. Eggs hatch into larvae.
3. Larvae feed internally within leaves, flowers, or fruits.
4. Pupation occurs in soil or plant debris.
5. Adults emerge and repeat the cycle.

## Economic Impact

* Reduced flowering.
* Poor fruit set.
* Premature fruit drop.
* Lower fruit quality.
* Yield reduction.

## Identification Features

* Leaf galls or swellings.
* Flower drying and drop.
* Tiny maggots inside affected tissues.
* Premature dropping of young fruits.

## Integrated Pest Management (IPM)

### Cultural Practices

* Remove and destroy infested plant parts.
* Maintain orchard sanitation.
* Collect fallen flowers and fruits.
* Prune overcrowded branches.

### Mechanical Control

* Remove affected panicles during early infestation.
* Destroy heavily infested tissues.

### Biological Control

* Encourage natural parasitoids and predators.
* Preserve beneficial insect populations.

### Chemical Control

Apply insecticides only when necessary and according to local agricultural recommendations.

Commonly recommended options may include:

* Neem-based formulations.
* Approved systemic insecticides recommended by agricultural extension services.

Always follow label instructions and local regulations.

## Prevention

* Monitor orchards during flowering season.
* Maintain good field sanitation.
* Avoid excessive canopy density.
* Remove infested material promptly.

## Monitoring Checklist

* Inspect flower panicles weekly.
* Check young leaves for galls.
* Observe premature flower or fruit drop.
* Monitor pest incidence during flowering.

## Best Management Practices

* Combine cultural and biological methods.
* Maintain orchard hygiene.
* Conduct regular inspections.
* Take action during early infestation stages.
* Follow integrated pest management practices.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Why are mango flowers drying and dropping?

Gall midge larvae feeding inside flower tissues can cause flower drying and drop.

### What are the small swellings on mango leaves?

Leaf galls caused by gall midge infestation are a common symptom.

### When is gall midge most damaging?

The pest is most damaging during flowering and early fruit development.
