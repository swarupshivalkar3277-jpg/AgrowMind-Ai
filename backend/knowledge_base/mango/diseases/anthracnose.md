# Anthracnose Disease in Mango

## Overview

Anthracnose is one of the most destructive fungal diseases of mango. It is caused by the fungus *Colletotrichum gloeosporioides*. The disease affects leaves, flowers, twigs, and fruits, resulting in reduced yield and poor fruit quality. Anthracnose is especially severe during periods of high humidity, rainfall, and warm temperatures.

## Scientific Classification

* Disease Name: Anthracnose
* Causal Organism: *Colletotrichum gloeosporioides*
* Disease Type: Fungal Disease
* Affected Crop: Mango (*Mangifera indica*)

## Symptoms

### On Leaves

* Small dark brown to black spots appear on leaves.
* Spots enlarge and merge, forming irregular dead areas.
* Severe infection causes premature leaf fall.

### On Flowers

* Black lesions develop on flower panicles.
* Flower drying and blossom blight occur.
* Poor fruit set due to flower damage.

### On Fruits

* Small black circular spots appear on immature fruits.
* Spots enlarge and become sunken.
* Infected fruits rot during storage and transportation.
* Pinkish-orange fungal spores may develop under humid conditions.

### On Twigs

* Dieback of young shoots and twigs.
* Dark necrotic lesions on branches.

## Favorable Conditions

* Relative humidity above 90%.
* Frequent rainfall.
* Temperature between 24°C and 30°C.
* Dense canopy with poor air circulation.
* Prolonged wetness on leaves and fruits.

## Disease Cycle

1. Fungus survives on infected plant debris and twigs.
2. Spores spread through rain splash and wind.
3. Infection occurs on young leaves, flowers, and fruits.
4. Disease develops rapidly under humid conditions.
5. New spores are produced and spread further.

## Economic Importance

* Significant reduction in fruit yield.
* Poor fruit quality and market value.
* High post-harvest losses.
* Reduced export quality fruits.

## Integrated Disease Management

### Cultural Practices

* Prune diseased twigs regularly.
* Remove infected fruits and plant debris.
* Maintain proper spacing between trees.
* Improve air circulation through canopy management.
* Avoid overhead irrigation.

### Biological Control

* Apply *Trichoderma harzianum* around the root zone.
* Use beneficial microbial formulations recommended by agricultural experts.

### Chemical Control

#### Preventive Sprays

* Copper Oxychloride 0.3%
* Bordeaux Mixture 1%

#### Fungicides

* Mancozeb 75 WP @ 2.5 g/L water
* Carbendazim 50 WP @ 1 g/L water
* Propiconazole @ recommended label dose
* Azoxystrobin @ recommended label dose

### Flowering Stage Protection

* Spray fungicides before flowering.
* Repeat at 10–15 day intervals during humid weather.

## Prevention

* Use healthy planting material.
* Maintain orchard sanitation.
* Conduct annual pruning.
* Monitor orchards during monsoon season.
* Follow integrated disease management practices.

## Monitoring Checklist

* Check leaves for dark lesions.
* Inspect flower panicles weekly.
* Examine developing fruits for black spots.
* Record disease incidence and severity.

## Key Identification Features

* Black spots on leaves and fruits.
* Blossom blight during flowering.
* Sunken lesions on fruits.
* Fruit rot during storage.
