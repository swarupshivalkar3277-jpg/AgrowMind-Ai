# Powdery Mildew Disease in Mango

## Overview

Powdery mildew is one of the most economically important diseases of mango, especially during the flowering season. The disease is caused by the fungus *Oidium mangiferae* and primarily affects flowers, young fruits, leaves, and tender shoots. Severe infections can result in flower drop, poor fruit set, and substantial yield losses.

## Scientific Classification

* Disease Name: Powdery Mildew
* Causal Organism: *Oidium mangiferae*
* Disease Type: Fungal Disease
* Affected Crop: Mango (*Mangifera indica*)

## Importance

Powdery mildew is considered a major constraint in mango production worldwide. If favorable environmental conditions persist during flowering, crop losses can exceed 50% due to reduced fruit set and premature fruit drop.

## Symptoms

### On Flower Panicles

* White or grayish powdery growth on flowers.
* Drying and browning of flower clusters.
* Flower drop before fruit set.
* Reduced pollination and fertilization.

### On Leaves

* White powder-like fungal growth on young leaves.
* Curling and distortion of leaves.
* Premature leaf fall in severe infections.

### On Young Fruits

* White fungal coating on fruit surface.
* Fruit discoloration.
* Cracking and deformation.
* Premature fruit drop.

### On Tender Shoots

* Powdery fungal growth on young shoots.
* Stunted growth and drying of infected shoots.

## Favorable Conditions

The disease develops rapidly under:

* Temperature between 20°C and 30°C.
* High humidity during nights and mornings.
* Cloudy weather.
* Dense tree canopy.
* Poor air circulation.
* Dry weather followed by humid conditions.

## Disease Cycle

1. The fungus survives on infected plant parts.
2. Spores spread through wind.
3. Infection occurs on flowers, leaves, and young fruits.
4. Fungal growth develops on plant surfaces.
5. New spores are produced and spread to nearby tissues.

## Economic Impact

* Significant reduction in fruit set.
* Premature flower drop.
* Reduced fruit quality.
* Lower marketable yield.
* Increased orchard management costs.

## Identification Features

* White powdery fungal growth on flowers and leaves.
* Drying of flower panicles.
* Fruit drop after flowering.
* Distorted young fruits and leaves.

## Integrated Disease Management

### Cultural Practices

* Prune trees regularly to improve airflow.
* Remove infected plant parts.
* Avoid overcrowding in orchards.
* Maintain orchard sanitation.
* Monitor trees closely during flowering season.

### Nutrient Management

* Apply balanced fertilizers.
* Avoid excessive nitrogen application.
* Ensure adequate micronutrient supply.

### Biological Management

* Encourage beneficial microorganisms in orchard soil.
* Use approved biological control agents where available.

## Chemical Management

### Preventive Sprays

Apply fungicides before flowering and during panicle emergence.

#### Recommended Fungicides

* Wettable Sulphur @ 2–3 g/L water.
* Carbendazim @ 1 g/L water.
* Hexaconazole @ recommended label dose.
* Propiconazole @ recommended label dose.
* Tridemorph @ recommended label dose.

### Spray Schedule

1. First spray at flower bud initiation.
2. Second spray during panicle emergence.
3. Third spray after fruit set if disease pressure continues.

Follow local agricultural recommendations and label instructions.

## Prevention

* Use healthy planting material.
* Maintain proper tree spacing.
* Conduct annual pruning.
* Monitor orchards during flowering.
* Follow integrated disease management practices.

## Monitoring Checklist

* Inspect flower panicles weekly.
* Check young fruits for fungal growth.
* Observe leaves for white powdery patches.
* Record disease severity and affected trees.

## Best Management Practices

* Begin disease monitoring before flowering starts.
* Improve canopy ventilation.
* Remove infected tissues promptly.
* Apply preventive fungicide sprays during susceptible stages.
* Maintain balanced nutrition and irrigation.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Why are mango flowers turning white and drying?

White powdery fungal growth on flowers is a characteristic symptom of powdery mildew infection.

### Can powdery mildew affect fruit production?

Yes. Severe infections reduce pollination, fruit set, and can cause heavy fruit drop.

### Which stage is most vulnerable?

Flowering and early fruit development stages are the most susceptible.
