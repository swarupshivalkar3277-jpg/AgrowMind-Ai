# Sooty Mould Disease in Mango

## Overview

Sooty mould is a fungal disease characterized by the development of a black, soot-like coating on leaves, twigs, flowers, and fruits of mango trees. Unlike many plant diseases, the fungus does not directly infect plant tissues. Instead, it grows on the sugary honeydew secretions produced by sap-sucking insects such as mango hoppers, mealybugs, aphids, whiteflies, and scale insects.

Severe infestations reduce photosynthesis, weaken tree growth, lower fruit quality, and decrease overall yield.

## Scientific Classification

- Disease Name: Sooty Mould
- Associated Fungi: Species of *Capnodium*, *Meliola*, and related fungi
- Disease Type: Fungal Growth on Honeydew Deposits
- Affected Crop: Mango (*Mangifera indica*)

## Importance

Sooty mould is commonly associated with insect infestations. Although the fungus itself does not penetrate plant tissues, heavy growth blocks sunlight and interferes with normal plant functions.

## Symptoms

### On Leaves

- Black soot-like coating on leaf surfaces.
- Reduced leaf brightness and vigor.
- Reduced photosynthetic activity.
- Premature leaf aging under severe infestation.

### On Twigs and Branches

- Black fungal growth covering twigs and stems.
- Sticky honeydew deposits visible before fungal growth develops.

### On Flowers

- Black fungal coating on flower panicles.
- Reduced pollination efficiency.
- Poor fruit set in severe cases.

### On Fruits

- Black deposits on fruit surface.
- Poor fruit appearance and market value.
- Reduced export quality.

## Causes

Sooty mould develops due to the presence of honeydew secreted by:

- Mango Hopper (*Idioscopus spp.*)
- Mealy Bugs
- Aphids
- Whiteflies
- Scale Insects

The fungus uses the honeydew as a food source and proliferates rapidly under humid conditions.

## Favorable Conditions

- High humidity.
- Warm temperatures.
- Dense tree canopy.
- Heavy infestation of sap-sucking insects.
- Poor orchard sanitation.
- Limited air circulation.

## Disease Cycle

1. Sap-sucking insects feed on plant sap.
2. Insects excrete sugary honeydew.
3. Fungal spores land on honeydew deposits.
4. Black fungal colonies develop and spread.
5. New spores disperse through wind and rain.

## Economic Impact

- Reduced photosynthesis.
- Poor flowering and fruit set.
- Lower fruit quality.
- Reduced marketability of fruits.
- Increased orchard management costs.

## Identification Features

- Black soot-like coating on leaves and fruits.
- Presence of sticky honeydew.
- Associated insect infestation.
- Fungal growth can often be wiped off the surface.

## Integrated Disease Management

### Cultural Practices

- Prune overcrowded branches.
- Improve air circulation within the canopy.
- Remove heavily infested plant parts.
- Maintain orchard sanitation.

### Insect Management

Control the insect pests responsible for honeydew production.

Common target pests include:

- Mango Hopper
- Mealy Bug
- Aphids
- Whiteflies
- Scale Insects

### Biological Control

Encourage natural predators such as:

- Ladybird beetles
- Lacewings
- Parasitoid wasps

### Mechanical Management

- Wash affected leaves and fruits using clean water where practical.
- Remove heavily infested shoots.

## Chemical Management

Control of sap-sucking insects is the primary strategy.

Recommended insecticides may include:

- Imidacloprid (as per local recommendations)
- Thiamethoxam (as per label instructions)
- Neem-based formulations
- Other approved insecticides recommended by local agricultural authorities

Always follow local agricultural guidelines and product label directions.

## Prevention

- Monitor orchards regularly for insect infestations.
- Maintain proper tree spacing.
- Conduct annual pruning.
- Encourage beneficial insects.
- Avoid excessive nitrogen fertilization that promotes pest outbreaks.

## Monitoring Checklist

- Check leaves for black fungal deposits.
- Inspect for honeydew accumulation.
- Monitor hopper and mealybug populations.
- Examine flowers and fruits during flowering and fruiting stages.

## Best Management Practices

- Control insect pests early.
- Maintain orchard hygiene.
- Improve canopy ventilation.
- Combine cultural, biological, and chemical control measures.
- Monitor regularly throughout the growing season.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is sooty mould a serious disease?

The fungus itself does not directly infect plant tissues, but heavy growth can significantly reduce photosynthesis and fruit quality.

### What causes black coating on mango leaves?

The black coating is usually sooty mould growing on honeydew secreted by sap-sucking insects.

### How can sooty mould be controlled?

The most effective control method is managing the insect pests that produce honeydew.
