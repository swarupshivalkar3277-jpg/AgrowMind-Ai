# Famous Organic Composts Used in Indian Agriculture

## Overview

Organic composts are natural fertilizers made from decomposed plant, animal, and microbial materials. They improve soil fertility, structure, water retention, and microbial activity.

---

# 🌿 1. Farmyard Manure (FYM)

## Description
Traditional compost made from cattle dung, urine, straw, and farm waste.

## Nutrient Content
- Low but balanced NPK
- Rich in organic carbon

## Benefits
- Improves soil structure
- Increases microbial activity
- Long-term soil fertility

---

# 🌿 2. Vermicompost

## Description
Produced using earthworms (Eisenia fetida).

## Features
- High nutrient availability
- Fast acting organic fertilizer

## Benefits
- Improves plant growth
- Increases soil aeration
- Enhances root development

---

# 🌿 3. Green Manure

## Description
Crops grown and ploughed into soil (e.g., dhaincha, sunhemp).

## Benefits
- Fixes nitrogen
- Improves soil organic matter
- Reduces chemical fertilizer need

---

# 🌿 4. Compost (General Yard Compost)

## Description
Decomposed kitchen waste, leaves, crop residues.

## Benefits
- Eco-friendly recycling
- Improves soil fertility
- Cheap and widely available

---

# 🌿 5. Neem Compost

## Description
Compost enriched with neem leaves and cake.

## Benefits
- Pest-repelling properties
- Improves soil health
- Reduces nematodes

---

# 🌿 6. Coir Compost

## Description
Made from coconut husk waste.

## Benefits
- Improves water retention
- Good for sandy soils
- Long-lasting organic matter

---

# 🌿 7. Press Mud Compost

## Description
By-product of sugar industry.

## Benefits
- Rich in organic carbon
- Good for sugarcane and fruit crops

---

# 🌿 8. Panchagavya Compost (Bio-enhancer)

## Description
Made from cow products (milk, dung, urine, curd, ghee).

## Benefits
- Boosts plant immunity
- Improves microbial activity
- Enhances yield

---

# 🌱 Summary

Best compost combinations:

- FYM + Vermicompost (universal best)
- Green manure + FYM (soil regeneration)
- Neem compost (pest-prone areas)

---

# ⚠️ Important Note

Always apply compost:
- Before planting
- During soil preparation
- With proper decomposition (not fresh raw waste)
