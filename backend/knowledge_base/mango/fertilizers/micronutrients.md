# Micronutrient Management in Mango

## Overview

Micronutrients play a crucial role in the growth, flowering, fruit setting, yield, and fruit quality of mango trees. Although required in small quantities, deficiencies can cause significant reductions in productivity and increase susceptibility to pests, diseases, and environmental stress.

Proper micronutrient management ensures healthy vegetative growth, improved flowering, better fruit retention, and higher-quality fruits.

## Important Micronutrients for Mango

### Zinc (Zn)

#### Functions

* Promotes shoot growth.
* Essential for enzyme activity.
* Improves flowering and fruit set.
* Enhances leaf development.

#### Deficiency Symptoms

* Small and narrow leaves.
* Rosetting of shoots.
* Short internodes.
* Reduced flowering.
* Poor fruit production.

#### Management

* Zinc Sulphate foliar spray @ 0.5%.
* Soil application based on soil test recommendations.

---

### Boron (B)

#### Functions

* Essential for pollen germination.
* Improves fruit set.
* Supports cell wall formation.
* Enhances fruit quality.

#### Deficiency Symptoms

* Poor flowering.
* Reduced fruit set.
* Fruit cracking.
* Deformed fruits.
* Premature fruit drop.

#### Management

* Borax or Solubor foliar spray @ 0.1–0.2%.
* Soil application where deficiencies are confirmed.

---

### Iron (Fe)

#### Functions

* Chlorophyll formation.
* Photosynthesis.
* Energy production.

#### Deficiency Symptoms

* Yellowing between leaf veins.
* Young leaves become pale yellow.
* Reduced plant vigor.

#### Management

* Ferrous Sulphate application.
* Iron chelate foliar sprays.
* Improve soil drainage and pH management.

---

### Manganese (Mn)

#### Functions

* Photosynthesis.
* Nitrogen metabolism.
* Enzyme activation.

#### Deficiency Symptoms

* Interveinal chlorosis.
* Reduced leaf growth.
* Poor plant development.

#### Management

* Manganese Sulphate foliar spray.
* Soil correction based on test results.

---

### Copper (Cu)

#### Functions

* Enzyme activity.
* Disease resistance.
* Lignin formation.

#### Deficiency Symptoms

* Weak shoots.
* Leaf curling.
* Dieback of young twigs.

#### Management

* Copper-based micronutrient mixtures.
* Balanced fertilizer programs.

---

### Magnesium (Mg)

#### Functions

* Central component of chlorophyll.
* Photosynthesis.
* Carbohydrate transport.

#### Deficiency Symptoms

* Yellowing between leaf veins on older leaves.
* Premature leaf drop.
* Reduced growth.

#### Management

* Magnesium Sulphate (Epsom Salt) application.
* Foliar sprays where necessary.

---

## Common Micronutrient Deficiency Symptoms in Mango

| Nutrient  | Major Symptoms                 |
| --------- | ------------------------------ |
| Zinc      | Small leaves, rosetting        |
| Boron     | Poor fruit set, fruit cracking |
| Iron      | Yellow young leaves            |
| Manganese | Interveinal chlorosis          |
| Copper    | Dieback, weak shoots           |
| Magnesium | Yellowing of older leaves      |

## Micronutrient Spray Schedule

### Pre-Flowering Stage

Apply:

* Zinc Sulphate 0.5%
* Boron 0.1%

Benefits:

* Better flowering.
* Improved fruit set.

### Fruit Development Stage

Apply:

* Micronutrient mixture recommended by local agricultural authorities.

Benefits:

* Improved fruit size.
* Better fruit quality.

### Post-Harvest Stage

Apply:

* Balanced micronutrient mixture.

Benefits:

* Tree recovery.
* Preparation for next season.

## Integrated Nutrient Management

For sustainable production:

* Conduct soil testing every 2–3 years.
* Conduct leaf nutrient analysis where available.
* Apply organic manures regularly.
* Use micronutrients only when needed.
* Maintain proper irrigation.

## Best Management Practices

* Follow soil-test-based fertilization.
* Avoid excessive fertilizer application.
* Combine organic and inorganic nutrient sources.
* Monitor orchards for deficiency symptoms.
* Apply foliar sprays during cooler parts of the day.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Why are mango leaves becoming yellow?

Yellowing may indicate deficiencies of iron, magnesium, manganese, or zinc depending on the pattern of discoloration.

### Which micronutrient improves fruit set?

Boron is particularly important for pollen germination, flowering, and fruit set.

### Can micronutrient sprays increase yield?

Yes. Correcting deficiencies can improve flowering, fruit retention, fruit quality, and overall yield.

### How often should micronutrients be applied?

Application frequency depends on soil test results, deficiency symptoms, and local agricultural recommendations.
