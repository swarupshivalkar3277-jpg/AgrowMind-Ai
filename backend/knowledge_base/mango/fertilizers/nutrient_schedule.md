# Mango Nutrient Schedule

## Overview

Proper nutrient management is essential for achieving healthy vegetative growth, regular flowering, improved fruit set, high yields, and superior fruit quality in mango orchards. Nutrient requirements vary according to tree age, soil fertility, climate, and production goals.

This schedule provides general fertilizer recommendations for mango cultivation. Soil testing should always be used to fine-tune nutrient applications.

---

# Importance of Nutrient Management

Balanced nutrition helps:

* Promote healthy canopy development.
* Increase flowering intensity.
* Improve fruit retention.
* Enhance fruit size and quality.
* Improve resistance to pests and diseases.
* Increase orchard productivity and longevity.

---

# Essential Nutrients for Mango

## Macronutrients

### Nitrogen (N)

Functions:

* Promotes vegetative growth.
* Enhances leaf development.
* Supports canopy formation.

### Phosphorus (P)

Functions:

* Root development.
* Flower initiation.
* Energy transfer within plants.

### Potassium (K)

Functions:

* Fruit development.
* Fruit quality improvement.
* Disease tolerance.
* Water regulation.

# Recommended Fertilizer Schedule by Tree Age

## 1–3 Year Old Trees

### Annual Requirement per Tree

* Farmyard Manure (FYM): 10–20 kg
* Nitrogen (N): 100–200 g
* Phosphorus (P₂O₅): 50–100 g
* Potassium (K₂O): 100–200 g

### Application Time

* Half dose before monsoon.
* Remaining half after monsoon.

---

## 4–6 Year Old Trees

### Annual Requirement per Tree

* FYM: 25–40 kg
* Nitrogen: 300–500 g
* Phosphorus: 150–250 g
* Potassium: 300–500 g

### Application Time

* Apply before monsoon.
* Split nitrogen into two applications.

---

## 7–10 Year Old Trees

### Annual Requirement per Tree

* FYM: 40–60 kg
* Nitrogen: 600–800 g
* Phosphorus: 300–400 g
* Potassium: 600–800 g

### Application Time

* Apply organic manure before monsoon.
* Split nitrogen applications.

---

## Mature Bearing Trees (10+ Years)

### Annual Requirement per Tree

* FYM: 50–100 kg
* Nitrogen: 1.0–1.5 kg
* Phosphorus: 0.5–0.75 kg
* Potassium: 1.0–1.5 kg

### Application Time

* After harvest.
* Before flowering.
* During fruit development as required.

---

# Month-Wise Nutrient Schedule

## June–July (Monsoon)

Apply:

* Farmyard manure.
* Compost.
* Full phosphorus dose.
* Full potassium dose.
* Half nitrogen dose.

Benefits:

* Root growth.
* Vegetative development.
* Soil fertility improvement.

---

## September–October

Apply:

* Remaining nitrogen dose.
* Micronutrient correction if required.

Benefits:

* Strong shoot growth.
* Flower bud preparation.

---

## November–December

Apply:

* Zinc spray (0.5%).
* Boron spray (0.1%).

Benefits:

* Flower initiation.
* Improved fruit set.

---

## January–February (Flowering)

Apply:

* Micronutrient sprays if deficiency symptoms appear.

Benefits:

* Better pollination.
* Improved fruit retention.

---

## March–May (Fruit Development)

Apply:

* Potassium-rich nutrition.
* Micronutrient sprays as required.

Benefits:

* Fruit enlargement.
* Better fruit quality.

---

# Fertigation Schedule for Drip Irrigation

Where drip irrigation is available:

### Vegetative Stage

Apply:

* Water-soluble NPK fertilizers.
* Split applications every 7–15 days.

### Flowering Stage

Apply:

* Phosphorus-rich fertilizers.
* Micronutrients.

### Fruit Development Stage

Apply:

* Potassium-rich fertilizers.

Benefits:

* Higher fertilizer efficiency.
* Reduced nutrient losses.
* Better yield response.

# Deficiency Monitoring

Regularly inspect trees for:

* Yellow leaves.
* Small leaves.
* Fruit cracking.
* Poor flowering.
* Excessive fruit drop.
* Weak shoot growth.

Conduct soil and leaf analysis where possible.

---

# Best Management Practices

* Conduct soil testing every 2–3 years.
* Apply fertilizers based on tree age.
* Split nitrogen applications.
* Incorporate organic manures annually.
* Use drip fertigation where available.
* Correct micronutrient deficiencies promptly.
* Maintain proper irrigation.

# Frequently Asked Questions

## When should fertilizers be applied to mango trees?

Major fertilizer applications are generally made before and during the monsoon season, with supplemental applications based on tree growth and fruiting stage.

## Which nutrient improves flowering?

Phosphorus, potassium, zinc, and boron play important roles in flowering and fruit set.

## Which nutrient improves fruit quality?

Potassium is particularly important for fruit size, sweetness, color, and shelf life.

## Is soil testing necessary?

Yes. Soil testing helps determine nutrient deficiencies and prevents excessive fertilizer application.
