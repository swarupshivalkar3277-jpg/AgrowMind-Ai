# Organic Fertilization in Mango

## Overview

Organic fertilization is an environmentally sustainable approach to mango cultivation that improves soil fertility, enhances microbial activity, increases water-holding capacity, and supports long-term orchard productivity. Organic nutrient sources provide essential macro and micronutrients while improving overall soil health.

Organic fertilization is particularly important for sustainable mango production, residue-free farming, and improving fruit quality.

---

# Benefits of Organic Fertilization

Organic nutrient management helps:

* Improve soil structure.
* Increase soil organic matter.
* Enhance beneficial microbial populations.
* Improve nutrient availability.
* Increase moisture retention.
* Reduce soil degradation.
* Improve fruit quality and shelf life.
* Promote sustainable orchard production.

---

# Major Organic Nutrient Sources

## Farmyard Manure (FYM)

### Description

Farmyard manure consists of decomposed cattle dung, urine, bedding material, and farm waste.

### Benefits

* Improves soil fertility.
* Enhances water retention.
* Supplies nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium.
* Promotes microbial activity.

### Recommended Application

| Tree Age     | FYM per Tree |
| ------------ | ------------ |
| 1–3 Years    | 10–20 kg     |
| 4–6 Years    | 25–40 kg     |
| 7–10 Years   | 40–60 kg     |
| Mature Trees | 50–100 kg    |

### Application Time

* Before monsoon season.
* Incorporate into soil around the tree basin.

---

## Vermicompost

### Description

Vermicompost is produced through the decomposition of organic materials by earthworms.

### Benefits

* Rich in plant nutrients.
* Improves soil aeration.
* Enhances root growth.
* Increases microbial activity.

### Recommended Application

* Young trees: 2–5 kg/tree/year.
* Mature trees: 10–20 kg/tree/year.

---

## Compost

### Description

Compost is decomposed organic matter derived from plant residues, crop waste, and kitchen waste.

### Benefits

* Improves soil texture.
* Supplies nutrients gradually.
* Supports beneficial microorganisms.

### Recommended Application

* 10–30 kg/tree annually depending on tree age and soil condition.

---

## Green Manure

### Description

Green manure crops are grown and incorporated into soil before flowering.

Common crops:

* Sunhemp
* Dhaincha
* Cowpea

### Benefits

* Adds organic matter.
* Improves nitrogen availability.
* Enhances soil structure.

---

## Poultry Manure

### Description

Poultry manure is a concentrated organic nutrient source rich in nitrogen and phosphorus.

### Benefits

* Rapid nutrient availability.
* Improved vegetative growth.

### Precautions

* Apply only well-decomposed manure.
* Avoid excessive application.

Benefits:

* Fixes atmospheric nitrogen.
* Improves nutrient uptake.
* Enhances root development.

---

## Azospirillum

Benefits:

* Supports root growth.
* Improves nitrogen availability.

## Phosphate Solubilizing Bacteria (PSB)

Benefits:

* Improves phosphorus availability.

## Mycorrhiza

Benefits:

* Improves nutrient absorption.

# Organic Fertilizer Schedule

## June–July (Monsoon)

Apply:

* Farmyard manure.
* Compost.
* Vermicompost.
* Biofertilizers.

Benefits:

* Soil enrichment.
* Root development.

---

## September–October

Apply:

* Additional compost if needed.
* Micronutrient correction through organic sources.

Benefits:

* Supports flower bud development.

---

## November–December

Apply:

* Organic micronutrient formulations.
* Biofertilizer inoculation if required.

Benefits:

* Improved flowering.

---

## Fruit Development Stage

Apply:

* Vermicompost.
* Liquid organic formulations.

Benefits:

* Better fruit growth and quality.

# Integrated Organic Nutrient Management

For best results combine:

* Farmyard manure.
* Vermicompost.
* Compost.
* Biofertilizers.
* Green manure crops.
* Mulching.

This integrated approach improves nutrient availability and orchard sustainability.

# Mulching in Organic Mango Orchards

* Dry leaves.
* Crop residues.

* Straw.

* Moisture conservation.
* Weed suppression.

# Best Management Practices

* Apply well-decomposed organic materials only.
* Conduct soil testing regularly.
* Maintain adequate soil moisture.
* Use biofertilizers annually.
* Incorporate organic matter before monsoon.
* Combine organic and biological nutrient sources.

# Frequently Asked Questions

## Can mango be grown completely organically?

Yes. Mango can be cultivated organically using approved organic fertilizers, biofertilizers, and integrated pest management practices.

## Which organic fertilizer is best for mango?

Farmyard manure, vermicompost, and compost are among the most widely recommended organic nutrient sources.

## When should organic fertilizers be applied?

The majority of organic manure should be applied before the monsoon season for maximum benefit.

## Do organic fertilizers improve fruit quality?

Yes. Organic nutrient management often improves fruit taste, color, shelf life, and overall quality.
