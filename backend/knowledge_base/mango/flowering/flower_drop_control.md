# Flower Drop Control in Mango

## Overview

Flower drop is one of the most common causes of reduced fruit yield in mango orchards. Mango trees naturally shed a portion of their flowers, but excessive flower drop can significantly reduce fruit set and overall production.

Flower drop may occur due to environmental stress, nutrient deficiencies, poor pollination, pest attacks, diseases, improper irrigation, or unfavorable weather conditions.

Effective flower drop management requires a combination of cultural, nutritional, irrigation, pest, and disease management practices.

---

# Importance of Flower Retention

Good flower retention helps:

* Increase fruit set.
* Improve yield potential.
* Enhance fruit quality.
* Improve orchard profitability.
* Reduce premature fruit loss.

---

# Causes of Flower Drop in Mango

## Environmental Factors

### Temperature Extremes

High temperatures may:

* Dry flower tissues.
* Reduce pollen viability.
* Cause flower abortion.

Low temperatures may:

* Delay pollination.
* Reduce fruit set.

---

### Strong Winds

Effects:

* Physical damage to flower panicles.
* Loss of pollen.
* Increased flower shedding.

---

### Rainfall During Flowering

Problems:

* Washes away pollen grains.
* Reduces pollinator activity.
* Encourages fungal diseases.

---

## Nutritional Deficiencies

### Boron Deficiency

Symptoms:

* Poor pollen germination.
* Low fruit set.
* Increased flower drop.

### Zinc Deficiency

Symptoms:

* Weak flowering.
* Reduced flower development.

### Potassium Deficiency

Symptoms:

* Poor flower retention.
* Reduced fruit development.

---

## Water Stress

### Drought Stress

Can cause:

* Wilting flowers.
* Premature flower drop.

### Excess Irrigation

May result in:

* Root stress.
* Nutrient imbalance.
* Reduced flowering efficiency.

---

## Poor Pollination

Causes:

* Lack of pollinating insects.
* Unfavorable weather.
* Excess pesticide use during flowering.

Effects:

* Poor fertilization.
* Flower and fruit drop.

---

## Pest Problems

Common pests causing flower loss:

### Mango Hopper

Damage:

* Sap sucking from flowers.
* Flower drying.
* Reduced fruit set.

### Thrips

Damage:

* Injury to flower tissues.
* Flower drop.

### Mealy Bugs

Damage:

* Weakening of flower panicles.

---

## Disease Problems

### Powdery Mildew

Symptoms:

* White powdery growth on flowers.
* Drying of flower panicles.
* Flower drop.

### Anthracnose

Symptoms:

* Black lesions on flowers.
* Blossom blight.
* Flower loss.

# Flower Drop Management

## Nutrient Management

### Boron Spray

Recommended:

* Borax or Solubor @ 0.1–0.2%

Benefits:

* Improved pollen germination.
* Better fruit set.

---

### Zinc Spray

Recommended:

* Zinc Sulphate @ 0.5%

Benefits:

* Improved flowering.
* Better flower retention.

---

### Potassium Nutrition

Benefits:

* Stronger flowers.
* Better fruit retention.

Apply according to soil test recommendations.

# Frequently Asked Questions

## Why are mango flowers falling before fruit formation?

Common causes include poor pollination, nutrient deficiencies, pest infestations, diseases, drought stress, and unfavorable weather conditions.

## Which nutrient helps reduce flower drop?

Boron and zinc are particularly important for flower retention and fruit set.

## Can powdery mildew cause flower drop?

Yes. Powdery mildew can infect flower panicles and cause significant flower loss.

## Does irrigation affect flowering?

Yes. Both drought stress and excessive irrigation can increase flower drop.
