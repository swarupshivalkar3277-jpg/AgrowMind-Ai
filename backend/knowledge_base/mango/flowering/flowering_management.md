# Flowering Management in Mango

## Overview

Flowering is one of the most critical stages in mango production. Proper flowering management directly influences fruit set, fruit retention, yield, and fruit quality. Successful flowering depends on variety, tree age, climate, nutrition, irrigation, pruning, and pest and disease management.

Effective flowering management helps maximize orchard productivity and ensures consistent annual yields.

---

# Importance of Flowering Management

Proper flowering management helps:

- Increase flower production.
- Improve fruit set.
- Reduce flower and fruit drop.
- Enhance fruit quality.
- Improve yield consistency.
- Minimize alternate bearing.

---

# Flowering Season in Mango

Flowering time varies depending on:

- Variety.
- Geographic region.
- Climatic conditions.

### Typical Flowering Periods in India

| Region | Flowering Period |
|----------|------------------|
| South India | November – January |
| Central India | December – February |
| North India | January – March |

# Factors Affecting Flowering

## Tree Age

Young trees:

- Focus mainly on vegetative growth.
- Produce fewer flowers.

Mature trees:

- Flower more consistently.
- Produce higher yields.

---

## Temperature

Optimal temperature:

- 15°C to 25°C during flower initiation.

Effects of high temperature:

- Reduced flower formation.
- Poor pollen viability.

Effects of low temperature:

- Delayed flowering.
- Reduced pollination success.

---

## Water Management

Controlled water stress before flowering can encourage flower initiation.

Excess irrigation before flowering may:

- Promote vegetative growth.
- Delay flowering.

---

## Nutrition

Balanced nutrition is essential.

Important nutrients:

### Nitrogen (N)

- Supports vegetative growth.
- Excess nitrogen may reduce flowering.

### Phosphorus (P)

- Encourages flower initiation.

### Potassium (K)

- Improves flowering and fruit set.

### Zinc (Zn)

- Essential for flower development.

### Boron (B)

- Improves pollen germination and fruit set.

---

# Flowering Induction Practices

## Water Stress Management

In mature orchards:

- Irrigation may be reduced before flowering.
- Mild stress can encourage flower bud formation.

Avoid severe stress that may damage trees.

---

## Pruning

Benefits:

- Improves sunlight penetration.
- Enhances air circulation.
- Encourages healthy flowering shoots.

Recommended pruning:

- Remove dead branches.
- Remove diseased shoots.
- Remove overcrowded growth.

---

## Canopy Management

Proper canopy structure:

- Improves light distribution.
- Enhances flower production.
- Reduces disease incidence.

---

# Nutrient Management for Flowering

## Pre-Flowering Nutrition

Apply:

- Recommended NPK fertilizers.
- Organic manures.
- Micronutrients based on soil tests.

Benefits:

- Better flower initiation.
- Improved panicle development.

---

## Micronutrient Sprays

### Zinc Sulphate

Recommended concentration:

- 0.5%

Benefits:

- Promotes flower development.

### Boron

Recommended concentration:

- 0.1–0.2%

Benefits:

- Improves pollen viability.
- Enhances fruit set.

---

# Irrigation Management During Flowering

### Before Flowering

- Reduce excessive irrigation.
- Avoid waterlogging.

### During Flowering

- Maintain adequate soil moisture.
- Prevent drought stress.

### After Fruit Set

- Resume regular irrigation schedule.

---

# Pollination Management

Mango flowers require effective pollination for fruit set.

## Important Pollinators

- Honey bees.
- Native bees.
- Flies.
- Other beneficial insects.

### Pollinator Protection

- Avoid insecticide sprays during peak flowering.
- Encourage pollinator habitats.

---

# Pest Management During Flowering

## Mango Hopper

Major flowering pest.

Symptoms:

- Sap sucking from flowers.
- Flower drying.
- Reduced fruit set.

Management:

- Orchard sanitation.
- Monitoring.
- Recommended IPM practices.

---

## Thrips

Damage:

- Injury to flower tissues.
- Flower drop.

Management:

- Regular scouting.
- Timely intervention.

---

# Disease Management During Flowering

## Powdery Mildew

Symptoms:

- White powdery growth on flowers.
- Flower drying.

Management:

- Preventive fungicide sprays.
- Proper canopy ventilation.

---

## Anthracnose

Symptoms:

- Flower blight.
- Black lesions.
- Flower drop.

Management:

- Copper-based fungicides.
- Orchard sanitation.

---

# Alternate Bearing Management

Alternate bearing refers to heavy yield in one year followed by low yield in the next.

### Management Practices

- Balanced nutrition.
- Proper pruning.
- Fruit load regulation.
- Regular irrigation.
- Timely pest and disease control.

# Frequently Asked Questions

## Why is my mango tree not flowering?

Possible reasons include excessive nitrogen, young tree age, improper pruning, water stress, nutrient deficiencies, or unsuitable weather conditions.

## Which nutrient promotes flowering in mango?

Phosphorus, potassium, zinc, and boron are particularly important for flowering and fruit set.

## Does pruning improve flowering?

Yes. Proper pruning improves sunlight penetration and stimulates productive flowering shoots.

## How can I improve fruit set after flowering?

Maintain adequate nutrition, encourage pollinators, manage pests and diseases, and ensure proper irrigation.
