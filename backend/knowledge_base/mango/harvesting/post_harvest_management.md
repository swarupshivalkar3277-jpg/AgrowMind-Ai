# Post-Harvest Management of Mango

## Overview

Post-harvest management refers to all operations performed after harvesting mango fruits to maintain quality, reduce losses, extend shelf life, and maximize market value. Proper post-harvest handling is essential because mango is a highly perishable fruit and can suffer significant losses due to poor handling, transportation, storage, and packaging.

Effective post-harvest management helps farmers obtain better prices and ensures high-quality fruits reach consumers.

---

# Objectives of Post-Harvest Management

* Reduce post-harvest losses.
* Maintain fruit quality.
* Increase shelf life.
* Improve marketability.
* Preserve nutritional value.
* Enhance export potential.

---

# Harvesting Practices

## Correct Harvesting Stage

Harvest fruits only when they reach physiological maturity.

Indicators include:

* Full shoulder development.
* Characteristic fruit size.
* Reduced latex flow.
* Variety-specific maturity period.

---

## Harvesting Time

Recommended:

* Early morning.
* Late afternoon.

Avoid:

* Harvesting during intense heat.
* Harvesting during rainfall.

---

## Harvesting Tools

Use:

* Secateurs.
* Fruit harvesters.
* Picking poles with collection bags.

Benefits:

* Reduced fruit damage.
* Better fruit quality.

---

# De-Sapping

## Importance

Freshly harvested mango fruits release latex that can cause:

* Skin burns.
* Black spots.
* Reduced market value.

---

## Procedure

* Keep harvested fruits stem-end downward.
* Allow latex to drain.
* Wash fruits if necessary.

Benefits:

* Cleaner fruit appearance.
* Reduced skin damage.

---

# Cleaning and Washing

## Purpose

Removes:

* Dirt.
* Dust.
* Latex residues.
* Surface contaminants.

---

## Recommended Practices

* Wash with clean water.
* Use food-safe sanitizing solutions if required.
* Dry fruits before packing.

---

# Sorting and Grading

## Sorting

Remove:

* Damaged fruits.
* Diseased fruits.
* Insect-infested fruits.
* Cracked fruits.

---

## Grading

Grade fruits based on:

* Size.
* Weight.
* Shape.
* Color.
* Variety.

Benefits:

* Uniform market lots.
* Better prices.
* Improved consumer acceptance.

---

# Ripening Management

## Natural Ripening

Recommended methods:

* Straw ripening.
* Controlled room ripening.

Advantages:

* Better flavor.
* Uniform ripening.

---

## Safe Ripening Practices

Use:

* Ethylene-based ripening systems.

Avoid:

* Calcium carbide.
* Unsafe chemical ripening agents.

Benefits:

* Food safety.
* Better fruit quality.

---

# Packaging

## Objectives

* Protect fruits from injury.
* Reduce moisture loss.
* Improve transport efficiency.

---

## Packaging Materials

Common options:

* Corrugated fiberboard boxes.
* Plastic crates.
* Wooden crates (where appropriate).

---

## Packing Recommendations

* Use cushioning material.
* Avoid overfilling boxes.
* Arrange fruits carefully.

Benefits:

* Reduced bruising.
* Improved shelf life.

---

# Storage Management

## Ambient Storage

Suitable for:

* Short-term storage.

Shelf life:

* Approximately 4–10 days depending on variety and temperature.

---

## Cold Storage

Recommended conditions:

* Temperature: 12–13°C
* Relative Humidity: 85–90%

Benefits:

* Extended shelf life.
* Reduced spoilage.
* Better fruit quality.

---

# Transportation

## Best Practices

* Use ventilated vehicles.
* Avoid rough handling.
* Protect fruits from direct sunlight.
* Prevent excessive stacking.

Benefits:

* Reduced mechanical damage.
* Better market quality.

---

# Common Post-Harvest Losses

## Mechanical Damage

Caused by:

* Dropping fruits.
* Poor packaging.
* Rough transportation.

Symptoms:

* Bruising.
* Cracks.
* Skin injuries.

---

## Disease Losses

Common diseases:

### Anthracnose

Symptoms:

* Black lesions.
* Fruit rot.

### Stem-End Rot

Symptoms:

* Rot near stem region.
* Reduced shelf life.

---

## Physiological Disorders

Examples:

* Uneven ripening.
* Shriveling.
* Chilling injury.

---

# Frequently Asked Questions

## Why do mango fruits develop black spots after harvest?

Latex injury, anthracnose infection, or mechanical damage can cause black spots.

## What is the ideal storage temperature for mango?

Generally 12–13°C with 85–90% relative humidity is recommended for mature mango fruits.

## Why is de-sapping important?

De-sapping prevents latex burn and improves fruit appearance and quality.

## How can post-harvest losses be reduced?

Proper harvesting, handling, grading, packaging, storage, and transportation significantly reduce losses.
