# Drip Irrigation System for Mango

## Overview

Drip irrigation is one of the most efficient irrigation methods for mango cultivation. It delivers water directly to the root zone of the tree through a network of pipes, valves, and emitters. This method minimizes water loss due to evaporation, runoff, and deep percolation while improving water-use efficiency and crop productivity.

Drip irrigation is particularly beneficial in water-scarce regions and is widely recommended for modern mango orchards.

---

# Importance of Drip Irrigation

Drip irrigation helps:

* Save water.
* Improve fertilizer efficiency.
* Enhance fruit yield.
* Improve fruit quality.
* Reduce weed growth.
* Minimize soil erosion.
* Improve orchard management.

# Components of a Drip Irrigation System

## Water Source

Possible sources:

* Borewell
* Open well
* Farm pond
* Reservoir
* Canal supply

---

## Pump Unit

Functions:

* Provides required water pressure.
* Ensures uniform water distribution.

---

## Filtration Unit

Types:

* Sand filter
* Screen filter
* Disc filter

Importance:

* Prevents emitter blockage.
* Improves system efficiency.

---

## Mainline and Sub-Main Pipes

Functions:

* Transport water from the source to the orchard.

Materials:

* PVC pipes
* HDPE pipes

---

## Laterals

Functions:

* Carry water to individual trees.

Usually made of:

* LDPE pipes

---

## Emitters (Drippers)

Functions:

* Deliver water slowly to the root zone.

Common discharge rates:

* 4 liters/hour
* 8 liters/hour
* 16 liters/hour

# Advantages of Drip Irrigation in Mango

## Water Conservation

Benefits:

* Saves 30–60% water compared to conventional irrigation.
* Reduces evaporation losses.

---

## Improved Nutrient Efficiency

Benefits:

* Supports fertigation.
* Delivers nutrients directly to roots.
* Reduces fertilizer wastage.

---

## Better Fruit Yield

Results:

* Improved flowering.
* Better fruit set.
* Increased fruit size.

---

## Reduced Weed Growth

Since only the root zone is wetted:

* Weed emergence decreases.
* Weed management becomes easier.

---

## Improved Soil Health

Benefits:

* Maintains favorable soil moisture.
* Reduces waterlogging.
* Improves root development.

---

# Drip Irrigation Layout for Mango Orchards

## Young Trees

Recommended:

* 1–2 emitters per tree.

Emitter placement:

* Near root zone.
* Around the tree basin.

---

## Mature Trees

Recommended:

* 4–8 emitters per tree.

Emitter placement:

* Around canopy drip line.
* Uniform distribution around the tree.

# Water Requirement of Mango

Water requirements vary according to:

* Tree age.
* Soil type.
* Climate.
* Season.

### General Water Requirement

| Tree Stage   | Approximate Daily Water Requirement |
| ------------ | ----------------------------------- |
| Young Trees  | 10–30 liters/tree/day               |
| Medium Trees | 30–60 liters/tree/day               |
| Mature Trees | 50–150 liters/tree/day              |

Actual requirements should be adjusted according to local conditions.

# Common Problems

## Emitter Clogging

Causes:

* Sediments.
* Algae.
* Mineral deposits.

Solutions:

* Proper filtration.
* Regular flushing.

---

## Uneven Water Distribution

Causes:

* Pressure variations.
* Damaged pipes.

Solutions:

* Pressure regulation.
* Timely repairs.

---

## Waterlogging

Causes:

* Excess irrigation.
* Poor drainage.

Solutions:

* Schedule irrigation properly.
* Improve drainage systems.

# Frequently Asked Questions

## Is drip irrigation suitable for mango?

Yes. Drip irrigation is one of the most efficient and widely recommended irrigation systems for mango cultivation.

## How many emitters are needed for mature mango trees?

Generally, 4–8 emitters per tree are used depending on canopy size and soil conditions.

## Can fertilizers be applied through drip irrigation?

Yes. Fertigation is commonly practiced in mango orchards and improves nutrient-use efficiency.

## Does drip irrigation increase yield?

Yes. Properly managed drip irrigation can improve water-use efficiency, fruit quality, and overall yield.
