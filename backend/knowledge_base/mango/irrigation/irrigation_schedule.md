# Irrigation Schedule for Mango

## Overview

Proper irrigation management is essential for healthy growth, flowering, fruit development, and yield in mango orchards. Although mango trees are relatively drought-tolerant once established, inadequate or excessive irrigation can significantly affect fruit quality and production.

An effective irrigation schedule depends on tree age, soil type, climate, season, and rainfall patterns.

---

# Importance of Proper Irrigation

Proper irrigation helps:

- Promote healthy root development.
- Improve flowering and fruit set.
- Increase fruit size and quality.
- Reduce flower and fruit drop.
- Improve nutrient uptake.
- Enhance overall orchard productivity.

---

# Factors Affecting Irrigation Requirement

## Tree Age

Water requirements increase as trees mature.

### Young Trees (1–3 Years)

- Shallow root system.
- Require frequent irrigation.

### Medium-Aged Trees (4–7 Years)

- Moderate water requirement.
- Less frequent irrigation than young trees.

### Mature Trees (8+ Years)

- Deep root system.
- Require larger quantities of water but at longer intervals.

---

## Soil Type

### Sandy Soils

Characteristics:

- Low water-holding capacity.
- Frequent irrigation required.

### Loamy Soils

Characteristics:

- Good water retention.
- Moderate irrigation frequency.

### Clay Soils

Characteristics:

- High water-holding capacity.
- Longer irrigation intervals.

---

## Climate

Higher temperatures and dry winds increase water demand.

Factors:

- Temperature
- Humidity
- Wind speed
- Rainfall

---

# General Irrigation Schedule

## Young Mango Trees (1–3 Years)

| Season | Irrigation Frequency |
|----------|---------------------|
| Summer | Every 2–4 days |
| Winter | Every 7–10 days |
| Monsoon | As needed based on rainfall |

Approximate water requirement:

- 10–30 liters/tree/day

---

## Medium-Aged Trees (4–7 Years)

| Season | Irrigation Frequency |
|----------|---------------------|
| Summer | Every 4–7 days |
| Winter | Every 10–15 days |
| Monsoon | Usually not required |

Approximate water requirement:

- 30–60 liters/tree/day

---

## Mature Mango Trees (8+ Years)

| Season | Irrigation Frequency |
|----------|---------------------|
| Summer | Every 7–10 days |
| Winter | Every 15–20 days |
| Monsoon | Usually not required |

Approximate water requirement:

- 50–150 liters/tree/day

---

# Seasonal Irrigation Management

## Summer Season

Water demand is highest during summer.

Recommendations:

- Irrigate regularly.
- Avoid moisture stress.
- Apply mulch around trees.

Benefits:

- Prevents fruit drop.
- Improves fruit size.
- Maintains tree vigor.

---

## Monsoon Season

Recommendations:

- Reduce irrigation.
- Monitor rainfall.
- Ensure proper drainage.

Avoid:

- Waterlogging.
- Root diseases.

---

## Winter Season

Recommendations:

- Reduce irrigation frequency.
- Maintain moderate soil moisture.

Benefits:

- Supports flowering preparation.
- Prevents excessive vegetative growth.

---

# Irrigation During Critical Growth Stages

## Flowering Stage

Water management is crucial.

Recommendations:

- Maintain adequate soil moisture.
- Avoid excessive irrigation.
- Avoid prolonged drought stress.

Benefits:

- Better flower retention.
- Improved pollination.

---

## Fruit Set Stage

Recommendations:

- Regular irrigation.
- Prevent moisture fluctuations.

Benefits:

- Reduced fruit drop.
- Improved fruit development.

---

## Fruit Development Stage

Recommendations:

- Ensure continuous moisture availability.
- Avoid severe water stress.

Benefits:

- Larger fruit size.
- Better fruit quality.

---

## Maturity Stage

Recommendations:

- Slightly reduce irrigation before harvest.
- Avoid excessive moisture.

Benefits:

- Better fruit quality.
- Reduced disease risk.

---

# Drip Irrigation Schedule

Drip irrigation is highly recommended for mango orchards.

### Advantages

- Water saving.
- Uniform moisture distribution.
- Improved fertilizer efficiency.
- Better yield.

### Typical Daily Water Requirement

| Tree Age | Water Requirement |
|-----------|-------------------|
| 1–3 years | 10–30 liters/day |
| 4–7 years | 30–60 liters/day |
| Mature trees | 50–150 liters/day |

Adjust according to local conditions.

---

# Mulching and Moisture Conservation

Benefits of mulching:

- Reduces evaporation.
- Conserves soil moisture.
- Suppresses weeds.
- Improves soil health.

Suitable mulch materials:

- Dry leaves.
- Straw.
- Coconut husk.
- Organic compost.

---

# Signs of Water Stress

## Under-Irrigation

Symptoms:

- Leaf wilting.
- Flower drop.
- Fruit drop.
- Reduced fruit size.
- Poor growth.

---

## Over-Irrigation

Symptoms:

- Yellowing leaves.
- Root rot.
- Waterlogging.
- Increased disease incidence.

---

# Monitoring Soil Moisture

Methods:

- Visual observation.
- Soil moisture meters.
- Finger test in root zone.

Recommended:

- Irrigate based on actual soil moisture rather than fixed schedules whenever possible.

---

# Best Management Practices

- Use drip irrigation whenever possible.
- Apply mulch around trees.
- Monitor soil moisture regularly.
- Avoid both drought stress and waterlogging.
- Adjust irrigation according to rainfall.
- Ensure proper drainage during monsoon.

---

# Monitoring Checklist

Regularly check:

- Soil moisture.
- Tree growth.
- Flower retention.
- Fruit development.
- Irrigation system performance.
- Signs of water stress.

# Frequently Asked Questions

## How often should mango trees be watered?

The frequency depends on tree age, soil type, and season. Young trees may require irrigation every 2–4 days during summer, while mature trees may require irrigation every 7–10 days.

## Is drip irrigation suitable for mango?

Yes. Drip irrigation is one of the most efficient irrigation methods for mango orchards.

## Can over-irrigation harm mango trees?

Yes. Excess irrigation can cause root diseases, nutrient leaching, and poor flowering.

## Should irrigation be stopped during monsoon?

In most cases, irrigation can be reduced or stopped depending on rainfall and soil moisture conditions.
