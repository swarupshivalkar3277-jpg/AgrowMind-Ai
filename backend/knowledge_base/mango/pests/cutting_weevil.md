# Cutting Weevil in Mango

## Overview

Cutting Weevil is an important insect pest of mango that damages tender shoots, flower stalks, leaves, and young fruits. The pest derives its name from its habit of cutting and feeding on young plant parts, resulting in drying and shedding of affected tissues.

Severe infestations can reduce flowering, fruit set, and overall yield.

---

# Scientific Classification

Common Name: Mango Cutting Weevil

Scientific Name: Deporaus marginatus (commonly associated with mango cutting damage)

Order: Coleoptera

Family: Attelabidae

---

# Distribution

The pest is found in many mango-growing regions and is more prevalent during periods of new vegetative growth and flowering.

---

# Identification

## Adult Weevil

Characteristics:

- Small brown to reddish-brown beetle.
- Elongated snout.
- Hard wing covers.
- Active during morning and evening hours.

---

## Eggs

Characteristics:

- Small and oval-shaped.
- Laid on tender plant tissues.

---

## Larvae

Characteristics:

- Creamy white.
- Legless grubs.
- Feed inside damaged plant tissues.

---

# Nature of Damage

The adult weevil causes most of the visible damage.

Affected plant parts:

- Tender shoots.
- Young leaves.
- Flower stalks.
- Developing fruits.

---

## Symptoms

### Cutting of Tender Shoots

Symptoms:

- Young shoots appear cut or severed.
- Drying of shoot tips.
- Reduced vegetative growth.

---

### Damage to Flower Panicles

Symptoms:

- Flower stalks are cut.
- Flowers dry and fall.
- Reduced fruit set.

---

### Fruit Damage

Symptoms:

- Young fruits may drop prematurely.
- Reduced fruit retention.

---

# Life Cycle

## Egg Stage

- Eggs are deposited on tender plant parts.
- Incubation period generally lasts a few days.

---

## Larval Stage

- Larvae feed within plant tissues.
- Continue development for several weeks.

---

## Pupal Stage

- Pupation occurs in protected locations.
- Adult emergence follows completion of pupation.

---

## Adult Stage

- Adults become active during new flush and flowering periods.
- Multiple generations may occur annually.

---

# Favorable Conditions

The pest is favored by:

- Warm temperatures.
- New vegetative flushes.
- Dense orchard canopy.
- Poor orchard sanitation.

---

# Economic Importance

Severe infestations can cause:

- Reduced flowering.
- Poor fruit set.
- Loss of young fruits.
- Lower yield potential.

Young orchards are particularly vulnerable.

---

# Integrated Pest Management (IPM)

## Orchard Sanitation

Recommendations:

- Remove damaged shoots.
- Collect fallen plant debris.
- Destroy infested material.

Benefits:

- Reduces pest population.
- Breaks pest life cycle.

---

## Pruning

Recommended:

- Remove overcrowded branches.
- Improve sunlight penetration.
- Improve air circulation.

Benefits:

- Less favorable environment for pests.
- Easier monitoring.

---

## Regular Monitoring

Inspect orchards weekly for:

- Cut shoots.
- Damaged flower stalks.
- Adult beetle activity.

Early detection improves management success.

---

# Mechanical Control

Recommended practices:

- Hand collection of adult weevils where feasible.
- Destruction of infested shoots.

Benefits:

- Reduces pest numbers without chemicals.

---

# Biological Control

Natural enemies may help suppress populations.

Examples:

- Predatory insects.
- Parasitoids.
- Entomopathogenic fungi.

Conservation of beneficial organisms is encouraged.

---

# Chemical Management

If infestation reaches damaging levels:

Use insecticides recommended by local agricultural authorities and follow label instructions.

Important:

- Apply only when necessary.
- Avoid spraying during peak pollinator activity.
- Follow integrated pest management principles.

---

# Monitoring Checklist

Regularly inspect for:

- Cut tender shoots.
- Damaged flower panicles.
- Adult weevil presence.
- Premature fruit drop.
- New infestation sites.

---

# Best Management Practices

- Maintain orchard sanitation.
- Monitor orchards regularly.
- Prune trees annually.
- Remove infested plant parts.
- Encourage beneficial insects.
- Follow integrated pest management practices.
- Apply recommended insecticides only when necessary.

---

# Frequently Asked Questions

## What is the main symptom of cutting weevil damage?

The most characteristic symptom is the cutting of tender shoots and flower stalks, resulting in drying and shedding.

## Does cutting weevil affect mango flowering?

Yes. The pest can damage flower panicles and reduce fruit set.

## Can pruning help control cutting weevil?

Yes. Proper pruning improves air circulation and reduces favorable conditions for infestation.

## Does cutting weevil cause fruit drop?

Yes. Damage to flowers and young fruits can result in premature fruit drop.
