# Fruit Fly in Mango

## Overview

Fruit fly is one of the most destructive pests of mango and can cause severe economic losses in commercial orchards. The pest attacks developing and ripening fruits, making them unsuitable for consumption and marketing.

Fruit fly infestation can cause significant yield reduction, especially during the fruit maturation period. Proper monitoring and integrated pest management are essential to minimize losses.

---

# Scientific Classification

Common Name: Mango Fruit Fly

Scientific Names:

- Bactrocera dorsalis (Oriental Fruit Fly)
- Bactrocera zonata (Peach Fruit Fly)

Order: Diptera

Family: Tephritidae

---

# Distribution

Fruit flies are widely distributed in tropical and subtropical mango-growing regions and are common throughout India.

Major outbreaks occur during:

- Fruit development stage
- Fruit ripening stage
- Warm and humid weather

---

# Identification

## Adult Fruit Fly

Characteristics:

- Small fly resembling a housefly.
- Yellowish-brown body.
- Transparent wings with dark markings.
- Active during daylight hours.

Size:

- Approximately 6–8 mm long.

---

## Eggs

Characteristics:

- White and elongated.
- Deposited beneath fruit skin.

---

## Larvae (Maggots)

Characteristics:

- Creamy white.
- Legless.
- Feed inside fruit pulp.

These are the most damaging stage of the pest.

---

## Pupae

Characteristics:

- Brown, barrel-shaped.
- Found in soil beneath infested trees.

---

# Nature of Damage

Female flies puncture fruit skin and lay eggs inside developing fruits.

After hatching:

- Maggots feed on fruit pulp.
- Internal fruit tissue is destroyed.
- Secondary fungal and bacterial infections develop.

---

# Symptoms

## Oviposition Punctures

Early signs:

- Small dark puncture marks on fruit surface.
- Resin or sap exudation may occur.

---

## Internal Feeding

Symptoms:

- Soft patches inside fruits.
- Tunneling by maggots.
- Fruit pulp breakdown.

---

## Fruit Rot

Infested fruits often develop:

- Foul smell.
- Internal decay.
- Secondary microbial infections.

---

## Premature Fruit Drop

Affected fruits:

- Fall before maturity.
- Become unsuitable for sale.

---

# Life Cycle

## Egg Stage

Duration:

- 1–3 days

Female flies deposit eggs beneath fruit skin.

---

## Larval Stage

Duration:

- 7–14 days

Maggots feed inside fruit pulp.

---

## Pupal Stage

Duration:

- 7–12 days

Maggots leave fruits and pupate in soil.

---

## Adult Stage

Adults emerge from pupae and begin reproduction.

Multiple generations may occur annually.

---

# Favorable Conditions

Fruit fly populations increase under:

- Warm temperatures.
- High humidity.
- Presence of ripening fruits.
- Poor orchard sanitation.

---

# Economic Importance

Severe infestations can cause:

- 20–80% fruit loss.
- Reduced fruit quality.
- Market rejection.
- Export restrictions.

Fruit fly is considered one of the most important quarantine pests in mango.

---

# Integrated Pest Management (IPM)

## Orchard Sanitation

Essential practice:

- Collect fallen fruits regularly.
- Destroy infested fruits.
- Remove rotting fruits from orchard.

Benefits:

- Breaks pest life cycle.
- Reduces pest population.

---

## Soil Cultivation

Light cultivation beneath trees:

- Exposes pupae.
- Reduces adult emergence.

---

## Fruit Bagging

Cover developing fruits using:

- Paper bags.
- Cloth bags.
- Special fruit protection bags.

Benefits:

- Prevents egg laying.
- Improves fruit quality.

---

# Monitoring

## Methyl Eugenol Traps

Widely used for monitoring and mass trapping.

Benefits:

- Detects pest activity.
- Reduces male population.

Recommended installation:

- Several traps per hectare.
- Before fruit maturation.

---

# Biological Control

Natural enemies include:

- Parasitoid wasps.
- Predatory insects.
- Entomopathogenic fungi.

Conservation of beneficial organisms is recommended.

---

# Chemical Management

Chemical control should be integrated with sanitation and trapping.

Use only insecticides approved by local agricultural authorities.

Important guidelines:

- Follow label instructions.
- Observe waiting periods before harvest.
- Avoid unnecessary applications.

---

# Quarantine Importance

Fruit fly infestation can affect:

- Domestic marketability.
- Export opportunities.

Export-quality fruits must be free from fruit fly infestation.

---

# Monitoring Checklist

Inspect orchards regularly for:

- Adult fruit flies.
- Puncture marks on fruits.
- Fallen fruits.
- Maggot infestation.
- Trap catches.

---

# Best Management Practices

- Install fruit fly traps before fruit maturation.
- Collect and destroy fallen fruits.
- Practice orchard sanitation.
- Use fruit bagging where feasible.
- Monitor orchards regularly.
- Follow integrated pest management practices.
- Apply approved control measures when required.

---

# Frequently Asked Questions

## How can I identify fruit fly damage?

Look for small puncture marks on fruits, premature fruit drop, internal rotting, and maggots inside the fruit.

## Why do mango fruits rot from inside?

Fruit fly larvae feed internally, causing pulp breakdown and secondary infections.

## Are fruit fly traps effective?

Yes. Methyl eugenol traps are widely used for monitoring and reducing fruit fly populations.

## Can fruit fly infestation affect exports?

Yes. Fruit fly is a major quarantine pest and can lead to rejection of export consignments.
