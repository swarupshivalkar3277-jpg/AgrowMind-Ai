# Mango Mealy Bug

## Overview

Mango mealy bug is a serious sap-sucking pest that affects mango orchards throughout many mango-growing regions. The pest attacks tender shoots, leaves, flower panicles, and young fruits, causing weakening of trees, reduced flowering, poor fruit set, and significant yield losses.

Heavy infestations may lead to severe economic damage, especially during the flowering and fruit-setting stages.

---

# Scientific Classification

Common Name: Mango Mealy Bug

Scientific Name: Drosicha mangiferae

Order: Hemiptera

Family: Monophlebidae

---

# Distribution

Mango mealy bug is commonly found in:

- India
- South Asia
- Tropical and subtropical mango-growing regions

The pest is particularly active during the flowering season.

---

# Identification

## Adult Female

Characteristics:

- Oval-shaped body.
- Covered with white waxy powder.
- Soft-bodied insect.
- Wingless.
- Larger than males.

Size:

- Approximately 8–12 mm long.

---

## Adult Male

Characteristics:

- Smaller than females.
- Possesses wings.
- Short lifespan.

Rarely observed in orchards.

---

## Nymphs

Characteristics:

- Small, pale yellow to pinkish.
- Covered with white wax.
- Active feeders.

Nymphs cause significant damage.

---

# Nature of Damage

Both nymphs and adult females suck sap from:

- Tender shoots.
- Leaves.
- Flower panicles.
- Developing fruits.

This feeding weakens the tree and affects fruit production.

---

# Symptoms

## Leaf Damage

Symptoms:

- Yellowing of leaves.
- Curling of leaves.
- Premature leaf fall.

---

## Flower Damage

Symptoms:

- Drying of flower panicles.
- Poor flowering.
- Reduced fruit set.

---

## Fruit Damage

Symptoms:

- Poor fruit development.
- Premature fruit drop.
- Reduced fruit quality.

---

## Honeydew Production

Mealy bugs secrete sugary honeydew.

Consequences:

- Sticky leaves and fruits.
- Growth of sooty mould fungus.
- Reduced photosynthesis.

---

## Heavy Infestation

Symptoms:

- Weak tree growth.
- Stunted shoots.
- Yield reduction.

---

# Life Cycle

## Egg Stage

Female insects lay eggs in soil cracks and protected locations.

Eggs remain dormant until favorable conditions occur.

---

## Nymph Stage

Duration:

- Several weeks

Nymphs climb trees and begin feeding on tender tissues.

This is the most damaging stage.

---

## Adult Stage

Adults continue feeding and reproduction.

One generation generally occurs per year.

---

# Favorable Conditions

Mealy bug populations increase under:

- Warm temperatures.
- Dry weather.
- Dense orchard conditions.
- Poor orchard sanitation.

---

# Economic Importance

Severe infestations may cause:

- Flower loss.
- Fruit drop.
- Reduced fruit quality.
- Significant yield reduction.

Young orchards may be particularly affected.

---

# Integrated Pest Management (IPM)

## Orchard Sanitation

Recommendations:

- Remove weeds around trees.
- Destroy alternate host plants.
- Maintain clean orchard conditions.

Benefits:

- Reduces pest survival.

---

## Deep Ploughing

Deep ploughing during summer:

- Exposes eggs to sunlight.
- Reduces future infestations.

---

## Tree Banding

Apply alkathene or sticky bands around tree trunks.

Benefits:

- Prevents nymphs from climbing trees.
- Highly effective in reducing infestations.

Recommended before flowering season.

---

# Mechanical Control

Recommended practices:

- Remove heavily infested branches.
- Destroy infested plant parts.

Benefits:

- Reduces pest population.

---

# Biological Control

Natural enemies include:

- Ladybird beetles.
- Lacewings.
- Predatory insects.
- Parasitic insects.

Conservation of beneficial insects is recommended.

---

# Chemical Management

When infestations exceed economic thresholds:

Use insecticides approved by local agricultural authorities.

Important:

- Follow label recommendations.
- Avoid unnecessary applications.
- Integrate chemical control with other management methods.

---

# Sooty Mould Association

Honeydew secreted by mealy bugs promotes the growth of sooty mould.

Symptoms:

- Black fungal coating on leaves.
- Reduced photosynthesis.
- Poor plant vigor.

Managing mealy bugs helps prevent sooty mould development.

---

# Monitoring Checklist

Inspect orchards regularly for:

- White cottony insect clusters.
- Sticky honeydew deposits.
- Black sooty mould growth.
- Flower damage.
- Fruit drop.

---

# Best Management Practices

- Perform deep summer ploughing.
- Install trunk bands before flowering.
- Maintain orchard sanitation.
- Monitor orchards regularly.
- Encourage beneficial insects.
- Remove heavily infested plant parts.
- Follow integrated pest management practices.

---

# Frequently Asked Questions

## How can I identify mango mealy bug?

Mealy bugs appear as white cottony insects clustered on shoots, leaves, flowers, and fruits.

## Why are my mango leaves sticky?

Sticky leaves are caused by honeydew secreted by mealy bugs.

## What causes black fungus on mango leaves after mealy bug attack?

Sooty mould fungus grows on honeydew deposits produced by mealy bugs.

## Is trunk banding effective against mango mealy bug?

Yes. Trunk banding is one of the most effective methods to prevent nymphs from climbing trees.
