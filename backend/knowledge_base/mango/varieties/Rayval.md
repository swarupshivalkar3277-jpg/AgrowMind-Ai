# Rayval Mango (Raywala / Local Coastal Variety)

## Overview

Rayval mango is a local mango variety name used in parts of Maharashtra, especially coastal and Konkan regions. It is not a single standardized commercial cultivar but rather a **regional/local mango type grouping** used by farmers and local markets.

It is mainly grown in small orchards and backyard farms and is valued for its taste and local availability.

---

# Classification Note (Important)

Rayval is considered:

- A **local landrace / regional cultivar group**
- Not a formally registered scientific variety
- May vary in fruit size, taste, and season depending on location

---

# Geographic Distribution

Commonly found in:

- Maharashtra (Konkan region)
- Raigad district
- Ratnagiri (local orchards)
- Coastal villages of Western India

---

# Tree Characteristics

## Growth Habit

Characteristics:

- Medium to large tree
- Dense canopy
- Strong vegetative growth in humid coastal climate

---

## Adaptability

Grows well in:

- Coastal humid regions
- Lateritic soils
- High rainfall zones

---

# Fruit Characteristics

## Fruit Size

- Small to medium-sized fruits
- Size varies significantly between trees

---

## Shape

- Oval to slightly elongated
- Irregular shape in some cases

---

## Skin Color

At maturity:

- Green to yellow-green
- Some fruits may show slight yellowing

---

## Pulp Characteristics

Features:

- Sweet taste (varies by tree)
- Medium juice content
- Fiber content varies (low to moderate)

---

## Aroma

- Mild to moderate aroma
- Less intense compared to Alphonso or Kesar

---

# Season

Harvesting period:

- Generally March to June
- Varies depending on local climate conditions

---

# Climatic Requirements

Preferred conditions:

- Warm tropical climate
- High humidity
- Heavy rainfall zones

Temperature range:

- 24°C to 32°C

---

# Soil Requirements

Suitable soils:

- Lateritic soils (common in Konkan)
- Red soils
- Coastal sandy loam soils

Important requirement:

- Good drainage (despite high rainfall)

---

# Yield Potential

Yield is variable:

- Low to moderate yield per tree
- Depends heavily on tree age and management

---

# Commercial Importance

Rayval is mainly:

- Locally consumed
- Sold in village markets
- Not widely used for export or large-scale commercial farming

---

# Advantages

## Local Adaptation

- Well adapted to coastal climate
- Tolerates high humidity

---

## Natural Growth

- Requires minimal intensive management
- Suitable for traditional farming systems

---

## Taste

- Acceptable sweetness
- Locally preferred flavor

---

# Limitations

## Lack of Standardization

- Not a genetically uniform variety
- High variation between trees

---

## Low Commercial Value

- Not suitable for export markets
- Limited shelf life compared to commercial varieties

---

## Irregular Yield

- Production may vary year to year

---

# Pest and Disease Susceptibility

Common issues:

- Mango hopper
- Fruit fly
- Mealy bug
- Anthracnose

Management is similar to other mango varieties.

---

# Nutrient Management

Basic recommendations:

- Apply organic manure annually
- Balanced NPK application improves fruiting
- Micronutrients improve fruit quality

---

# Irrigation Management

- Rainfed in most coastal regions
- Supplemental irrigation during dry spells improves yield

---

# Post-Harvest Characteristics

## Shelf Life

- Short shelf life compared to commercial varieties

---

## Storage

- Best consumed fresh after harvest
- Limited storage suitability

---

# Best Management Practices

- Maintain orchard hygiene
- Apply organic manure regularly
- Control pests during flowering season
- Improve canopy ventilation through pruning
- Harvest at proper maturity stage

---

# Frequently Asked Questions

## Is Rayval a commercial mango variety?

No. It is mainly a local/regional mango type used in coastal Maharashtra.

## What does Rayval mango taste like?

It is moderately sweet with variable aroma depending on the tree.

## Where is Rayval mango found?

Mostly in the Konkan region of Maharashtra.

## Is Rayval mango good for export?

No. It is not suitable for export due to variability and low shelf life.
