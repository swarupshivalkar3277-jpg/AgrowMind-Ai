# Totapuri Mango

## Overview

Totapuri mango is one of the most widely cultivated commercial mango varieties in South India. It is highly valued for its firm texture, distinctive beak-shaped tip, and excellent suitability for processing industries such as pulp, juice, and pickles.

Unlike premium table varieties like Alphonso or Kesar, Totapuri is primarily used for processing due to its slightly tangy flavor and strong transportability.

---

# Scientific Classification

Common Name: Totapuri Mango  
Botanical Name: *Mangifera indica* L.  
Family: Anacardiaceae  

---

# Origin and Distribution

Totapuri mango is widely grown in:

- Karnataka (major production)
- Andhra Pradesh
- Telangana
- Tamil Nadu

It is one of the backbone varieties for mango pulp industries in India.

---

# Tree Characteristics

## Growth Habit

- Medium to large vigorous tree
- Upright growth pattern
- Dense canopy if not pruned

---

## Tree Height

- 10–15 meters in mature trees

Canopy management is recommended for easier harvesting and spraying.

---

# Fruit Characteristics

## Shape

- Oblong shape
- Distinct pointed “parrot beak” tip (key identifying feature)

---

## Fruit Weight

- 250–400 grams on average

---

## Skin Color

At maturity:

- Greenish-yellow
- Does not turn fully deep yellow like Alphonso or Kesar

---

## Pulp Characteristics

- Firm flesh
- Less fiber compared to many desi varieties
- Slightly tangy to sweet taste depending on maturity
- High pulp yield (important for industry use)

---

# Taste Profile

Totapuri is known for:

- Mild sweetness
- Light tanginess
- Less aromatic compared to premium varieties

It is not primarily considered a dessert mango but is excellent for processing.

---

# Flowering and Fruiting

## Flowering Season

- December to February

## Harvest Season

- May to July (varies by region)

---

# Climatic Requirements

- Tropical to subtropical climate
- Temperature: 24°C to 32°C
- Performs well in dry zones
- Tolerates moderate drought conditions better than many varieties

---

# Soil Requirements

Best suited for:

- Well-drained sandy loam soils
- Red soils
- Black soils with good drainage

Preferred pH:

- 5.5 to 7.5

Avoid waterlogged conditions.

---

# Yield Potential

- High-yielding variety
- Average: 150–300 fruits per tree annually (varies widely with management)

---

# Commercial Importance

Totapuri is extremely important for:

## Mango Processing Industry

Used for:

- Mango pulp
- Juice concentrates
- Puree production
- Pickles

---

## Transport and Storage

Advantages:

- Firm fruit structure
- Good shelf life
- Excellent transportability
- Less damage during shipping

---

# Major Advantages

## High Processing Value

- One of the best pulp varieties in India
- High pulp recovery percentage

---

## Disease and Climate Tolerance

- Relatively hardy
- Performs well under varied climatic conditions

---

## Good Shelf Life

- Suitable for long-distance transport

---

# Limitations

## Low Table Quality

- Less aromatic
- Mild taste compared to premium mangoes

---

## Market Price

- Lower price in fresh fruit markets compared to Alphonso/Kesar

---

# Major Pests

- Mango hopper
- Fruit fly
- Mealy bug
- Scale insects

---

# Major Diseases

- Anthracnose
- Powdery mildew
- Dieback

---

# Nutrient Management

Recommended nutrients:

- Nitrogen (N) for vegetative growth
- Phosphorus (P) for root development
- Potassium (K) for fruit quality

Micronutrients:

- Zinc
- Boron
- Iron

Organic manure improves soil health and yield stability.

---

# Irrigation Management

- Young trees: regular irrigation required
- Mature trees: drought tolerant but irrigation improves yield
- Drip irrigation recommended for efficiency

---

# Best Management Practices

- Regular pruning for canopy control
- Integrated pest management (IPM)
- Balanced fertilization
- Proper irrigation scheduling
- Timely harvesting for processing quality

---

# Frequently Asked Questions

## Why is Totapuri mango important?

Because it is one of the most important mangoes for pulp and juice industries in India.

## Is Totapuri good for eating?

It is edible but mainly used for processing due to its tangy flavor.

## Which state produces most Totapuri mango?

Karnataka and Andhra Pradesh.

## Why is Totapuri called "parrot beak mango"?

Because of its distinct pointed tip resembling a parrot’s beak.
