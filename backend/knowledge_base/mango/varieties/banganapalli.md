# Banganapalli Mango

## Overview

Banganapalli mango is one of the most popular table mango varieties in India. It is known for its large fruit size, attractive golden-yellow skin, sweet taste, and long shelf life. The variety is highly valued in both domestic and international markets.

Banganapalli is also known by names such as:

- Benishan
- Safeda
- Baneshan

It is one of the most widely cultivated commercial mango varieties in South India.

---

# Scientific Classification

Common Name: Banganapalli Mango

Botanical Name: Mangifera indica L.

Family: Anacardiaceae

---

# Origin

The variety originated in:

- Banganapalle, Andhra Pradesh, India

Today it is extensively cultivated in:

- Andhra Pradesh
- Telangana
- Karnataka
- Tamil Nadu
- Maharashtra
- Gujarat

---

# Tree Characteristics

## Growth Habit

Characteristics:

- Vigorous growth
- Upright tree structure
- Large canopy development

---

## Tree Size

Mature trees may reach:

- 10–15 meters in height

Regular pruning helps maintain manageable canopy size.

---

# Fruit Characteristics

## Fruit Shape

Characteristics:

- Oblong shape
- Smooth surface
- Attractive appearance

---

## Fruit Weight

Average fruit weight:

- 300–500 grams

Under good management, fruits may exceed 600 grams.

---

## Skin Color

At maturity:

- Bright golden yellow

The fruit develops an attractive appearance suitable for marketing.

---

## Pulp Characteristics

Features:

- Deep yellow pulp
- Firm texture
- Fiberless to nearly fiberless
- High pulp recovery

---

## Taste

Known for:

- Sweet flavor
- Mild aroma
- Excellent eating quality

---

# Flowering and Fruiting

## Flowering Season

Generally occurs during:

- December to February

Depending on regional climatic conditions.

---

## Harvest Season

Harvesting period:

- April to June

Peak harvesting may vary by region.

---

# Climatic Requirements

Ideal temperature:

- 24°C to 32°C

Suitable climate:

- Tropical
- Subtropical

Excessive rainfall during flowering should be avoided.

---

# Soil Requirements

Best suited for:

- Well-drained loamy soils
- Sandy loam soils
- Red soils

Preferred pH:

- 5.5 to 7.5

Avoid:

- Waterlogged conditions
- Poorly drained soils

---

# Yield Potential

Average yield:

- 200–400 fruits per mature tree annually

Yield depends on:

- Tree age
- Nutrition management
- Irrigation practices
- Pest and disease control

Trees may exhibit:

- Heavy production in one year
