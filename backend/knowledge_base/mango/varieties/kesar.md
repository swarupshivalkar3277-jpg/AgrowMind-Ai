# Kesar Mango

## Overview

Kesar mango is one of the most popular premium mango varieties in India, known for its bright saffron-colored pulp, sweet flavor, and rich aroma. It is widely grown in Gujarat and is highly valued in both domestic and export markets.

It is often called the "Queen of Mangoes" due to its distinct aroma and taste.

---

# Scientific Classification

Common Name: Kesar Mango

Botanical Name: Mangifera indica L.

Family: Anacardiaceae

---

# Origin

Kesar mango originated in:

- Gir region of Gujarat, India

Major cultivation areas include:

- Gujarat (Junagadh, Gir Somnath)
- Maharashtra
- Madhya Pradesh
- Some parts of Rajasthan

---

# Tree Characteristics

## Growth Habit

Characteristics:

- Medium-sized tree
- Dense canopy
- Vigorous vegetative growth

---

## Tree Height

Mature trees may reach:

- 8–15 meters

Proper pruning is required for commercial orchards.

---

# Fruit Characteristics

## Fruit Shape

Characteristics:

- Oval to oblong shape
- Slightly pointed tip
- Smooth skin

---

## Fruit Weight

Average fruit weight:

- 200–300 grams

---

## Skin Color

At maturity:

- Greenish-yellow turning to golden yellow

---

## Pulp Characteristics

Features:

- Deep saffron (kesari) colored pulp
- Very sweet taste
- Smooth, fine texture
- Low fiber content

---

## Aroma and Taste

Kesar is known for:

- Strong, pleasant aroma
- Rich sweetness
- High juice content

---

# Flowering and Fruiting

## Flowering Season

Occurs during:

- December to February

---

## Harvest Season

Harvesting period:

- April to June

---

# Climatic Requirements

Ideal conditions:

- Tropical to subtropical climate
- Temperature: 24°C to 32°C
- Dry weather during flowering is beneficial

Excess humidity may affect flowering.

---

# Soil Requirements

Suitable soils:

- Well-drained loamy soils
- Red soils
- Black cotton soils (with good drainage)

Preferred pH:

- 5.5 to 7.5

Avoid waterlogged soils.

---

# Yield Potential

Average yield:

- 150–300 fruits per mature tree annually

Yield depends on:

- Orchard management
- Nutrition
- Irrigation
- Pest and disease control

# Major Challenges

## Alternate Bearing

Trees may show:

- High yield in one year
- Low yield in next year

Proper management helps reduce this issue.

---

## Disease Susceptibility

- Anthracnose

- Dieback

---

## Pest Problems

Major pests:

- Mango hopper

# Nutrient Management

Recommended nutrients:

- Nitrogen (N)
- Phosphorus (P)
- Potassium (K)

Micronutrients:

- Zinc
- Boron
- Iron

Organic manure improves fruit quality.

---

# Irrigation Management

Young trees:

- Regular irrigation required

Mature trees:

- Irrigate during dry periods

Drip irrigation is recommended for efficiency.

Moderate shelf life under proper storage.

## Transportability

Good transportability when harvested at correct maturity stage.

- Maintain proper canopy management.
- Follow balanced fertilization schedule.
- Use drip irrigation.

# Frequently Asked Questions

## Why is Kesar mango famous?

Because of its saffron-colored pulp, sweet taste, and strong aroma.

## Which state produces Kesar mango?

Primarily Gujarat, especially the Gir region.

## Is Kesar mango good for export?

Yes, it has strong export demand due to its flavor and appearance.

## What is the pulp color of Kesar mango?

Deep saffron (orange-yellow) color.
