# Other Mango Varieties (India) - Comprehensive Guide

## Overview

This document contains important mango varieties that are not covered in your main premium set (Alphonso, Kesar, Banganapalli, Dashehari). These include regional, processing, hybrid, and lesser-known but agriculturally important cultivars.

These varieties are widely used in:
- Local markets
- Juice/pulp industry
- Regional cultivation
- Genetic breeding programs

---

# 🥭 1. Chausa (Chotapuri type group)

## Overview
Chausa is one of the most famous late-season mango varieties in North India.

## Key Features
- Extremely sweet taste
- Strong aroma
- Fiberless pulp
- Very juicy fruit

## Season
- July to August (late mango season)

## Regions
- Uttar Pradesh
- Bihar
- Punjab
- Haryana

## Importance
- High market demand in North India
- Excellent table mango
- Premium local export potential

---

# 🥭 2. Langra (Banarasi Langda)

## Overview
One of India’s most iconic mango varieties.

## Key Features
- Green skin even when ripe
- Sweet + mild tangy flavor
- Fiberless pulp
- Medium-sized fruit

## Season
- June to July

## Regions
- Varanasi (UP)
- Bihar
- Eastern India

## Importance
- High cultural value
- Strong domestic demand
- Premium table mango

---

# 🥭 3. Totapuri (Bitki-type in some regions)

## Overview
A commercial mango widely used for processing industry.

## Key Features
- Distinct parrot-beak shape
- Tangy taste
- Firm pulp
- High juice content

## Season
- May to July

## Regions
- Karnataka
- Andhra Pradesh
- Tamil Nadu

## Importance
- No.1 mango for pulp industry
- Used in juices, nectar, puree

---

# 🥭 4. Neelum

## Overview
Late-season South Indian mango variety.

## Key Features
- Small to medium fruit
- Sweet taste with slight tang
- Excellent shelf life
- Good transportability

## Season
- July to August

## Regions
- Tamil Nadu
- Andhra Pradesh
- Karnataka

## Importance
- Extends mango season
- Good for market supply continuity

---

# 🥭 5. Raspuri

## Overview
Highly juicy mango variety from Karnataka.

## Key Features
- Very soft pulp
- Extremely juicy
- Sweet flavor
- Medium fruit size

## Season
- May to June

## Importance
- Ideal for juice industry
- Local market favorite

---

# 🥭 6. Himsagar

## Overview
Premium mango from Eastern India.

## Key Features
- Fiberless pulp
- Very sweet taste
- Strong aroma
- Medium-sized fruit

## Season
- May to June

## Regions
- West Bengal
- Odisha
- Bihar

## Importance
- Highly premium local variety
- Limited availability increases value

---

# 🥭 7. Fazli

## Overview
One of the largest mango varieties in India.

## Key Features
- Very large fruit size
- Mild sweet taste
- Soft pulp
- Less aromatic

## Season
- July to August

## Importance
- High yield per fruit weight
- Used in local markets

---

# 🥭 8. Mallika (Hybrid)

## Overview
Hybrid mango developed from Neelum × Dashehari.

## Key Features
- Excellent sweetness
- Strong aroma
- Fiberless pulp
- Good shelf life

## Season
- June to July

## Importance
- High-quality hybrid mango
- Good export potential

---

# 🥭 9. Amrapali (Hybrid dwarf variety)

## Overview
Popular dwarf hybrid mango variety.

## Key Features
- Compact tree size
- High density planting suitable
- Sweet flavor
- Fiberless pulp

## Season
- July to August

## Importance
- Ideal for high-density orchards
- Suitable for small farmers

---

# 🥭 10. Bombay Green

## Overview
Early-season mango variety.

## Key Features
- Green even when ripe
- Slight tangy taste
- Firm texture

## Season
- April to May

## Importance
- Early market advantage
- High price in early season

---

# 🥭 11. Safeda (Baneshan)

## Overview
South Indian traditional mango variety.

## Key Features
- Large fruit size
- Sweet taste
- Firm pulp
- Good shelf life

## Regions
- Andhra Pradesh
- Telangana

---

# 🥭 12. Pairi (Payri)

## Overview
Popular variety in Maharashtra.

## Key Features
- Strong aroma
- Sweet taste
- Medium fruit size

## Importance
- Local favorite in Konkan region
