# Mancozeb Fungicide

## 1. Overview

Mancozeb is a broad-spectrum, non-systemic, contact fungicide used for the prevention and control of many fungal diseases in field crops, vegetables, fruits, plantation crops, and ornamentals. It belongs to the dithiocarbamate group and is widely used because it controls many diseases at a comparatively low cost.

Mancozeb is mainly a **protectant fungicide**, meaning it works best when applied before disease infection or at the very early stage of disease development. It does not move inside the plant like systemic fungicides and does not cure already established infections.

Common brand/product names may include formulations such as Mancozeb 75% WP, Mancozeb 75% WG, Mancozeb 80% WP, and mixture products containing mancozeb with systemic fungicides such as metalaxyl, carbendazim, cymoxanil, or others.

Mancozeb is classified under **FRAC Group M03**, which means it has **multi-site activity**. Multi-site fungicides affect several biochemical processes in fungal cells, reducing the chance of resistance development compared with single-site fungicides.

## 3. Mode of Action

Mancozeb acts on fungal pathogens by interfering with multiple enzyme systems. It reacts with sulfhydryl groups in fungal amino acids and enzymes, which disrupts important metabolic processes such as respiration, lipid metabolism, and energy production.

Because mancozeb acts on multiple sites in the fungus, pathogens find it difficult to develop resistance. This makes mancozeb useful in fungicide-resistance management programs.

### Important Mode-of-Action Points

- Mancozeb remains mostly on the plant surface.
- It prevents fungal spores from germinating.
- It protects new tissue only if spray coverage is good.
- It is not highly effective after the disease has already spread inside plant tissue.
- It is commonly used in rotation or tank-mix programs with systemic fungicides.
- It helps reduce resistance pressure on high-risk single-site fungicides.

## 4. Type of Fungicide Action

Mancozeb is mainly:

- **Protective**
- **Contact**
- **Preventive**
- **Broad-spectrum**
- **Multi-site**

It is **not primarily curative**. Farmers should apply it before disease pressure becomes severe, especially when weather is humid, rainy, cloudy, or favorable for fungal infection.

## 6. Crop-Wise Uses

### 6.1 Tomato

Mancozeb is commonly used in tomato for:

- Early blight
- Late blight
- Leaf spot
- Buckeye rot

It should be applied as a preventive spray when weather conditions favor disease, such as high humidity, cloudy weather, or repeated rainfall.

#### Tomato Advisory

- Start spraying when early symptoms appear or when disease-conducive weather begins.
- Ensure full coverage of lower and upper leaf surfaces.
- Avoid spraying during strong sunlight, high wind, or expected rain.
- For severe late blight pressure, rotate or combine with recommended systemic fungicides as per local label guidance.

### 6.2 Potato

Mancozeb is widely used in potato for:

- Late blight
- Early blight

Potato late blight spreads rapidly during cool, humid, and wet weather. Mancozeb is useful as a preventive protectant spray before late blight becomes established.

#### Potato Advisory

- Begin preventive sprays before disease outbreak in blight-prone regions.
- Maintain spray coverage on foliage.
- Repeat applications may be needed based on label instructions, disease pressure, and rainfall.
- Use in rotation with other fungicide groups during high disease pressure.

### 6.3 Grapes

Mancozeb is commonly used in grapes for:

- Downy mildew
- Anthracnose
- Angular leaf spot

Grape downy mildew is favored by humid and rainy conditions. Mancozeb helps protect foliage and bunches from new infection.

#### Grapes Advisory

- Use preventively before disease pressure increases.
- Ensure good canopy coverage.
- Avoid repeated unnecessary applications close to harvest unless allowed by label and PHI.
- Follow local residue and export-market restrictions if grapes are produced for export.

### 6.4 Apple

Mancozeb is used in apple against:

- Scab
- Premature leaf fall
- Alternaria leaf spot/blight
- Sooty blotch

Apple scab management requires preventive sprays, especially during wet weather and early crop stages.

#### Apple Advisory

- Apply before infection periods.
- Follow variety-specific label cautions.
- Avoid use where local recommendations warn about phytotoxicity or fruit russeting on sensitive varieties.
- Follow pre-harvest interval strictly.

### 6.5 Paddy / Rice

Mancozeb may be used in rice against:

- Blast
- Leaf spot diseases in some local recommendations

Rice blast spreads under humid conditions, dense crop canopy, excessive nitrogen, and susceptible varieties.

#### Paddy Advisory

- Use as part of integrated disease management.
- Avoid excessive nitrogen fertilizer.
- Maintain proper spacing and field aeration.
- Use resistant varieties where available.

### 6.6 Wheat

Mancozeb may be used in wheat against:

- Brown rust
- Black rust
- Blight

Rust and blight management should include resistant varieties, balanced nutrition, and timely fungicide sprays.

### 6.7 Maize

Mancozeb may be used in maize against:

- Leaf blight
- Downy mildew

Maize disease management should include seed treatment, resistant hybrids, field sanitation, and preventive sprays where recommended.

### 6.8 Banana

Mancozeb is used in banana for:

- Sigatoka leaf spot
- Tip rot
- Cigar-end rot

Banana leaf disease management requires good sanitation, removal of infected leaves, proper spacing, and timely fungicide sprays.

### 6.9 Guava

Mancozeb may be used in guava for:

- Fruit rot
- Leaf spot diseases

Sprays should be timed before high humidity or rainy periods, especially when fruits are developing.

### 6.10 Chilli

Mancozeb may be used in chilli disease management programs for:

- Anthracnose
- Leaf spot
- Dieback-related fungal infections, depending on local recommendation

For chilli anthracnose, sanitation, seed treatment, resistant varieties, and preventive sprays are important.

## 7. Common Formulations

### Mancozeb 75% WP

Wettable powder formulation. It must be mixed thoroughly in water before spraying. It is one of the most common formulations used by farmers.

### Mancozeb 75% WG

Water-dispersible granule formulation. It is easier to handle than WP and produces less dust during mixing.

### Mancozeb 80% WP

Higher concentration wettable powder formulation. Dose must be adjusted according to product label.

### Mancozeb 35% SC

Suspension concentrate formulation. It is easier to mix and may have different crop recommendations compared with WP/WG formulations.

## 8. General Dosage Guidance

> Important: Always follow the product label, local agriculture university recommendation, and CIBRC-approved use pattern. Dose varies by formulation, crop, disease, spray volume, and country/state registration.

Common Indian label-style recommendations for Mancozeb 75% WP often fall around:

- **1.5 to 2.0 kg formulation per hectare**
- With about **500 to 750 litres of water per hectare**, depending on crop canopy and sprayer
- For some crops or tree crops, dose may be given per tree or per 100 litres of water

Official Indian major-use documents list examples such as Mancozeb 75% WG for tomato early blight and potato late blight, and Mancozeb 75% WP for crops such as wheat, maize, paddy, potato, tomato, grapes, banana, and others, depending on disease and formulation. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

## 9. Application Timing

Mancozeb should be applied:

- Before disease infection occurs
- At first appearance of symptoms
- During humid, cloudy, rainy, or disease-favorable weather
- After field scouting shows early disease risk
- As a protective spray before expected rainfall, if label allows

Mancozeb should not be used as the only solution when the disease is already severe. In severe infections, farmers may need a recommended systemic or translaminar fungicide along with protectant disease-management practices.

## 10. Spray Interval

Spray interval depends on:

- Crop
- Disease pressure
- Weather
- Rainfall
- Product label
- Disease stage
- Local advisory

Common preventive intervals are often around 7 to 10 days under moderate disease pressure. Under heavy rainfall or severe disease pressure, shorter intervals may be recommended by local experts, but label limits must not be exceeded.

## 11. Rainfastness and Coverage

Mancozeb is a contact fungicide, so spray coverage is very important.

### Good Spray Coverage Means

- Leaves are uniformly covered.
- Lower leaves are sprayed.
- Both sides of leaves receive spray where possible.
- Dense crop canopy is penetrated.
- Spray solution is not dripping excessively.

Heavy rainfall after spraying may wash off mancozeb because it remains on the plant surface. Reapplication may be needed if allowed by label and if disease risk remains high.

## 12. Compatibility

Mancozeb is commonly used in fungicide programs and may be compatible with many pesticides, but compatibility depends on product formulation.

### General Compatibility Guidance

- Do a jar test before tank mixing.
- Do not mix with strongly alkaline materials unless the label allows.
- Avoid mixing with unknown products.
- Do not mix with oils or highly alkaline sprays unless specifically recommended.
- Follow the label sequence for tank mixing.
- Use spray mixture soon after preparation.

## 13. Resistance Management

Mancozeb is valuable for resistance management because it has multi-site activity and low resistance risk. FRAC multi-site fungicides are important tools for sustaining the effectiveness of single-site fungicides. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

### Best Resistance-Management Practices

- Rotate fungicides with different FRAC codes.
- Avoid repeated use of only one systemic fungicide group.
- Use mancozeb as a protectant partner in disease programs.
- Do not apply fungicides unnecessarily.
- Use recommended dose only.
- Combine fungicide use with resistant varieties and sanitation.
- Remove infected plant debris where practical.
- Avoid dense canopy and poor aeration.
- Follow IPM-based disease monitoring.

## 14. Integrated Disease Management

Mancozeb works best as part of integrated disease management, not as a standalone solution.

### IDM Practices

- Use disease-free seed or planting material.
- Select resistant or tolerant varieties.
- Maintain proper plant spacing.
- Avoid excessive nitrogen.
- Improve field drainage.
- Remove infected leaves or crop debris.
- Avoid overhead irrigation where possible.
- Monitor weather-based disease risk.
- Use crop rotation.
- Follow recommended fungicide rotation.
- Apply fungicides at the right disease stage.

## 15. Safety Precautions

Mancozeb should be handled carefully. Exposure may occur during mixing, loading, spraying, cleaning equipment, or entering treated fields too soon.

### Personal Protective Equipment

Use PPE as recommended on the product label. Basic PPE may include:

- Gloves
- Long-sleeved shirt
- Long pants
- Mask or respirator where required
- Eye protection
- Shoes or boots
- Apron during mixing

EPA documents identify toxicological concern related to ETU, a contaminant and breakdown product associated with mancozeb/EBDC fungicides, especially regarding thyroid effects. Therefore, safe handling and label compliance are important. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

### Handling Safety

- Do not inhale dust from WP formulations.
- Avoid contact with skin and eyes.
- Do not eat, drink, or smoke while mixing or spraying.
- Wash hands and exposed skin after use.
- Wash contaminated clothes separately.
- Do not allow children or animals near mixing or spraying area.
- Store in original container only.
- Keep away from food, feed, seed, and drinking water.

## 16. Environmental Precautions

Mancozeb should not be allowed to contaminate water bodies.

### Environmental Safety

- Do not spray near ponds, streams, wells, or irrigation channels.
- Avoid drift to nearby crops, houses, or water sources.
- Do not wash sprayer tanks in ponds or canals.
- Dispose of empty containers safely as per local rules.
- Avoid spraying during high wind.
- Avoid spraying before heavy rainfall unless advised.

## 17. Phytotoxicity Caution

Mancozeb is generally safe for many crops when used as directed, but phytotoxicity can occur if:

- Dose is too high
- Spray is applied during extreme heat
- Product is mixed with incompatible chemicals
- Sensitive crop variety is sprayed
- Spray is applied during stress conditions
- Wrong formulation or concentration is used

Always test on a small area when using a new tank mix or spraying sensitive varieties.

## 18. Pre-Harvest Interval

Pre-harvest interval, also called PHI or waiting period, varies by crop, formulation, dose, and country registration.

Examples from Indian approved-use documents show different waiting periods for different mancozeb formulations and crops, such as short intervals for some vegetable uses and longer intervals for some fruit/tree crops. Always follow the exact label and local legal recommendation. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

## 19. Re-Entry Interval

Do not enter the treated field immediately after spraying unless wearing protective clothing. Re-entry interval must be followed as per product label. If no local label information is available, wait until spray deposits have dried and follow local safety guidance.

## 20. Symptoms of Exposure

Possible exposure symptoms may include:

- Skin irritation
- Eye irritation
- Coughing or throat irritation
- Headache
- Nausea
- Dizziness
- Allergic skin reaction in sensitive individuals

If poisoning or severe exposure is suspected:

- Stop exposure immediately.
- Move the person to fresh air.
- Remove contaminated clothing.
- Wash skin with clean water and soap.
- Rinse eyes with clean water if exposed.
- Seek medical attention immediately.
- Carry product label or container to the doctor.

## 21. Storage

Store mancozeb:

- In a cool and dry place
- Away from sunlight and moisture
- Away from children and animals
- Away from food, feed, and seed
- In the original labeled container
- Tightly closed after use

Wettable powder formulations should be protected from moisture because lumps reduce mixing quality.

## 22. When Mancozeb Works Best

Mancozeb works best when:

- Disease is not yet severe.
- Weather-based disease risk is predicted.
- Spray coverage is uniform.
- It is used before rainfall or infection periods.
- It is rotated with other fungicide groups.
- Crop canopy is not too dense.
- Field sanitation is maintained.

## 23. When Mancozeb May Not Work Well

Mancozeb may give poor control when:

- Disease is already severe.
- Spray coverage is poor.
- Rain washes off spray soon after application.
- Wrong dose is used.
- Application is delayed.
- New plant growth emerges after spraying and remains unprotected.
- The disease requires systemic or curative fungicide action.
- Weather remains continuously favorable for disease and spray interval is too long.

## 24. Farmer Advisory

Mancozeb is a reliable preventive fungicide for many crops, but farmers should not depend on it alone during severe disease outbreaks. It should be used as part of an integrated disease-management plan.

### Simple Farmer Message

Use mancozeb before disease becomes severe. It protects the plant surface but does not cure deep infection. Good spray coverage is necessary. Repeat only as per label. Use PPE while spraying. Follow waiting period before harvest.
