Management in Tomato Cultivation

## Overview

Nursery management is a critical step in tomato cultivation because healthy seedlings are essential for obtaining vigorous plant growth, better disease resistance, and high yield. Tomato seedlings are generally raised in nurseries before transplanting to the main field.

Proper nursery management helps ensure:

* Healthy seed germination
* Strong root development
* Uniform seedling growth
* Reduced pest and disease incidence
* Better transplant survival

Well-managed nurseries produce vigorous seedlings capable of establishing quickly in the field.

---

# Importance of Nursery Management

Good nursery practices help in:

* Producing healthy seedlings
* Reducing seed wastage
* Improving crop establishment
* Minimizing disease problems
* Ensuring uniform crop growth
* Increasing productivity

---

# Selection of Nursery Site

The nursery site should have:

* Good sunlight exposure
* Well-drained soil
* Easy irrigation access
* Fertile soil
* Protection from strong winds

The area should be free from:

* Waterlogging
* Heavy shade
* Severe soil-borne diseases

---

# Soil Requirements for Nursery

Ideal nursery soil should be:

* Loose and friable
* Rich in organic matter
* Well-drained
* Free from stones and debris

---

## Ideal Soil Conditions

| Factor         | Recommended Condition |
| -------------- | --------------------- |
| Soil Type      | Sandy loam            |
| Drainage       | Good                  |
| Soil pH        | 6.0–7.0               |
| Organic Matter | High                  |

---

# Nursery Bed Preparation

Raised nursery beds are commonly used.

## Advantages of Raised Beds

* Better drainage
* Reduced waterlogging
* Improved root aeration
* Easier seedling management

---

## Recommended Bed Dimensions

Typical nursery beds may have:

* Width: 1–1.2 m
* Height: 15–20 cm
* Convenient length based on area

Pathways should be maintained between beds for easy operations.

---

# Soil Sterilization

Soil sterilization helps control:

* Soil-borne diseases
* Harmful fungi
* Nematodes
* Weed seeds

---

## Methods of Sterilization

### Solarization

* Cover moist soil with transparent plastic sheets.
* Leave for several weeks under sunlight.

### Chemical Treatment

Recommended fungicides may be used where necessary.

---

# Seed Selection

Healthy and certified seeds should be used.

## Characteristics of Good Seeds

* High germination percentage
* Disease-free
* Uniform size
* Good vigor

Hybrid seeds are often preferred for commercial cultivation.

---

# Seed Treatment

Seed treatment protects seedlings from early diseases.

## Common Purposes

* Disease prevention
* Improved germination
* Better seedling vigor

---

## Seed Treatment Methods

### Fungicide Treatment

Helps prevent:

* Damping-off disease
* Seed rot

### Biological Treatment

Beneficial microbes may be used for eco-friendly protection.

---

# Seed Sowing

Seeds are sown in rows on raised nursery beds.

## Recommended Practices

* Maintain proper spacing
* Sow at shallow depth
* Cover lightly with fine soil or compost

Overcrowding should be avoided.

---

# Mulching After Sowing

Mulching materials may include:

* Straw
* Dry grass
* Thin organic materials

### Benefits

* Moisture conservation
* Protection from heavy rain
* Better germination

Mulch is removed after seed germination.

---

# Irrigation Management

Proper watering is very important.

## Recommended Practices

* Use light irrigation
* Avoid waterlogging
* Maintain adequate moisture

Excess irrigation can cause fungal diseases.

---

# Shade Management

Young seedlings may require temporary shade.

## Benefits

* Protection from excessive heat
* Reduced moisture loss
* Better seedling establishment

Shade should be reduced gradually as seedlings grow.

---

# Weed Management

Nursery beds should be kept weed-free.

## Benefits

* Reduced competition
* Better nutrient availability
* Lower pest incidence

Manual weeding is commonly practiced.

---

# Nutrient Management

Organic manure and balanced nutrients improve seedling growth.

## Common Inputs

* Compost
* Vermicompost
* Well-decomposed farmyard manure

Foliar nutrient sprays may also be used if required.

---

# Pest and Disease Management

Nursery seedlings are vulnerable to several pests and diseases.

---

## Common Diseases

### Damping-Off

Symptoms include:

* Seedling collapse
* Stem rot near soil surface

### Leaf Spot Diseases

Can affect young leaves under humid conditions.

---

## Common Pests

* Aphids
* Whiteflies
* Thrips

These pests may also spread viral diseases.

---

# Integrated Nursery Management

Integrated management practices include:

* Healthy seed selection
* Soil sterilization
* Proper watering
* Biological control methods
* Regular monitoring

---

# Hardening of Seedlings

Before transplanting, seedlings are hardened.

## Hardening Process

* Reduce irrigation gradually
* Expose seedlings to outdoor conditions

### Benefits

* Better transplant survival
* Reduced transplant shock
* Improved field establishment

---

# Seedling Age for Transplanting

Tomato seedlings are generally ready for transplanting in:

* About 25–35 days after sowing

Healthy seedlings usually have:

* 4–6 true leaves
* Strong stem
* Healthy root system

---

# Characteristics of Healthy Seedlings

Healthy seedlings show:

* Green vigorous leaves
* Strong stem growth
* Well-developed roots
* Uniform growth
* Disease-free appearance

---

# Common Mistakes to Avoid

Farmers should avoid:

* Excess watering
* Overcrowded sowing
* Poor drainage
* Use of infected seeds
* Excess shade
* Delayed transplanting

---

# Sustainable Nursery Practices

Eco-friendly nursery practices include:

* Organic manure use
* Biological seed treatment
* Water conservation
* Reduced chemical use

---

# Farmer Recommendations

Farmers should:

1. Use certified quality seeds.
2. Prepare raised nursery beds.
3. Sterilize nursery soil properly.
4. Maintain proper irrigation.
5. Monitor pests and diseases regularly.
6. Harden seedlings before transplanting.

---

# Economic Importance

Good nursery management contributes to:

* Higher survival rate
* Better crop establishment
* Increased yield
* Reduced disease losses
* Improved farm profitability
