# Bacterial Wilt in Tomato

## Overview

Bacterial wilt is one of the most destructive diseases affecting tomato crops in tropical and subtropical regions. The disease causes sudden wilting of plants and can lead to severe yield losses under favorable conditions.

The disease is caused by soil-borne bacteria that invade the vascular system of the plant, blocking water movement and causing rapid wilting.

Bacterial wilt is difficult to manage because:

* The bacteria survive in soil
* It spreads through water and infected materials
* It affects many host plants

Severe infection can destroy entire tomato fields.

# Distribution

Bacterial wilt is common in:

* Tropical regions
* Subtropical climates
* Warm humid areas

The disease is widespread in:

* Rainfed farming areas
* Poorly drained soils
* Intensive vegetable-growing regions

---

# Symptoms of Bacterial Wilt

The disease mainly affects the vascular system of tomato plants.

---

# Early Symptoms

* Slight wilting during daytime
* Recovery during cooler hours
* Drooping of leaves

---

# Advanced Symptoms

* Permanent wilting of entire plant
* Sudden collapse of healthy-looking plants
* Leaf yellowing in some cases
* Plant death without major leaf spots

Wilting usually progresses rapidly.

---

# Stem Symptoms

When infected stems are cut:

* Brown discoloration may be visible
* Milky bacterial ooze may appear

---

# Root Symptoms

Roots may show:

* Browning
* Decay in severe infection

---

# Disease Cycle

The bacteria survive in:

* Soil
* Crop residues
* Infected weeds
* Irrigation water
* Alternative host plants

The pathogen enters plants through:

* Root injuries
* Natural openings
* Damaged roots

---

# Spread of the Disease

The disease spreads through:

* Contaminated soil
* Irrigation water
* Infected seedlings
* Farm tools
* Human activities

Movement of infected soil can rapidly spread the disease.

---

# Favorable Conditions for Disease Development

Bacterial wilt develops rapidly under:

* Warm temperatures
* High soil moisture
* Poor drainage
* Acidic soils
* Continuous tomato cultivation

Waterlogged conditions increase disease severity.

---

# Economic Importance

Severe infection may cause:

* Sudden plant death
* Major yield losses
* Reduced fruit production
* Increased production costs
* Economic losses to farmers

In highly infected fields, crop failure may occur.

---

# Integrated Disease Management (IDM)

Integrated disease management is essential because chemical control alone is often ineffective.

---

# Cultural Management

## Crop Rotation

Rotate tomato with non-host crops such as:

* Cereals
* Grasses

Avoid repeated tomato cultivation in the same field.

---

## Use Healthy Seedlings

Always use:

* Disease-free seedlings
* Healthy planting material
* Certified nursery plants

---

## Field Sanitation

Important practices include:

* Removal of infected plants
* Destruction of crop residues
* Weed control

This helps reduce bacterial survival.

---

## Proper Drainage

Good drainage is critical.

Water stagnation should be avoided because it:

* Increases disease spread
* Promotes bacterial multiplication

Raised beds are beneficial.

---

# Soil Management

Improving soil health helps reduce disease severity.

## Recommended Practices

* Addition of organic matter
* Soil solarization
* Balanced fertilization
* pH management

Healthy soils support beneficial microorganisms.

---

# Resistant Varieties

Where available, resistant or tolerant tomato varieties should be used.

Benefits include:

* Reduced disease incidence
* Improved crop survival
* Better productivity

---

# Biological Management

Beneficial microorganisms may help suppress the pathogen.

## Common Biological Approaches

* Bio-control agents
* Beneficial bacteria
* Organic disease management methods

These methods are useful in integrated management systems.

---

# Chemical Management

Chemical control of bacterial wilt is difficult because the bacteria live inside plant tissues and soil.

Preventive treatments may help under limited conditions.

Applications should follow agricultural recommendations.

---

# Nursery Management

Healthy nursery management is important.

## Recommended Practices

* Use sterilized nursery soil
* Avoid overwatering
* Maintain nursery hygiene
* Remove diseased seedlings immediately

---

# Monitoring and Early Detection

Regular field inspection is essential.

Farmers should monitor:

* Wilting plants
* Sudden plant collapse
* Stem discoloration
* Patchy field symptoms

Early removal reduces spread.

---

# Preventive Measures

Farmers should:

1. Use disease-free seedlings.
2. Maintain proper drainage.
3. Practice crop rotation.
4. Remove infected plants immediately.
5. Avoid movement of contaminated soil.
6. Improve soil health regularly.

---

# Common Mistakes to Avoid

Farmers should avoid:

* Continuous tomato cultivation
* Waterlogging
* Poor drainage
* Use of infected seedlings
* Excess irrigation
* Ignoring wilt symptoms

---

# Sustainable Disease Management

Sustainable management includes:

* Crop rotation
* Soil health improvement
* Biological control
* Organic matter application
* Integrated disease management

---

# Farmer Recommendations

Farmers are advised to:

* Inspect fields regularly.
* Remove infected plants immediately.
* Maintain proper field drainage.
* Use resistant varieties where available.
* Follow integrated disease management practices.

Severely infected fields may show:

* Large patches of wilted plants
* Sudden crop collapse
* Reduced fruit production
* Extensive plant death
