# Late Blight in Tomato

## Overview

Late blight is one of the most destructive diseases of tomato crops worldwide. The disease spreads rapidly under cool and humid conditions and can destroy entire fields within a short period if not managed properly.

Late blight affects:

* Leaves
* Stems
* Fruits

The disease causes severe foliage damage, fruit rot, and major yield losses. It is especially dangerous during rainy and cloudy weather conditions.

---

# Causal Organism

| Feature         | Details                       |
| --------------- | ----------------------------- |
| Disease Name    | Late Blight                   |
| Causal Organism | *Phytophthora infestans*      |
| Disease Type    | Oomycete Disease (Water Mold) |
| Affected Crop   | Tomato                        |

---

# Distribution

Late blight occurs widely in:

* Cool humid regions
* Rainy season cultivation areas
* Temperate and subtropical climates

The disease is common in:

* High humidity environments
* Areas with prolonged leaf wetness
* Fields with poor air circulation

---

# Symptoms of Late Blight

The disease affects multiple plant parts and spreads rapidly.

---

# Symptoms on Leaves

## Early Symptoms

* Small water-soaked spots on leaves
* Pale green to dark lesions
* Rapid enlargement of spots

---

## Advanced Symptoms

* Large brown or black lesions
* Leaf blighting
* Wilting of foliage
* Complete leaf collapse

Under humid conditions:

* White fungal-like growth may appear on the underside of leaves.

---

# Symptoms on Stems

* Dark brown to black stem lesions
* Stem rot in severe cases
* Weakening of plant structure

Severely infected stems may collapse.

---

# Symptoms on Fruits

## Fruit Symptoms

* Brown greasy-looking spots
* Firm dark lesions
* Fruit rotting under humid conditions

Infected fruits become unmarketable.

---

# Disease Cycle

The pathogen survives in:

* Infected plant debris
* Volunteer tomato plants
* Alternative host plants
* Infected potato crops

Spores spread through:

* Wind
* Rain splash
* Irrigation water
* Contaminated tools and equipment

The disease can spread very rapidly under favorable conditions.

---

# Favorable Conditions for Disease Development

Late blight develops rapidly under:

* Cool temperatures
* High humidity
* Frequent rainfall
* Foggy weather
* Prolonged leaf wetness

Dense crop canopy increases disease severity.

---

# Economic Importance

Severe infection may cause:

* Rapid crop destruction
* Fruit losses
* Reduced yield
* Poor fruit quality
* Heavy economic losses

Under severe outbreaks, entire fields may fail.

---

# Integrated Disease Management (IDM)

Integrated disease management is essential for controlling late blight.

---

# Cultural Management

## Crop Rotation

Rotate tomato with non-host crops to reduce pathogen survival.

Avoid continuous tomato cultivation in the same field.

---

## Field Sanitation

Important practices include:

* Removal of infected plants
* Destruction of crop residues
* Weed management

Sanitation reduces disease sources.

---

## Proper Plant Spacing

Maintain proper spacing to:

* Improve air circulation
* Reduce humidity
* Minimize leaf wetness

---

## Avoid Overhead Irrigation

Drip irrigation is preferred because it:

* Reduces leaf moisture
* Minimizes disease spread

---

# Use of Healthy Planting Material

Always use:

* Healthy seedlings
* Disease-free planting material
* Certified seeds

This helps prevent disease introduction.

---

# Mulching

Mulching helps:

* Reduce soil splash
* Lower disease spread
* Maintain soil moisture balance

Organic and plastic mulches may be beneficial.

---

# Resistant Varieties

Where available, resistant or tolerant varieties should be used.

Benefits include:

* Reduced disease severity
* Lower fungicide dependence
* Better crop protection

---

# Biological Management

Biological control methods may help suppress disease development.

## Common Biological Approaches

* Beneficial fungi
* Beneficial bacteria
* Organic disease management methods

These are useful in integrated management systems.

---

# Chemical Management

Protective fungicide sprays are commonly used during favorable disease conditions.

## Common Practices

* Preventive fungicide application
* Timely spray schedules
* Rotation of fungicides

Applications should follow agricultural recommendations and safety guidelines.

---

# Monitoring and Early Detection

Regular crop inspection is critical.

Farmers should monitor:

* Water-soaked lesions
* Rapid leaf blighting
* Stem lesions
* Fruit rotting

Early action helps reduce disease spread.

---

# Preventive Measures

Farmers should:

1. Use disease-free seedlings.
2. Maintain proper plant spacing.
3. Avoid excess leaf wetness.
4. Follow crop rotation practices.
5. Remove infected plants immediately.
6. Monitor weather conditions regularly.

---

# Common Mistakes to Avoid

Farmers should avoid:

* Dense planting
* Poor drainage
* Overhead irrigation
* Delayed disease management
* Ignoring early symptoms

---

# Sustainable Disease Management

Sustainable management includes:

* Integrated disease management
* Crop rotation
* Biological control
* Proper irrigation management
* Reduced chemical dependence

---

# Farmer Recommendations

Farmers are advised to:

* Inspect fields regularly during humid weather.
* Remove infected plants immediately.
* Maintain good field ventilation.
* Apply preventive disease management practices.
* Seek expert guidance during severe outbreaks.

---

# Signs of Severe Infection

Severely infected fields may show:

* Rapid leaf blighting
* Stem collapse
* Extensive fruit rot
* Complete crop destruction
* Heavy yield loss

---

# Role of Weather Conditions

Weather plays a major role in disease outbreaks.

High-risk conditions include:

* Cool humid weather
* Continuous rainfall
* Foggy mornings
* Prolonged leaf wetness

Disease forecasting can help in early management.

---

# Economic and Agricultural Importance

Effective management helps:

* Protect tomato yield
* Improve fruit quality
* Reduce crop losses
* Maintain farm profitability
