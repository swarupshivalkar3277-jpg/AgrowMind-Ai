# Target Spot in Tomato

## Overview

Target spot is a fungal disease that affects tomato crops in warm and humid environments. The disease mainly attacks leaves, stems, and fruits, causing leaf lesions, defoliation, fruit damage, and reduced yield.

The disease is named "target spot" because the lesions often develop concentric ring patterns resembling a target board.

Target spot can become severe under:

* High humidity
* Warm temperatures
* Frequent rainfall
* Dense crop canopy

If not managed properly, the disease may lead to:

* Premature leaf drop
* Poor fruit quality
* Reduced plant vigor
* Significant economic losses

# Distribution

Target spot occurs widely in:

* Tropical regions
* Subtropical climates
* Warm humid tomato-growing areas

The disease is especially common in:

* Rainy season cultivation
* Greenhouse production
* Fields with poor air circulation

---

# Symptoms of Target Spot

The disease affects leaves, stems, and fruits.

---

# Symptoms on Leaves

## Early Symptoms

* Small brown spots on leaves
* Circular to irregular lesions
* Water-soaked appearance in early stages

Symptoms usually begin on older lower leaves.

---

## Advanced Symptoms

* Lesions enlarge gradually
* Concentric ring patterns develop
* Gray or tan centers appear
* Yellowing around lesions
* Premature leaf drop

Severe infection may cause extensive defoliation.

---

# Symptoms on Stems

* Dark elongated lesions
* Stem discoloration
* Weak stem growth

Stem infection may reduce plant vigor.

---

# Symptoms on Fruits

## Fruit Symptoms

* Dark sunken lesions
* Circular spots with concentric rings
* Fruit cracking in severe cases
* Reduced market quality

Fruit infection lowers commercial value.

---

# Disease Cycle

The fungus survives in:

* Crop residues
* Infected plant debris
* Soil
* Volunteer plants
* Alternative host plants

Spores spread through:

* Wind
* Rain splash
* Irrigation water
* Farm activities

The disease spreads rapidly under wet conditions.

---

# Favorable Conditions for Disease Development

Target spot develops rapidly under:

* Warm temperatures
* High humidity
* Frequent rainfall
* Prolonged leaf wetness
* Dense crop canopy

Poor ventilation increases disease severity.

---

# Economic Importance

Severe infection may cause:

* Reduced photosynthesis
* Premature defoliation
* Reduced fruit yield
* Poor fruit quality
* Economic losses to farmers

Heavy disease pressure can significantly reduce marketable produce.

---

# Integrated Disease Management (IDM)

Integrated disease management is essential for effective control.

---

# Cultural Management

## Crop Rotation

Rotate tomato with non-host crops to reduce fungal survival.

Avoid continuous tomato cultivation in the same field.

---

## Field Sanitation

Important practices include:

* Removal of infected leaves
* Destruction of crop residues
* Weed management

Sanitation reduces disease sources.

---

## Proper Plant Spacing

Maintain adequate spacing to:

* Improve air circulation
* Reduce humidity
* Minimize leaf wetness

---

## Avoid Overhead Irrigation

Drip irrigation is preferred because it:

* Reduces leaf wetness
* Minimizes disease spread

---

# Use of Healthy Planting Material

Always use:

* Certified seeds
* Healthy seedlings
* Disease-free planting material

Healthy planting material reduces disease introduction.

---

# Mulching

Mulching helps:

* Reduce soil splash
* Lower fungal spread
* Conserve moisture

Organic and plastic mulches may be beneficial.

---

# Nutrient Management

Balanced nutrition improves plant resistance.

## Recommended Practices

* Balanced fertilizer application
* Adequate potassium supply
* Organic matter addition

Excess nitrogen should be avoided.

---

# Resistant Varieties

Resistant or tolerant tomato varieties may help reduce disease severity.

Benefits include:

* Better crop protection
* Reduced disease incidence
* Lower fungicide dependence

---

# Biological Management

Biological control methods may help suppress fungal growth.

## Common Biological Approaches

* Beneficial fungi

* Organic disease management methods

These methods support sustainable agriculture.

# Chemical Management

Fungicide applications may be necessary during favorable disease conditions.

## Common Practices

* Preventive fungicide sprays

Applications should follow agricultural recommendations and safety guidelines.

# Monitoring and Early Detection

Regular field inspection is essential.

Farmers should monitor:

* Circular leaf lesions
* Concentric ring patterns
* Yellowing leaves
* Fruit spotting

Early detection improves disease management.

---

# Preventive Measures

Farmers should:

1. Practice crop rotation.
2. Remove infected plant debris.
3. Maintain proper spacing.
4. Avoid excess leaf wetness.
5. Use healthy seedlings.
6. Monitor fields regularly.
